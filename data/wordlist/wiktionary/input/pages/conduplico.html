Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

con- +‎ duplicō (“to double”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈdu.pli.koː/, [kɔn̪ˈd̪ʊplʲɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈdu.pli.ko/, [kon̪ˈd̪uːpliko]

Verb[edit]

conduplicō (present infinitive conduplicāre, perfect active conduplicāvī, supine conduplicātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to double
