Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkum.ba/, [ˈkʊmbä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkum.ba/, [ˈkumbä]

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

cumba f (genitive cumbae); first declension

  1. Alternative form of cymba (“skiff”)
Declension[edit]

Etymology 2[edit]

Borrowed from Gaulish, from Proto-Celtic *kumbā (“valley”).

Alternative forms[edit]

  • comba, cumma, comma, cuma, coma, cumbus

Noun[edit]

cumba f (genitive cumbae); first declension

  1. (Medieval Latin) valley
