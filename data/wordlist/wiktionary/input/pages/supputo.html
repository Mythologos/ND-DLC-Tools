Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • subputō

Etymology[edit]

From sub- +‎ puto (“clean”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsup.pu.toː/, [ˈs̠ʊpːʊt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsup.pu.to/, [ˈsupːut̪o]

Verb[edit]

supputō (present infinitive supputāre, perfect active supputāvī, supine supputātum); first conjugation

  1. to prune, lop or trim
  2. to reckon or compute
