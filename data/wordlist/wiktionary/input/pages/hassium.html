English[edit]

Chemical element
Hs
Previous: bohrium (Bh)
Next: meitnerium (Mt)

Etymology[edit]

From Latin Hassia (“Hesse”), the location of the institute where the element was first synthesized, +‎ -ium.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: hăs'ēəm, IPA(key): /ˈhæsiəm/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • Rhymes: -æsiəm

Noun[edit]

hassium (uncountable)

English Wikipedia has an article on:
hassium
Wikipedia
  1. An artificially-produced transuranic chemical element (symbol Hs) with atomic number 108.

Synonyms[edit]

  • eka-osmium (used before the element was discovered)
  • hahnium (a name proposed by the IUPAC, but rejected; see also dubnium)
  • unniloctium (used before the element was officially named)

Derived terms[edit]

  • hassium-270
  • hassate

Related terms[edit]

  • Hessen
