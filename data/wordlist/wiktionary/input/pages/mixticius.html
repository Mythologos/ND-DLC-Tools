Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From mixtus (“mixed”) +‎ -īcius. Attested in Jerome and Isidore.[1]

Adjective[edit]

mixtīcius (feminine mixtīcia, neuter mixtīcium); first/second-declension adjective (Late Latin)

  1. of mixed race; mixed-blood; half-breed
