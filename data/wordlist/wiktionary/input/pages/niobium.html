English[edit]

Chemical element
Nb
Previous: zirconium (Zr)
Next: molybdenum (Mo)

Etymology[edit]

Niobe +‎ -ium, because of the element's affinity with tantalum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: nīō'bēəm, IPA(key): /naɪˈoʊbiəm/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)

Noun[edit]

niobium (countable and uncountable, plural niobiums)

English Wikipedia has an article on:
niobium
Wikipedia
  1. A chemical element (symbol Nb) with an atomic number of 41: a light grey, crystalline, ductile transition metal used in superconducting materials.
  2. (countable) A single atom of this element.

Synonyms[edit]

  • columbium (in metallurgy; not in scientific use)
  • dianium (obsolete)
