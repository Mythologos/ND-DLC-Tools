English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Pactolus
Wikipedia

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /pækˈtəʊləs/
  • Hyphenation: Pac‧to‧lus

Proper noun[edit]

Pactolus

  1. A river, formerly an important feature of ancient Lydia and said to contain gold; now rising and emptying in modern Turkey.

Derived terms[edit]

  • Pactolian

Translations[edit]

river
  • French: Pactole (fr) m

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Πακτωλός (Paktōlós).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /pakˈtoː.lus/, [päkˈt̪oːɫ̪ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /pakˈto.lus/, [päkˈt̪ɔːlus]

Proper noun[edit]

Pactōlus m sg (genitive Pactōlī); second declension

  1. Pactolus

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun, singular only.
