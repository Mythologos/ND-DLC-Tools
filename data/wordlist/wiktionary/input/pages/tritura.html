Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From terō (“I rub”) +‎ -tūra (action noun suffix), formed from the supine.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /triːˈtuː.ra/, [t̪riːˈt̪uːrä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /triˈtu.ra/, [t̪riˈt̪uːrä]

Noun[edit]

trītūra f (genitive trītūrae); first declension

  1. rubbing, chafing
  2. friction
  3. threshing (of grain)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Romanian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Verb[edit]

  1. to triturate
    Synonym: pulveriza
