Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From sub- +‎ introeo.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /su.binˈtroe̯.oː/, [s̠ʊbɪn̪ˈt̪roe̯oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /su.binˈtre.o/, [subin̪ˈt̪rɛːo]

Verb[edit]

subintroeō (present infinitive subintroīre, perfect active subintroiī or subintroīvī, supine subintroitum); irregular conjugation, irregular

  1. to enter, go into
