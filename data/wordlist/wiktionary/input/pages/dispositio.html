English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
dispositio
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin dispositio. Doublet of disposition.

Noun[edit]

dispositio (uncountable)

  1. One of the five canons of classical rhetoric: the organization of arguments.

  • elocutio
  • inventio
  • memoria
  • pronuntiatio

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dispōnō (“dispose, arrange”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /dis.poˈsi.ti.oː/, [d̪ɪs̠pɔˈs̠ɪt̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /dis.poˈsit.t͡si.o/, [d̪ispoˈs̬it̪ː͡s̪io]

Noun[edit]

dispositiō f (genitive dispositiōnis); third declension

  1. a regular disposition, arrangement; management, direction

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.
