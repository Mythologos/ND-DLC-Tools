Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From re- +‎ foveō (“keep warm; nurture; support”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reˈfo.u̯e.oː/, [rɛˈfou̯eoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈfo.ve.o/, [reˈfɔːveo]

Verb[edit]

refoveō (present infinitive refovēre, perfect active refōvī, supine refōtum); second conjugation

  1. to warm again
  2. to restore, revive, refresh
