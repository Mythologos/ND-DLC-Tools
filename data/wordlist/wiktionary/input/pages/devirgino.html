Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dē- +‎ virgō (“virgin”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deːˈu̯ir.ɡi.noː/, [d̪eːˈu̯ɪrɡɪnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /deˈvir.d͡ʒi.no/, [d̪eˈvird͡ʒino]

Verb[edit]

dēvirginō (present infinitive dēvirgināre, perfect active dēvirgināvī, supine dēvirginātum); first conjugation

  1. to deflower
