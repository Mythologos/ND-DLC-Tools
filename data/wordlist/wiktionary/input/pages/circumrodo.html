Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

circum- +‎ rōdō (“to gnaw”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈroː.doː/, [kɪrkʊmˈroːd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈro.do/, [t͡ʃirkumˈrɔːd̪o]

Verb[edit]

circumrōdō (present infinitive circumrōdere, perfect active circumrōsī, supine circumrōsum); third conjugation

  1. (transitive) to nibble around at
  2. (transitive, figurative) to slander
