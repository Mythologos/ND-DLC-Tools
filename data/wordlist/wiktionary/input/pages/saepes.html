Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • sēpēs

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *seh₂ip- (“to cram, fence”), possibly from *sh₂ey-. Cognate with saepis, saepiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsae̯.peːs/, [ˈs̠äe̯peːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈse.pes/, [ˈsɛːpes]

Noun[edit]

saepēs f (genitive saepis); third declension

  1. hedge, fence

Declension[edit]
