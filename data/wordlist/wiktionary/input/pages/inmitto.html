Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ mittō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈmit.toː/, [ɪmˈmɪt̪ːoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈmit.to/, [inˈmit̪ːo]

Verb[edit]

inmittō (present infinitive inmittere, perfect active inmīsī, supine inmissum); third conjugation

  1. to send in, into or against
  2. to insert
