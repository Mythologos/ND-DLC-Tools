Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Mentioned by Pliny; from Ancient Greek λοβός (lobós, “pod, lobe of fruit”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈlo.ba/, [ˈɫ̪ɔbä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈlo.ba/, [ˈlɔːbä]

Noun[edit]

loba f (genitive lobae); first declension

  1. straw of Indian millet (Sorghum bicolor)
  2. nightshade

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin lupa.

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈlo.bɐ/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈlo.bɐ/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈlo.ba/
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ˈlo.bɐ/ [ˈlo.βɐ]

  • Hyphenation: lo‧bas

Noun[edit]

loba f (plural lobas)

  1. female equivalent of lobo, a she-wolf
  2. (derogatory slang) a bitch, a slut: a sexually promiscuous woman
  3. (archaic, ecclesiastical) a wide cassock worn by Portuguese clergy

Spanish[edit]
