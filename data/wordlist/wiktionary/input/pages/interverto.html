Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

inter- +‎ vertō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈu̯er.toː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈu̯ɛrt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈver.to/, [in̪t̪erˈvɛrt̪o]

Verb[edit]

intervertō (present infinitive intervertere, perfect active intervertī, supine interversum); third conjugation

  1. to turn aside or divert
  2. to purloin, pilfer or embezzle
  3. to cheat or defraud
