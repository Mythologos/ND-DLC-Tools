English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Spanish Guatemala (originally the name of Iximche, the location of Guatemala's first capital), from Classical Nahuatl Cuauhtēmallān, from cuauhtēmalli (“woodpile”) + -tlān (“place”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK, US) IPA(key): /ˌɡwɑː.təˈmɑː.lə/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Proper noun[edit]

Guatemala

  2. A country in Central America. Official name: Republic of Guatemala.
  3. A department of Guatemala.

Derived terms[edit]

  • Guatemalan

Related terms[edit]

  • Guatemala City

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Spanish Guatemala, from Classical Nahuatl Cuauhtēmallān.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ɡʷaː.teːˈmaː.la/, [ɡʷäːt̪eːˈmäːɫ̪ä] or IPA(key): /ɡʷaˈte.ma.la/, [ɡʷäˈt̪ɛmäɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ɡwa.teˈma.la/, [ɡwät̪eˈmäːlä] or IPA(key): /ɡwaˈte.ma.la/, [ɡwäˈt̪ɛːmälä]
  • Note: the former corresponds more closely to Spanish, the latter is the scansion in Rafael Landívar's Rusticatio Mexicana.[1]

Proper noun[edit]

Guā̆tē̆mā̆la f sg (genitive Guā̆tē̆mā̆lae); first declension

  1. (New Latin) Guatemala (a country in Central America)
