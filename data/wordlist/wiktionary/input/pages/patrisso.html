Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • patrizō

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek πατρίζω (patrízō).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /paˈtris.soː/, [päˈt̪rɪs̠ːoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /paˈtris.so/, [päˈt̪risːo]

Verb[edit]

patrissō (present infinitive patrissāre); first conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. (Old Latin) take after one's father
