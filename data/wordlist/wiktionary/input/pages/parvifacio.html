Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From parvus (“small, little, puny”) +‎ faciō (“I do, make”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /par.u̯iˈfa.ki.oː/, [päru̯ɪˈfäkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /par.viˈfa.t͡ʃi.o/, [pärviˈfäːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

parvifaciō (present infinitive parvifacere, perfect active parvifēcī, supine parvifactum); third conjugation iō-variant, irregular passive voice

  1. to make light of
  2. to despise
