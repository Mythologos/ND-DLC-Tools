Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ vibrō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkon.u̯i.broː/, [ˈkɔnu̯ɪbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkon.vi.bro/, [ˈkɔɱvibro]

Verb[edit]

convibrō (present infinitive convibrāre, perfect active convibrāvī, supine convibrātum); first conjugation

  1. to move one's self rapidly
  2. to move a thing quickly, set in rapid motion
    • Lucius Appuleius, Florida 12.4
    • c. 330 CE – c. 390 CE, Ammianus Marcellinus, Rerum Gestarum 17.7.15
