Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From super- (“over”) +‎ indūcō (draw over, cover).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /su.pe.rinˈduː.koː/, [s̠ʊpɛrɪn̪ˈd̪uːkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /su.pe.rinˈdu.ko/, [superin̪ˈd̪uːko]

Verb[edit]

superindūcō (present infinitive superindūcere, perfect active superindūxī, supine superinductum); third conjugation, irregular short imperative

  1. to draw or cover over
