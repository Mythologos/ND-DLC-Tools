Etymology[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

Noun[edit]

meo 

  1. cat

Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmɛ.o/
  • Rhymes: -ɛo
  • Hyphenation: mè‧o

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin meus.

Adjective[edit]

meo (feminine mea, masculine plural mei, feminine plural mee)

  1. Old Italian form of mio

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

meo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of meare

Anagrams[edit]

  • Emo, emo-, omè

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈme.oː/, [ˈmeoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈme.o/, [ˈmɛːo]

Etymology 1[edit]

From Proto-Italic *meā, from Proto-Indo-European *mey-.

Verb[edit]

meō (present infinitive meāre, perfect active meāvī, supine meātum); first conjugation

  1. to go along, pass or traverse

Vietnamese[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Hà Nội) IPA(key): [mɛw˧˧]
  • (Huế) IPA(key): [mɛw˧˧]
  • (Hồ Chí Minh City) IPA(key): [mɛw˧˧]

Etymology 1[edit]

Onomatopoeic.

Interjection[edit]

meo • (猫)

  1. (onomatopoeia) meow; miaow
Related terms[edit]
  • méo; mèo
  • Mẹo; Mão
  • miêu

Etymology 2[edit]

Non-Sino-Vietnamese reading of Chinese 毛 (SV: mao).

Noun[edit]

meo • (苗)

  1. (now not used in isolation) mold; mildew
    • 1937, Ngô Tất Tố, chapter 5, in Trong rừng nho:
      Giở đến hộp mực thì ra nước đã cạn khô. Lớp bông mực lên meo trắng xóa.
      When he opened the ink box, the ink had already gone dry. The inkpad was white due to mold infestation.
