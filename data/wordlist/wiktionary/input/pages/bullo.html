Catalan[edit]

Verb[edit]

bullo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of bullir

Galician[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈbuʎo̝/

Etymology 1[edit]

From bagullo, from baga (“grape, berry”).

Noun[edit]

bullo m (plural bullos)

  1. pomace
    Synonyms: bagaño, bagazo
  2. cud
    Synonyms: gaspallo, remoallo, rumiallo

Etymology 2[edit]

From abullar.

Noun[edit]

bullo m (plural bullos)

  1. (geography) muddy well, spring or puddle
    Synonyms: bulleiro, lamazal, lameiro, trollo
Derived terms[edit]
  • bulleiro

Etymology 3[edit]

Verb[edit]

bullo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of bullar

Ilocano[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Hyphenation: bul‧ló
  • IPA(key): /bulˈlu/, [bʊlˈlo]

Noun[edit]

bulló

  1. dislocation
  2. dismemberment

Derived terms[edit]

  • mabullo
  • nablo
 

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Middle High German buole (“friend”). Compare German Buhle and English bully.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈbul.lo/
  • Rhymes: -ullo
  • Hyphenation: bùl‧lo

Noun[edit]

bullo m (plural bulli)

  1. tough, tough guy, bully, guy

Derived terms[edit]

  • bullismo

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From bulla +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbul.loː/, [ˈbʊlːʲoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbul.lo/, [ˈbulːo]

Verb[edit]

bullō (present infinitive bullāre, perfect active bullāvī, supine bullātum); first conjugation

  1. to bubble, boil, effervesce
