Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈkɔm.pu.to/
  • Rhymes: -ɔmputo
  • Hyphenation: còm‧pu‧to

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Latin compŭtus. Doublet of compito and conto.

Noun[edit]

computo m (plural computi)

  1. counting, calculation

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

computo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of computare

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • conputō

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ putō (“I reckon”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkom.pu.toː/, [ˈkɔmpʊt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkom.pu.to/, [ˈkɔmput̪o]

Verb[edit]

computō (present infinitive computāre, perfect active computāvī, supine computātum); first conjugation

  1. to calculate, compute, reckon together
    Synonyms: numerō, ēnumerō, recēnseō
