Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

New Latin; from crassus (“stout”) +‎ rostrum (“beak”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kras.siˈroːs.tris/, [kräs̠ːɪˈroːs̠t̪rɪs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kras.siˈros.tris/, [kräsːiˈrɔst̪ris]

Adjective[edit]

crassirōstris (neuter crassirōstre); third-declension two-termination adjective

  1. (New Latin) having a stout beak

Usage notes[edit]

  • Used almost exclusively as a taxonomic epithet and thus not normally in inflected forms other than the nominative singular.

Declension[edit]
