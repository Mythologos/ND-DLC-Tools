Italian[edit]

Verb[edit]

oppilo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of oppilare

Anagrams[edit]

  • loppio, polipo, popoli

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • obpīlō

Etymology[edit]

From ob- (“against”, “facing”) +‎ pīlō (“I ram down or fix firmly”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /opˈpiː.loː/, [ɔpˈpiːɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /opˈpi.lo/, [opˈpiːlo]

Verb[edit]

oppīlō (present infinitive oppīlāre, perfect active oppīlāvī, supine oppīlātum); first conjugation

  1. to block (stop up)
