Galician[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From a derivative of Latin medius.[1]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • meolo, molo

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmolo̝/

Noun[edit]

molo m (plural molos)

  1. central piece of the Galician cart wheel, which is united on its extremes with the cambas, or curved pieces of the wheel's felly, and has in its center the wheel's nave
    Synonyms: mil, mión, miúl

Etymology 2[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • miolo

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmolo̝/

Noun[edit]

molo m (plural molos)

  1. Alternative form of miolo

Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmɔ.lo/
  • Rhymes: -ɔlo
  • Hyphenation: mò‧lo

Etymology 1[edit]

Of Ligurian origin, from Ancient Greek μῶλος (môlos), μόλος (mólos), itself from Latin mōlēs.

Noun[edit]

molo m (plural moli)

  1. jetty, pier, mole, quay, dock

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *melam (“to grind”). Cognate with Latin mollis, Ancient Greek μύλη (múlē), English meal. See also English maelstrom.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈmo.loː/, [ˈmɔɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈmo.lo/, [ˈmɔːlo]

Verb[edit]

molō (present infinitive molere, perfect active moluī, supine molitum); third conjugation

  1. to grind or mill
