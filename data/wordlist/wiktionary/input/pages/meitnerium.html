English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
meitnerium
Wikipedia
Chemical element
Mt
Previous: hassium (Hs)
Next: darmstadtium (Ds)

Etymology[edit]

Meitner +‎ -ium; named for Lise Meitner.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: mītnēr'ēəm, IPA(key): /maɪtˈnɪəɹi.əm/[1]
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɪəɹiəm

Noun[edit]

meitnerium (uncountable)

  1. A transuranic chemical element (symbol Mt) with atomic number 109.

Synonyms[edit]

  • eka-iridium (used before the element was discovered)
  • unnilennium (used before the element was officially named)

Related terms[edit]

  • Meitner
  • meitnerate
