English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
tricolour
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

tri- +‎ color

Adjective[edit]

tricolor (not comparable)

  1. (American spelling) Alternative form of tricolour

Noun[edit]

tricolor (plural tricolors)

  1. (American spelling) Alternative form of tricolour

Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From tri- +‎ color.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central) [tɾi.kuˈlor]
  • IPA(key): (Balearic, Valencian) [tɾi.koˈlor]
  • Rhymes: -oɾ

Adjective[edit]

tricolor m or f (masculine and feminine plural tricolors)

  1. tricolour, three-coloured

Noun[edit]

tricolor f (plural tricolors)

  1. tricolour (flag)

Further reading[edit]

  • “tricolor” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.

French[edit]

Adjective[edit]

tricolor (feminine tricolore, masculine plural tricolors, feminine plural tricolores)

  1. Obsolete form of tricolore.

Further reading[edit]

  • “tricolore”, in Trésor de la langue française informatisé [Digitized Treasury of the French Language], 2012.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

tri- +‎ color

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtri.ko.lor/, [ˈt̪rɪkɔɫ̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtri.ko.lor/, [ˈt̪riːkolor]

Adjective[edit]

tricolor (genitive tricolōris); third-declension one-termination adjective

  1. three-coloured

Romanian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French tricolore, from Latin tricolor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Adjective[edit]

tricolor m or n (feminine singular tricoloră, masculine plural tricolori, feminine and neuter plural tricolore)

  1. trichromatic

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /tɾikoˈloɾ/ [t̪ɾi.koˈloɾ]
  • Rhymes: -oɾ
  • Syllabification: tri‧co‧lor

Adjective[edit]

tricolor m or f (masculine and feminine plural tricolores)

  1. tricolor (having three colors)
    Synonym: tricrómico

Derived terms[edit]

  • falaropo tricolor
 

Noun[edit]

tricolor m (plural tricolores)

  1. tricolour (flag)
  2. (soccer, preceded by "el") the Mexican national football team
    Synonym: tri

Noun[edit]

tricolor f (plural tricolores)

  1. (soccer, preceded by "la") the Ecuador national football team
    Synonym: tri
