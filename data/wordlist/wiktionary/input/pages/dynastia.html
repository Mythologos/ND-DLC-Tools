Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek δυναστεία (dunasteía, “lordship, dominion”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /dy.nasˈtiː.a/, [d̪ʏnäs̠ˈt̪iːä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /di.nasˈti.a/, [d̪inäsˈt̪iːä]

Noun[edit]

dynastīa f (genitive dynastīae); first declension

  1. (Late Latin) rule, dominion
  2. (Medieval Latin, New Latin) dynasty

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Medieval Latin dynastīa, from Ancient Greek δυναστεία (dunasteía, “power, dominion”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /dɨˈnas.tja/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -astja
  • Syllabification: dy‧nas‧tia

Noun[edit]

dynastia f (diminutive dynastyjka)

  1. dynasty, house (series of rulers or dynasts from one family)
