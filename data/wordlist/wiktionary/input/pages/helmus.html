Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • elmus, hermus

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Frankish *helm (“helmet”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈhel.mus/, [ˈhɛɫ̪mʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈel.mus/, [ˈɛlmus]

Noun[edit]

helmus m (genitive helmī); second declension[1][2][3]

  1. helmet

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative helmus helmī
Genitive helmī helmōrum
Dative helmō helmīs
Accusative helmum helmōs
Ablative helmō helmīs
Vocative helme helmī

        • Italian: elmetto
