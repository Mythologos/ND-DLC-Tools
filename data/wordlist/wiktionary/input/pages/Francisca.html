Cebuano[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Spanish Francisca, from Latin Francisca.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Hyphenation: fran‧cis‧ca

Proper noun[edit]

Francisca

  1. (dated) a female given name

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /franˈkis.ka/, [fräŋˈkɪs̠kä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /franˈt͡ʃis.ka/, [frän̠ʲˈt͡ʃiskä]

Proper noun[edit]

Francisca f (genitive Franciscae); first declension

  1. a female given name, equivalent to English Frances

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative Francisca Franciscae
Genitive Franciscae Franciscārum
Dative Franciscae Franciscīs
Accusative Franciscam Franciscās
Ablative Franciscā Franciscīs
Vocative Francisca Franciscae

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin Francisca.

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /fɾɐ̃ˈsis.kɐ/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /fɾɐ̃ˈsis.kɐ/
    • (Rio de Janeiro) IPA(key): /fɾɐ̃ˈsiʃ.kɐ/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /fɾɐ̃ˈsis.ka/
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /fɾɐ̃ˈsiʃ.kɐ/

  • Hyphenation: Fran‧cis‧ca

Proper noun[edit]

Francisca f (plural Franscicas)

  1. a female given name from Latin, equivalent to English Frances
    Synonym: (pet name) Chica

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Spain) /fɾanˈθiska/ [fɾãn̟ˈθis.ka]
  • IPA(key): (Latin America) /fɾanˈsiska/ [fɾãnˈsis.ka]
  • Rhymes: -iska
  • Syllabification: Fran‧cis‧ca

Proper noun[edit]

Francisca f

  1. a female given name
