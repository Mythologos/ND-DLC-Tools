Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • battālia (Late Latin)

Etymology[edit]

From battuō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /bat.tuˈaː.li.a/, [bät̪ːuˈäːlʲiä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /bat.tuˈa.li.a/, [bät̪ːuˈäːliä]

Noun[edit]

battuālia f (genitive battuāliae); first declension or battuālia n pl (genitive battuālium); third declension

  1. military exercises (originally for gladiators)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative battuālia battuāliae
Genitive battuāliae battuāliārum
Dative battuāliae battuāliīs

Ablative battuāliā battuāliīs

Sometimes this word is treated as a neuter plurale tantum: Third-declension noun (neuter, “pure” i-stem), plural only.
