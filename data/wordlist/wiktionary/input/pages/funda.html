English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Contraction of fundamental, originating in Indian English.

Noun[edit]

funda (countable and uncountable, plural fundas or fundae or fundaes)

  1. (India) Basics or fundamentals, considered as a unit.
    Get your funda correct before you submit the paper.
    What is the funda behind relativity?
    • 1992 [1]
      In my opinion, the funda is as follows:
  2. (India) Understanding.
    • 1993, [2]
      You did not have the funda to realize that the statistics is an ass.
    • 1993, [3]
      But then, your funda is weak.

Usage notes[edit]

Whilst fundaes and fundae both occur as the plural of funda, fundas is far more widespread. The pluralization to fundae is by analogy to formula/formulae. The pluralization to fundas is by the simple addition of -s.

The word is frequently used in Indian quizzing circles to denote the interesting fact or concept behind a question.

Galician[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Attested with this meaning since the 14th century. From Latin funda. Doublet of fonda. Compare Portuguese fonda and Spanish funda and honda.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈfundɐ]

Noun[edit]

funda f (plural fundas)

  1. case, cover
    Synonym: estoxo
    • c. 1350, Lourdes Soriano, editor, Livro de Tristan:
      Quando eles uirõ Lãça[rote] nõ no conoçerõ porque fezera el meter seu escudo em sua fonda
      When they saw Lancelot they didn't recognise him, because he ordered to put his shield inside its case
  2. overalls
    Synonym: mono
