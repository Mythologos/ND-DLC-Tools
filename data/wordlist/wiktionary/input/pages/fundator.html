Interlingua[edit]

Noun[edit]

fundator (plural fundatores)

  1. founder

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From fundō (“found, establish”) +‎ -tor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /funˈdaː.tor/, [fʊn̪ˈd̪äːt̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /funˈda.tor/, [fun̪ˈd̪äːt̪or]

Noun[edit]

fundātor m (genitive fundātōris); third declension

  1. (rare) founder

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative fundātor fundātōrēs
Genitive fundātōris fundātōrum
Dative fundātōrī fundātōribus
Accusative fundātōrem fundātōrēs
Ablative fundātōre fundātōribus
Vocative fundātor fundātōrēs

Synonyms[edit]

  • (founder): conditor, creātor

Related terms[edit]

  • fundāmen
  • fundāmentum
  • fundātiō
  • fundātus
  • fundō

Descendants[edit]

  • Catalan: fundador
  • French: fondateur
  • Italian: fondatore
  • Portuguese: fundador
  • Romanian: fondator, fundator
  • Spanish: fundador

Verb[edit]

fundātor

  1. second/third-person singular future passive imperative of fundō

Polish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin fundātor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /funˈda.tɔr/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -atɔr
  • Syllabification: fun‧da‧tor

Noun[edit]

fundator m pers (female equivalent fundatorka)

  1. founder
  2. donor, backer, sponsor
    Synonyms: (archaic) benefaktor, (archaic) dobrodziej, dobroczyńca, ofiarodawca
