Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Frankish *bastijan (“to sew, weave”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbas.ti.oː/, [ˈbäs̠t̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbas.ti.o/, [ˈbäst̪io]

Verb[edit]

bastiō (present infinitive bastīre, perfect active bastīvī, supine bastītum); fourth conjugation

  1. (Medieval Latin) to build, construct
  2. (Medieval Latin) to sew, weave
