Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Dacian olma.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈol.ma/, [ˈɔɫ̪mä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈol.ma/, [ˈɔlmä]

Noun[edit]

olma f (genitive olmae); first declension

  1. The plant ebulum among the Dacians

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Romansch[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin anima, possibly through a dissimilated Vulgar Latin form *alima. Compare to Italian, Spanish and Portuguese alma.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈolmə]

Noun[edit]

olma f (plural olmas)

  1. soul

Turkish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /oɫmɑ/

Noun[edit]

olma (definite accusative olmayı, plural olmalar)

  1. verbal noun of olmak

Verb[edit]

olma

  1. second-person singular negative imperative of olmak
