English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin phaseolus.

Noun[edit]

phaseolus (plural phaseoli)

  1. any of several domesticated beans, of the genus Phaseolus, native to the Americas

References[edit]

  • Phaseolus on Wikipedia.Wikipedia
  • Phaseolus on Wikispecies.Wikispecies
  • Phaseolus on Wikimedia Commons.Wikimedia Commons

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • faseolus

Etymology[edit]

Diminutive of phasēlus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /pʰaˈse.o.lus/, [pʰäˈs̠eɔɫ̪ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /faˈse.o.lus/, [fäˈs̬ɛːolus]

Noun[edit]

phaseolus m (genitive phaseolī); second declension

  1. A type of bean with an edible pod; in Antiquity: cowpea, New Latin: kidney bean.

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.
