Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Internationalism (see English allegory), ultimately from Latin allēgoria.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɑlːeɡoriɑ/, [ˈɑ̝lːe̞ˌɡo̞riɑ̝]
  • Rhymes: -oriɑ
  • Syllabification(key): al‧le‧go‧ri‧a

Noun[edit]

allegoria

  1. allegory

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin allēgoria.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /al.le.ɡoˈri.a/
  • Rhymes: -ia
  • Hyphenation: al‧le‧go‧rì‧a

Noun[edit]

allegoria f (plural allegorie)

  1. allegory
  2. parable

Related terms[edit]

  • allegorico

Further reading[edit]

  • allegoria in Treccani.it – Vocabolario Treccani on line, Istituto dell'Enciclopedia Italiana

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek ἀλληγορία (allēgoría).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /al.leːˈɡo.ri.a/, [älːʲeːˈɡɔriä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /al.leˈɡo.ri.a/, [älːeˈɡɔːriä]

Noun[edit]

allēgoria f (genitive allēgoriae); first declension

  1. allegory
  2. parable

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.
