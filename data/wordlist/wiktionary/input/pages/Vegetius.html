English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From the Latin Vegetius.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Received Pronunciation) IPA(key): /vɪˈɡɛtɪəs/, /vɪˈdʒɛtɪəs/

Proper noun[edit]

Vegetius

  1. A male given name from Latin — famously held by:
    1. Publius Flavius Vegetius Renatus (fl. late 4th C.), Late Latin writer on military matters and veterinary medicine

Translations[edit]

male given name of Latin origin
Publius Flavius Vegetius Renatus

Further reading[edit]

  • Publius Flavius Vegetius Renatus on Wikipedia.Wikipedia

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Probably vegetus (“vigorous”) +‎ -ius.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /u̯eˈɡe.ti.us/, [u̯ɛˈɡɛt̪iʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /veˈd͡ʒet.t͡si.us/, [veˈd͡ʒɛt̪ː͡s̪ius]

Proper noun[edit]

Vegetius m sg (genitive Vegetiī or Vegetī); second declension

  1. A male cognomen — famously held by:
    1. Publius Flavius Vegetius Renatus (fl. late 4th C.), Late Latin writer on military matters and veterinary medicine

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun, singular only.
