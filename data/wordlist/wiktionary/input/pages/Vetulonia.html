Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From the Latin Vetulōnia.

Proper noun[edit]

Vetulonia ?

  1. a part of Castiglione della Pescaia in Tuscany; once an Etruscan town

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

vetulus (“little old”, adjective) +‎ -ōnia ≈ “little old town”

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /u̯e.tuˈloː.ni.a/, [u̯ɛt̪ʊˈɫ̪oːniä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ve.tuˈlo.ni.a/, [vet̪uˈlɔːniä]

Proper noun[edit]

Vetulōnia f sg (genitive Vetulōniae); first declension

  1. A town in Etruria
