Translingual[edit]

Proper noun[edit]

Eunomia

  1. A taxonomic genus within the family Erebidae – certain moths.

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Εὐνομία (Eunomía, “Order of Law, Good Order”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /juˈnoʊmiə/

Proper noun[edit]

Eunomia

  1. (Greek mythology) One of the Horae.
  2. (astronomy) 15 Eunomia, a main belt asteroid.

Synonyms[edit]

  • (Horae): Auxo (the Grower), Carpo (the Fruit-bringer), Thallo (the Plant-raiser), Dike (Justice), Eirene (Peace)
  • (astronomy): 15 Eunomia
