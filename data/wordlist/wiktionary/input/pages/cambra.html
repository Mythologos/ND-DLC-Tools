Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Latin camera, from Ancient Greek καμάρα (kamára). Compare Occitan cambra and chambra. Doublet of càmera, a latinism.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [ˈkam.bɾə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [ˈkam.bɾa]
  • Audio(file)

Noun[edit]

cambra f (plural cambres)

  1. room
  2. bedroom
  3. chamber
  4. (euphemistic) stool

Derived terms[edit]

  • anar de cambra
  • antecambra
  • avantcambra
  • cambra de gas
  • cambrejar
  • cambrer
  • cambres clares
  • cambreta
  • cambró
  • de cambra
  • recambra

French[edit]

Verb[edit]

cambra

  1. third-person singular past historic of cambrer

Galician[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Perhaps ultimately from Proto-Germanic *krampō (“cramp”). Cognate with Portuguese cãibra and Spanish calambre; see also French crampe. Compare also Italian cambra, French cambre, from a different origin.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈkambɾa̝/

Noun[edit]

cambra m (plural cambras)

  1. cramp (a painful contraction of a muscle which cannot be controlled)
    Synonym: breca

Related terms[edit]

  • grampa (“clamp”)
  • grampo (“clamp”)

References[edit]

  • “cambra” in Dicionario de Dicionarios da lingua galega, SLI - ILGA 2006–2013.
  • “cambra” in Tesouro informatizado da lingua galega. Santiago: ILG.
  • “cambra” in Álvarez, Rosario (coord.): Tesouro do léxico patrimonial galego e portugués, Santiago de Compostela: Instituto da Lingua Galega.


Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French cambre, from Picard or Norman, from Old Northern French, derived from Latin camur (“curved, bent”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈkam.bra/
  • Rhymes: -ambra
  • Hyphenation: càm‧bra

Noun[edit]

cambra f (plural cambre)

  1. cramp, clamp
    Synonym: grappa

Related terms[edit]

  • cambretta

Latin[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkam.bra/, [ˈkämbrä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkam.bra/, [ˈkämbrä]

Noun[edit]

cambra f (genitive cambrae); first declension

Old Occitan[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • chambra

Etymology[edit]

From Latin camera, from Ancient Greek καμάρα (kamára). Gallo-Romance cognate with Old French chambre.

Noun[edit]

cambra f (oblique plural cambras, nominative singular cambra, nominative plural cambras)

  1. room, in particular a bedroom

Descendants[edit]

  • Occitan: cambra, chambra

References[edit]

  • Walther von Wartburg (1928–2002), “camera”, in Französisches Etymologisches Wörterbuch (in German), volume 2: C Q K, page 130

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Latin camara, from Ancient Greek καμάρα (kamára). Doublet of cámara.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈkambɾa/ [ˈkãm.bɾa]
  • Rhymes: -ambɾa
  • Syllabification: cam‧bra

Noun[edit]

cambra f (plural cambras) (obsolete)

  1. room
    Synonym: cuarto
  2. bedroom
    Synonym: habitación
