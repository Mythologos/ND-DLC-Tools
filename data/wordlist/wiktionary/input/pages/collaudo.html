Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /kolˈlaw.do/
  • Rhymes: -awdo
  • Hyphenation: col‧làu‧do

Etymology 1[edit]

From collaudare.

Noun[edit]

collaudo m (plural collaudi)

  1. testing, trialing
  2. test, trial

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

collaudo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of collaudare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ laudō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kolˈlau̯.doː/, [kɔlˈlʲäu̯d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kolˈlau̯.do/, [kolˈläːu̯d̪o]

Verb[edit]

collaudō (present infinitive collaudāre, perfect active collaudāvī, supine collaudātum); first conjugation

  1. to praise or extol highly
    Synonyms: admīror, laudō, amplificō, mīrō, suspiciō, augeō, praedicō, intueor
  2. to commend
