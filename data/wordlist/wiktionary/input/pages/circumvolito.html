Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

circum- +‎ volitō (“to fly about”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈu̯o.li.toː/, [kɪrkʊmˈu̯ɔlʲɪt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈvo.li.to/, [t͡ʃirkumˈvɔːlit̪o]

Verb[edit]

circumvolitō (present infinitive circumvolitāre, perfect active circumvolitāvī, supine circumvolitātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive, intransitive) to fly around
  2. (figurative, of a person) to roam about, hover around
