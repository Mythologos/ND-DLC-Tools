Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /afˈfi.do/
  • Rhymes: -ido
  • Hyphenation: af‧fì‧do

Etymology 1[edit]

Deverbal from affidare (“to entrust”) +‎ -o.

Noun[edit]

affido m (plural affidi)

  1. (law) Synonym of affidamento (“custody (of a child)”)
Related terms[edit]
  • affidabile
  • affidabilità
  • affidamento
  • affidare
 

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

affido

  1. first-person singular present indicative of affidare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

ad- (“to”) +‎ fīdō (“trust”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /afˈfiː.doː/, [äfˈfiːd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /afˈfi.do/, [äfˈfiːd̪o]

Verb[edit]

affīdō (present infinitive affīdāre, perfect active affīdāvī, supine affīdātum); first conjugation

  1. (Medieval Latin) to swear, to promise fidelity
