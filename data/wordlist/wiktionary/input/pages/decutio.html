Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

dē- +‎ quatiō (“to shake”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deːˈku.ti.oː/, [d̪eːˈkʊt̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /deˈkut.t͡si.o/, [d̪eˈkut̪ː͡s̪io]

Verb[edit]

dēcutiō (present infinitive dēcutere, perfect active dēcussī, supine dēcussum); third conjugation iō-variant

  1. (transitive) to shake off, throw aside
  2. (transitive) to strike down
    Synonym: percutiō
