English Wikipedia has an article about Pisces.

Etymology[edit]

From Latin piscēs, the nominative plural of piscis (“fish”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈpaɪsiːz/, /ˈpɪsiːz/
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɪsiːz

Proper noun[edit]

Pisces (symbol ♓︎)

  2. (astronomy) The Fish, a Greco-Roman constellation of the zodiac named for its presumed resemblance to a pair of fish
  4. (astrology) The zodiac sign for the fish, supposed to be ruled by Neptune and to influence events occurring between February 20 and March 20 (tropical) or, less often, between March 15 and April 14 (sidereal).

Derived terms[edit]

  • Piscean
  • Piscium

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From the plural form of piscis (“fish”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈpis.keːs/, [ˈpɪs̠keːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpiʃ.ʃes/, [ˈpiʃːes]

Proper noun[edit]

Piscēs m pl (genitive Piscium); third declension

  1. the Fishes, Pisces (constellation)

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun (i-stem), plural only.
