Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From sub- (“under, beneath; behind”) +‎ legō (“gather, choose”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsub.le.ɡoː/, [ˈs̠ʊbɫ̪ɛɡoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsub.le.ɡo/, [ˈsubleɡo]

Verb[edit]

sublegō (present infinitive sublegere, perfect active sublēgī, supine sublēctum); third conjugation

  1. to gather or pick up or from below, gather or search for underneath
  2. to take away secretly or by stealth; listen to secretly
  3. to choose or elect in the place of another, appoint, substitute
