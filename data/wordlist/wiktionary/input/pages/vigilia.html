Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From vigil.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /u̯iˈɡi.li.a/, [u̯ɪˈɡɪlʲiä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /viˈd͡ʒi.li.a/, [viˈd͡ʒiːliä]

Noun[edit]

vigilia f (genitive vigiliae); first declension

  1. watch, vigil
  2. wakefulness
  3. city guard, constabulary, police

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin vigilia.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /biˈxilja/ [biˈxi.lja]
  • Rhymes: -ilja
  • Syllabification: vi‧gi‧lia

Noun[edit]

vigilia f (plural vigilias)

  1. wakefulness, being awake
  2. watchfulness
  3. vigil
  4. (religion) fast, abstinence
  5. night shift
  6. eve (the day or night before)
    Synonym: víspera
