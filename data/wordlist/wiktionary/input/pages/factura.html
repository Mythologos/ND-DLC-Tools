Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin factūra.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [fəkˈtu.ɾə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [fakˈtu.ɾa]

Noun[edit]

factura f (plural factures)

  1. bill, invoice

Derived terms[edit]

  • facturar

Further reading[edit]

  • “factura” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.

French[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Homophones: facturas, facturât

Verb[edit]

factura

  1. third-person singular past historic of facturer

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • factūra: (Classical) IPA(key): /fakˈtuː.ra/, [fäkˈt̪uːrä]
  • factūra: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /fakˈtu.ra/, [fäkˈt̪uːrä]
  • factūrā: (Classical) IPA(key): /fakˈtuː.raː/, [fäkˈt̪uːräː]
  • factūrā: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /fakˈtu.ra/, [fäkˈt̪uːrä]

Etymology 1[edit]

From faciō +‎ -tūra.

Noun[edit]

factūra f (genitive factūrae); first declension

  1. a making, manufacture
  2. a thing that has been manufactured
Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

factura f (plural facturas)

  1. Pre-reform spelling (until Brazil 1943/Portugal 1990) of fatura. Still used in countries where the agreement hasn't come into effect; may occur as a sporadic misspelling.

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

factura

  1. inflection of facturar:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French facturer.

Verb[edit]

a factura (third-person singular present facturează, past participle facturat) 1st conj.

  1. to charge, to invoice, to bill

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /faɡˈtuɾa/ [faɣ̞ˈt̪u.ɾa]

Borrowed from Latin factūra. Compare hechura, inherited from the same source.

Noun[edit]

factura f (plural facturas)

  1. bill, invoice
  2. (Argentina) Danish pastry
    Synonyms: pan danés, masa danesa, (Uruguay) bizcocho
