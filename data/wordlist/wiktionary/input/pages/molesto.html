Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From molestus

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /moˈles.toː/, [mɔˈɫ̪ɛs̠t̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /moˈles.to/, [moˈlɛst̪o]

Verb[edit]

molestō (present infinitive molestāre, perfect active molestāvī, supine molestātum); first conjugation

  1. to bother, harass

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /moˈlesto/ [moˈles.t̪o]
  • Rhymes: -esto
  • Syllabification: mo‧les‧to

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin molestus.

Adjective[edit]

molesto (feminine molesta, masculine plural molestos, feminine plural molestas)

  1. annoyed, vexed
  2. annoying, obtrusive
  3. uncomfortable
