Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek καρχαρίας (karkharías, “shark”), from κάρχαρος (kárkharos, “sharp, jagged”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /karˈkʰa.ri.aːs/, [kärˈkʰäriäːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /karˈka.ri.as/, [kärˈkäːriäs]

Noun[edit]

carchariās m (genitive carchariae); first declension

  1. (New Latin) dogfish (shark)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun (masculine Greek-type with nominative singular in -ās).
