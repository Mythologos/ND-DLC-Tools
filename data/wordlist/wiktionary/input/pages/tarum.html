Indonesian[edit]

Indonesian Wikipedia has an article on:
Tarum
Wikipedia id

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Malay tarum, from Classical Malay تاروم (tarum), from Proto-Malayic *tarum, from Proto-Malayo-Polynesian *taʀum. Doublet of tom.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈt̪a.rʊm]
  • Hyphenation: ta‧rum

Noun[edit]

tarum (first-person possessive tarumku, second-person possessive tarummu, third-person possessive tarumnya)

  1. true indigo, Indigofera tinctoria
    Synonyms: nila, indigo

Derived terms[edit]

  • tarum akar
  • tarum areui
  • tarum daun alus
  • tarum kembang
 

Further reading[edit]

  • “tarum” in Kamus Besar Bahasa Indonesia, Jakarta: Language Development and Fostering Agency — Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology of the Republic Indonesia, 2016.

Unknown[1]

Noun[edit]

tarum n (genitive tarī); second declension

  1. An aloe wood

Malay[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Malayic *tarum, from Proto-Malayo-Polynesian *taʀum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /tarum/
  • (Johor-Riau) IPA(key): [ˈt̪ä.rʊm], [ˈt̪ärom]
  • Rhymes: -rum, -um, -rom, -om

Noun[edit]

tarum (Jawi spelling تاروم, plural tarum-tarum, informal 1st possessive tarumku, 2nd possessive tarummu, 3rd possessive tarumnya)

  1. true indigo, Indigofera tinctoria

Derived terms[edit]

  • akar tarum
  • tarum hutan

Descendants[edit]

  • Indonesian: tarum
