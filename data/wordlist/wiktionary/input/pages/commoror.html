Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ moror.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkom.mo.ror/, [ˈkɔmːɔrɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkom.mo.ror/, [ˈkɔmːoror]

Verb[edit]

commoror (present infinitive commorārī, perfect active commorātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. to stop, stay or remain
  2. to linger or delay
  3. to detain or be detained
