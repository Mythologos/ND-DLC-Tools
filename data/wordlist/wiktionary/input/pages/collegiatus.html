Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From collēgium (“college”) +‎ -ātus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kol.leː.ɡiˈaː.tus/, [kɔlːʲeːɡiˈäːt̪ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kol.le.d͡ʒiˈa.tus/, [kolːed͡ʒiˈäːt̪us]

Noun[edit]

collēgiātus m (genitive collēgiātī); second declension

  1. A fellow member of a college, corporation, etc.; collegiate, collegian.

Related terms[edit]

Adjective[edit]

collēgiātus (feminine collēgiāta, neuter collēgiātum); first/second-declension adjective

  1. collegiate
  2. corporate
