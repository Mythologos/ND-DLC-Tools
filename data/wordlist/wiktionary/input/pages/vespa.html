Catalan[edit]

Catalan Wikipedia has an article on:
vespa
Wikipedia ca

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Old Catalan vespa, from Latin vespa, from Proto-Indo-European *wobʰseh₂ (“wasp”), from *webʰ- (“to weave”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central) [ˈbes.pə]
  • IPA(key): (Balearic) [ˈves.pə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [ˈves.pa]
  • Audio(file)

Noun[edit]

vespa f (plural vespes)

  1. wasp

Related terms[edit]

  • vesper

References[edit]

  • “vespa” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.
  • “vespa”, in Gran Diccionari de la Llengua Catalana, Grup Enciclopèdia Catalana, 2024
  • “vespa” in Diccionari normatiu valencià, Acadèmia Valenciana de la Llengua.
  • “vespa” in Diccionari català-valencià-balear, Antoni Maria Alcover and Francesc de Borja Moll, 1962.

Etymology[edit]

Generalized from the Italian motor scooter brand name Vespa.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈʋespɑ/, [ˈʋe̞s̠pɑ̝]
  • Rhymes: -espɑ
  • Syllabification(key): ves‧pa

Noun[edit]

vespa (informal)

  1. scooter (motorcycle or moped equipped with a platform for the operator's feet)

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *wospā, metathesized from earlier Proto-Italic *wopsā, from Proto-Indo-European *wops-éh₂, from *wóps; see also wasp.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈu̯es.pa/, [ˈu̯ɛs̠pä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈves.pa/, [ˈvɛspä]

Noun[edit]

vespa f (genitive vespae); first declension

  1. wasp
