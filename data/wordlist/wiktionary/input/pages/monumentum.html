Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • monimentum

Etymology[edit]

From monēre (“to remind”) +‎ -mentum (noun suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /mo.nuˈmen.tum/, [mɔnʊˈmɛn̪t̪ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /mo.nuˈmen.tum/, [monuˈmɛn̪t̪um]

Noun[edit]

monumentum n (genitive monumentī); second declension

  1. reminder, memorial
  2. monument
    • 8 CE, Ovid, Fasti 6.195:
      haec hominum monumenta patent
      These monuments of men are evident.
  3. tomb, burial place

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
