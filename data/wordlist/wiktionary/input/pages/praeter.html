Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • prēter (Medieval Latin)

Etymology[edit]

Continuing Proto-Italic *praiteros, from Proto-Indo-European *préh₂i (“before, across”) + *-teros (“contrastive suffix”). Equivalent to prae +‎ -ter.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈprae̯.ter/, [ˈpräe̯t̪ɛr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpre.ter/, [ˈprɛːt̪er]

Preposition[edit]

praeter (+ accusative)

  1. past, by (of motion)
  2. besides, except
    Synonyms: praeterquam, nisi
  3. beyond
  4. more than
  5. contrary

Descendants[edit]

  • → English: preter-, praeter-, præter-
  • → Esperanto: preter
