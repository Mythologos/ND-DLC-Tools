Alternative forms[edit]

  • Sabbatum
  • *sambatum (Vulgar Latin) (Compare the etymologies of French samedi (“lit., Sabbath day, now Saturday”), German Samstag (“lit., Sabbath day, now Saturday”))

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek σάββατον (sábbaton, “Sabbath”), from Biblical Hebrew שַׁבָּת (šabbāṯ, “Sabbath”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsab.ba.tum/, [ˈs̠äbːät̪ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsab.ba.tum/, [ˈsäbːät̪um]

Noun[edit]

sabbatum n (genitive sabbatī); second declension

  1. Sabbath (the Jewish day of rest, i.e., the biblical seventh day of the week)
  2. (Late Latin, Ecclesiastical Latin) Saturday

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
