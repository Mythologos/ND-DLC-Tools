Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dis- +‎ lavō (“I wash”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈdiː.lu.oː/, [ˈd̪iːɫ̪uoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈdi.lu.o/, [ˈd̪iːluo]

Verb[edit]

dīluō (present infinitive dīluere, perfect active dīluī, supine dīlūtum); third conjugation, limited passive

  1. (transitive) to wash away
  2. (transitive) to drench or moisten
  3. (transitive) to purge, clear or empty (the bowels)
  4. (transitive) to dissolve, cause to melt
  5. (transitive) to dilute
  6. (transitive) to dissipate
