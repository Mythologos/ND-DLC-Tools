English[edit]

Chemical element
Cd
Previous: silver (Ag)
Next: indium (In)

Etymology[edit]

1817, from Ancient Greek Καδμεία (Kadmeía, “calamine”), a cadmium-bearing mixture of minerals, which was named after the king Κάδμος (Kádmos, “Cadmus”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: kăd'mēəm, IPA(key): /ˈkædmiəm/
    • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Hyphenation: cad‧mi‧um

Noun[edit]

cadmium (countable and uncountable, plural cadmiums)

English Wikipedia has an article on:
cadmium
Wikipedia
  2. A chemical element (symbol Cd) with an atomic number of 48: a soft, silvery-white metal.
  3. (countable) A single atom of this element.
