Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin ruptūra.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [rupˈtu.ɾə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [rupˈtu.ɾa]

Noun[edit]

ruptura f (plural ruptures)

  1. rupture, breaking, breakup

Related terms[edit]

  • rompre

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From rumpō (“break, burst”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /rupˈtuː.ra/, [rʊpˈt̪uːrä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /rupˈtu.ra/, [rupˈt̪uːrä]

Noun[edit]

ruptūra f (genitive ruptūrae); first declension

  1. breaking or breaking off of a limb; fracture, breach, rupture

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative ruptūra ruptūrae
Genitive ruptūrae ruptūrārum
Dative ruptūrae ruptūrīs
Accusative ruptūram ruptūrās
Ablative ruptūrā ruptūrīs
Vocative ruptūra ruptūrae

Synonyms[edit]

  • (fracture): ruptiō

Related terms[edit]

  • abrumpō
  • corrumpō

  • rumpō
  • ruptiō
  • ruptor
  • ruptus

  • Piedmontese: rotura
  • Portuguese: rotura, → ruptura, rutura
  • Romanian: ruptură
  • Sicilian: ruttura
  • Spanish: rotura, ruptura
  • Venetian: rotura

  1. ablative feminine singular of ruptūrus

Noun[edit]
