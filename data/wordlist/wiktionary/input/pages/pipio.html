Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈpiː.pi.oː/, [ˈpiːpioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpi.pi.o/, [ˈpiːpio]

Etymology 1[edit]

From pīpiō (“chirp”, verb; see etymology 2) +‎ -ō (noun-forming suffix).

Noun[edit]

pīpiō m (genitive pīpiōnis); third declension

  1. chirping bird
  2. squab

Etymology 2[edit]

Of imitative origin.

Verb[edit]

pīpiō (present infinitive pīpiāre); first conjugation, no passive, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to pipe or chirp

Etymology 3[edit]

From the previous verb.

Verb[edit]

pīpiō (present infinitive pīpiāre, perfect active pīpiāvī, supine pīpiātum); first conjugation

  1. to whimper
  2. (transitive) to pour out (whimpers)
