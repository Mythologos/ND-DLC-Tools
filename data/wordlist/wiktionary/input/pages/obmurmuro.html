Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

ob- +‎ murmurō (“to murmur, grumble”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /obˈmur.mu.roː/, [ɔmˈmʊrmʊroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /obˈmur.mu.ro/, [obˈmurmuro]

Verb[edit]

obmurmurō (present infinitive obmurmurāre, perfect active obmurmurāvī, supine obmurmurātum); first conjugation

  1. (intransitive) to murmur, grumble against, at, or to [+dative or accusative]
