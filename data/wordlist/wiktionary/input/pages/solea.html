Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Sound-symbolic. Related to Ingrian solliia and Karelian šolie.[1]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈsoleɑ/, [ˈs̠o̞le̞ɑ̝]
  • Rhymes: -oleɑ
  • Syllabification(key): so‧le‧a

Adjective[edit]

solea (comparative soleampi, superlative solein)

  1. slender, tall

Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
solea
Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

From solum (“bottom, base”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈso.le.a/, [ˈs̠ɔɫ̪eä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈso.le.a/, [ˈsɔːleä]

Noun[edit]

solea f (genitive soleae); first declension

  1. sandal
  2. sole of a shoe

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Synonyms[edit]

  • (all): crepida
  • (sandal): sandalium
