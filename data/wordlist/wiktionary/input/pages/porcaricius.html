Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From porcus (“pig”) +‎ -āricius. Attested in the Lex Alamannorum.[1]

Adjective[edit]

porcāricius (feminine porcāricia, neuter porcāricium); first/second-declension adjective (Early Medieval Latin)

  1. pertaining to a pig
