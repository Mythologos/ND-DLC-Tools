Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From prae (“before”) +‎ nōscō (“know”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /prae̯ˈnoːs.koː/, [präe̯ˈnoːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /preˈnos.ko/, [preˈnɔsko]

Verb[edit]

praenōscō (present infinitive praenōscere, perfect active praenōvī, supine praenōtum); third conjugation

  1. to foreknow
