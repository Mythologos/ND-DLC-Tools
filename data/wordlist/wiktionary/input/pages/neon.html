Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek νέον (néon), neuter of νέος (néos, “new”), from earlier *νέϝος (*néwos), from Proto-Hellenic *newos, from Proto-Indo-European *néwos.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: nē'ŏn, IPA(key): /ˈni.ɒn/
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • Rhymes: -iɒn
  • Hyphenation: ne‧on

Noun[edit]

neon (countable and uncountable, plural neons)

  2. (uncountable) The chemical element (symbol Ne) with an atomic number of 10. The second of the noble gases, it is a colourless, odorless inert gas.
  3. (countable) A form or sample of the element.
  4. (countable, uncountable) Neon signs or lights, collectively.
    • 2009, Jason Syner, How to Install Automotive Mobile Electronic Systems, page 27:
      Neons and LED lighting can be controlled by remotes.
  5. (countable) A neon tetra fish.
