Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin sardīna.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [sərˈdi.nə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [saɾˈði.na]
  • Audio (Valencian)(file)

Noun[edit]

sardina f (plural sardines)

  1. European pilchard
    Synonym: sarda

Further reading[edit]

  • “sardina” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.
  • “sardina”, in Gran Diccionari de la Llengua Catalana, Grup Enciclopèdia Catalana, 2024
  • “sardina” in Diccionari normatiu valencià, Acadèmia Valenciana de la Llengua.
  • “sardina” in Diccionari català-valencià-balear, Antoni Maria Alcover and Francesc de Borja Moll, 1962.

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin sardina.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /sarˈdi.na/
  • Rhymes: -ina
  • Hyphenation: sar‧dì‧na

Noun[edit]

sardina f (plural sardine)

  1. sardine (Sardina pilchardus)
    Synonyms: sarda, sardella
  2. (politics, neologism) a member of the Sardines movement

Descendants[edit]

  • → Tunisian Arabic: سردينة (sardīna, “sardine, Sardina pilchardus”)[1]

See also[edit]

  • sardo

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /sarˈdiː.na/, [s̠ärˈd̪iːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /sarˈdi.na/, [särˈd̪iːnä]
  • (Classical) IPA(key): /sarˈdiː.naː/, [s̠ärˈd̪iːnäː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /sarˈdi.na/, [särˈd̪iːnä]

Noun[edit]

sardīna f (genitive sardīnae); first declension

  1. Synonym of sarda (kind of fish, sardine or mackerel)

  1. inflection of sardīnus:

Adjective[edit]

sardīnā

  1. ablative feminine singular of sardīnus
