Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From nūtrīx (“wet nurse”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /nuːˈtriː.koː/, [nuːˈt̪riːkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /nuˈtri.ko/, [nuˈt̪riːko]

Verb[edit]

nūtrīcō (present infinitive nūtrīcāre, perfect active nūtrīcāvī, supine nūtrīcātum); first conjugation

  1. to suckle, nourish, rear
  2. to sustain, support
