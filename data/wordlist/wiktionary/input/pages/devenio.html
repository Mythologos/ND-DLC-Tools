Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dē- (“from”) +‎ veniō (“come”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deːˈu̯e.ni.oː/, [d̪eːˈu̯ɛnioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /deˈve.ni.o/, [d̪eˈvɛːnio]

Verb[edit]

dēveniō (present infinitive dēvenīre, perfect active dēvēnī, supine dēventum); fourth conjugation

  1. to come down, descend; come from or to; arrive at (from somewhere), land at, reach
    • 29 BCE – 19 BCE, Virgil, Aeneid 4.165–166:
      Spēluncam Dīdō dux et Troiānus eandem / dēveniunt [...].
      Dido and the Trojan captain arrive at the same cavern.
      (The “dux Troianus” is Aeneas.)
