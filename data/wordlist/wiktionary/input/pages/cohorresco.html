Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

co(n)- +‎ horrēscō (“to begin to shake”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ko.horˈreːs.koː/, [ko(ɦ)ɔrˈreːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ko.orˈres.ko/, [koːrˈrɛsko]

Verb[edit]

cohorrēscō (present infinitive cohorrēscere, perfect active cohorruī); third conjugation, no supine stem

  1. (intransitive) to (begin to) shudder, shiver with fear or cold
