Latin[edit]

FWOTD – 10 March 2015

Etymology[edit]

Probably a denominative in -ō from a lost adjective, Proto-Italic *aizoskos (“demanding”), or an s-derivative *aizos (“demand”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eys- (“to request, search”). Cognate with Old Armenian հայց (haycʿ).[1]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ae̯ˈrus.koː/, [äe̯ˈrʊs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /eˈrus.ko/, [eˈrusko]

Verb[edit]

aeruscō (present infinitive aeruscāre); first conjugation, no passive, no perfect or supine stem (archaic)

  1. to get money by going about and exhibiting tricks; play the juggler
  2. (by extension) to go begging
