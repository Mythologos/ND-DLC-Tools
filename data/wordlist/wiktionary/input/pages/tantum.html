Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Adverbial accusative of tantus. Compare with tam.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtan.tum/, [ˈt̪än̪t̪ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtan.tum/, [ˈt̪än̪t̪um]

Adverb[edit]

tantum (not comparable)

  1. so much, to such a degree (so greatly, so little)
    Synonyms: eō, adeō, tam, tantopere
  2. (when coupled with quantum) as much of […] as
    da mihi tantum aquae quantum vini ― give me as much of water as wine
  3. only, alone, merely, but
    Synonyms: modo, sōlum

Derived terms[edit]

  • plūrāle tantum
  • singulāre tantum
  • alterum tantum (“again as much”)
  • *eccum tantum

Adjective[edit]

tantum

  1. inflection of tantus:
    1. accusative masculine singular
    2. nominative/accusative/vocative neuter singular
