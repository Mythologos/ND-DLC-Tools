English[edit]

Chemical element
Es
Previous: californium (Cf)
Next: fermium (Fm)

Etymology[edit]

Einstein +‎ -ium. Named after Albert Einstein.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (US) IPA(key): /aɪnˈstaɪni.əm/, enPR: īnstīn'ēəm
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)

Noun[edit]

einsteinium (uncountable)

  1. A transuranic chemical element (symbol Es) with atomic number 99: a soft, silvery, paramagnetic metal.
    • 2021 February 3, Becky Ferreira, “Scientists Just Studied a Dangerous Element Discovered in a 50s Nuke Test”, in VICE‎[1]:
      Einsteinium, the 99th element of the periodic table, was discovered in the fallout of the first hydrogen bomb, detonated by the United States in the Marshall Islands on November 1, 1952.

Related terms[edit]

  • einsteinate
  • Einsteinian
