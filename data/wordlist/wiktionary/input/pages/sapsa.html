Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Finnic *sapsa.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈsɑpsɑ/, [ˈs̠ɑ̝ps̠ɑ̝]
  • Rhymes: -ɑpsɑ
  • Syllabification(key): sap‧sa

Noun[edit]

sapsa

  1. the axehead between the bit and the eye

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Contracted from sua (“his, her, its”) + ipsa (“own”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsap.sa/, [ˈs̠äps̠ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsap.sa/, [ˈsäpsä]

Pronoun[edit]

sapsa

  1. his own, her own, its own

Related terms[edit]

  • ipse
  • suus
