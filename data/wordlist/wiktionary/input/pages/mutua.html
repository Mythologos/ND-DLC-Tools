English[edit]

Noun[edit]

mutua

  1. plural of mutuum

Anagrams[edit]

  • autum

Asturian[edit]

Adjective[edit]

mutua

  1. feminine singular of mutuu

Finnish[edit]

Noun[edit]

mutua

  1. partitive singular of mutu

Anagrams[edit]

  • muuta, tuuma, uumat

Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmu.tu.a/, /ˈmu.twa/[1]
  • Rhymes: -utua, -utwa
  • Hyphenation: mù‧tu‧a, mù‧tua

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

mutua f (plural mutue)

  1. sickness benefit fund (or similar)

Adjective[edit]

mutua f sg

  1. feminine singular of mutuo

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

mutua

  1. inflection of mutuare:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

References[edit]

  1. ^ mutua in Luciano Canepari, Dizionario di Pronuncia Italiana (DiPI)

Latin[edit]

Adverb[edit]

mutua (not comparable)

  1. reciprocally, interchangeably, mutually

Adjective[edit]

mūtua

  1. inflection of mūtuus:
    1. nominative/ablative/vocative feminine singular
    2. nominative/accusative/vocative neuter plural

References[edit]

  • mutua in Charles du Fresne du Cange’s Glossarium Mediæ et Infimæ Latinitatis (augmented edition with additions by D. P. Carpenterius, Adelungius and others, edited by Léopold Favre, 1883–1887)
  • Dizionario Latino, Olivetti

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmutwa/ [ˈmu.t̪wa]
  • Rhymes: -utwa
  • Syllabification: mu‧tua

Adjective[edit]

mutua f sg

  1. feminine singular of mutuo
