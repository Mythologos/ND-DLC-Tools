Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ūnus (“one”) +‎ cornū (“horn”) +‎ -us.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /uː.niˈkor.nu.us/, [uːnɪˈkɔrnuʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /u.niˈkor.nu.us/, [uniˈkɔrnuːs]

Noun[edit]

ūnicornuus m (genitive ūnicornuī); second declension

  1. the unicorn.
    Synonyms: monoceros, ūnicornis

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.
