Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Biblical Hebrew יִתְרוֹ (yitró).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈi̯e.tʰroː/, [ˈi̯ɛt̪ʰroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈje.tro/, [ˈjɛːt̪ro]

Proper noun[edit]

Iethrō m

  1. Jethro (father-in-law of Moses in the Hebrew Bible)

Old English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin Iethrō, from Biblical Hebrew יִתְרוֹ (yitró).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈje.troː/

Proper noun[edit]

Iethrō m

  1. Jethro (father-in-law of Moses in the Hebrew Bible)
