English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Italian intrico (“tangle”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Received Pronunciation) enPR: ĭntrēʹkō, IPA(key): /ɪnˈtɹiːkəʊ/

Noun[edit]

intrico (plural intrichi or intricoes)

  1. an intricacy; a web of intrigue

References[edit]

  • “‖inˈtrico” listed in the Oxford English Dictionary [2nd Ed.; 1989]

Anagrams[edit]

  • noritic, trionic

Galician[edit]

Verb[edit]

intrico

  1. first-person singular present indicative of intricar

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Rhymes: -iko
  • Hyphenation: in‧trì‧co

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

intrico m (plural intrichi)

  1. tangle

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

intrico

  1. first-person singular present indicative of intricare

Anagrams[edit]

  • citrino, nitrico, notrici, ricinto, riconti, rincoti, trincio, trinciò

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ trīcor (“dally, trifle”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈtriː.koː/, [ɪn̪ˈt̪riːkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈtri.ko/, [in̪ˈt̪riːko]

Verb[edit]

intrīcō (present infinitive intrīcāre, perfect active intrīcāvī, supine intrīcātum); first conjugation

  1. to entangle, perplex, embarrass
