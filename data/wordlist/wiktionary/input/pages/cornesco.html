Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From cornū (“horn”) +‎ -ēscō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /korˈneːs.koː/, [kɔrˈneːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /korˈnes.ko/, [korˈnɛsko]

Verb[edit]

cornēscō (present infinitive cornēscere); third conjugation, no passive, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to become like horn; to turn to horn
