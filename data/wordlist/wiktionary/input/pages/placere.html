Danish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Via German platzieren from French placer (“to place”), derived from the noun place.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [pʰl̥aˈseˀɐ]
  • Rhymes: -eːˀər

Verb[edit]

placere (imperative placer, infinitive at placere, present tense placerer, past tense placerede, perfect tense har placeret)

  1. place
  2. invest
  3. site

Synonyms[edit]

  • anbringe
  • sætte

Further reading[edit]

  • “placere” in Den Danske Ordbog

Latin[edit]

Verb[edit]

plācēre

  1. second-person singular present passive subjunctive of plācō

Verb[edit]

placēre

  1. present active infinitive of placeō

Noun[edit]

placēre n (indeclinable)

  1. (Medieval Latin) pleasure
