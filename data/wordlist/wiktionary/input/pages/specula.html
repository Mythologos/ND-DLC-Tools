English[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Rhymes: -ɛkjʊlə

Noun[edit]

specula

  1. plural of speculum

Anagrams[edit]

  • capsule, laces up, scale up, scaleup, upscale

Italian[edit]

Verb[edit]

specula

  1. inflection of speculare:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Anagrams[edit]

  • capsule, lupesca, scapule

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From speciō. Cf. scopulus, through Ancient Greek, from the same root.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈspe.ku.la/, [ˈs̠pɛkʊɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈspe.ku.la/, [ˈspɛːkulä]

Noun[edit]

specula f (genitive speculae); first declension

  1. watchtower, lookout

perfect passive participle of speculor

Etymology 2[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈspe.ku.la/, [ˈs̠pɛkʊɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈspe.ku.la/, [ˈspɛːkulä]

Noun[edit]

specula

  1. nominative/accusative/vocative plural of speculum

Etymology 3[edit]

Diminutive form of spēs: spēs +‎ -culus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈspeː.ku.la/, [ˈs̠peːkʊɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈspe.ku.la/, [ˈspɛːkulä]

Noun[edit]

spēcula f (genitive spēculae); first declension

  1. slight hope
