Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ex- (“out of”) +‎ misceō (“mix”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /eːˈmis.ke.oː/, [eːˈmɪs̠keoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /eˈmiʃ.ʃe.o/, [eˈmiʃːeo]

Verb[edit]

ēmisceō (present infinitive ēmiscēre, perfect active ēmiscuī, supine ēmixtum); second conjugation

  1. to mix or mingle (by pouring out)

Conjugation[edit]

  • The fourth principal part may be ēmixtum or ēmistum.
