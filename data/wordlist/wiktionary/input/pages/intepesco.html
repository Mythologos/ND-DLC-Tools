Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ tepēscō or intepeō +‎ -scō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.teˈpeːs.koː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛˈpeːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.teˈpes.ko/, [in̪t̪eˈpɛsko]

Verb[edit]

intepēscō (present infinitive intepēscere); third conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. (intransitive) to become lukewarm, be warmed
  2. (intransitive) to become weaker, milder
