Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

circum- +‎ farciō (“cram, stuff”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈfar.ki.oː/, [kɪrkũːˈfärkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈfar.t͡ʃi.o/, [t͡ʃirkumˈfärt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

circumfarciō (present infinitive circumfarcīre, perfect active circumfarsī, supine circumfartum); fourth conjugation

  1. to fill up all around (with), pack all around (with), stuff (with)
