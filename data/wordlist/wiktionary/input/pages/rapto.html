Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From raptus +‎ -ō, from rapiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈrap.toː/, [ˈräpt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈrap.to/, [ˈräpt̪o]

Verb[edit]

raptō (present infinitive raptāre, perfect active raptāvī, supine raptātum); first conjugation

  1. to seize and carry off, abduct
  2. to drag along
  3. to ravage, plunder

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

  1. abduction, kidnap
