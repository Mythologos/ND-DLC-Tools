Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

tele- +‎ visio; adapted form of French télévision.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈteleʋisio/, [ˈt̪e̞le̞ˌʋis̠io̞]
  • Rhymes: -isio
  • Syllabification(key): te‧le‧vi‧si‧o

Noun[edit]

televisio

  1. (television) television
  2. television set

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /teː.leˈu̯iː.si.oː/, [t̪eːɫ̪eˈu̯iːs̠ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /te.leˈvi.si.o/, [t̪eleˈviːs̬io]

Noun[edit]

tēlevīsiō f (genitive tēlevīsiōnis); third declension

  1. (Contemporary Latin) television (medium)
  2. (Contemporary Latin) television (device)
    • 1957, Pope Pius XII, Miranda Prorsus [2], Vatican (Litterae Encyclicae):
      Televisioni plura sunt; cum cinematographica arte communia, quandoquidem eius spectationes ipsam vitae actionem agitationemque oculis exhibent.
      Many have a television, since its shows provide to the eyes, with ordinary cinematographic art, the very action and activity of life.
