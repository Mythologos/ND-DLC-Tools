Catalan[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic, Valencian) [ˈkɔ.ʎis]

Verb[edit]

collis

  1. second-person singular present subjunctive of collar

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *kolnis, from Proto-Indo-European *kl̥Hnís (“hill”), from the root *kelH-. Cognate with Proto-Germanic *hulliz (English hill).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkol.lis/, [ˈkɔlːʲɪs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkol.lis/, [ˈkɔlːis]

Noun[edit]

collis m (genitive collis); third declension

  1. a hill
    • 29 BCE – 19 BCE, Virgil, Aeneid 1.419–420:
      Iamque ascendēbant collem, quī plūrimus urbī
      imminet, adversāsque aspectat dēsuper arcēs.
      And now they were climbing the hill, which menaces high above the city, and looks down upon the opposite defenses.
