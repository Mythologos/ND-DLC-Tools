Catalan[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [ˈsar.də]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [ˈsaɾ.ða]

Etymology 1[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Adjective[edit]

sarda f sg

  1. feminine singular of sard

Noun[edit]

sarda f (plural sardes)

  1. female equivalent of sard

Etymology 2[edit]

Borrowed from Latin sarda.

Noun[edit]

sarda f (plural sardes)

  1. European pilchard (Sardina pilchardus)
    Synonym: sardina

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Seemingly ultimately from Ancient Greek Σαρδώ (Sardṓ, “Sardinia”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • sarda: (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsar.da/, [ˈs̠ärd̪ä]
  • sarda: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsar.da/, [ˈsärd̪ä]

Noun[edit]

sarda f (genitive sardae); first declension

  1. A kind of fish, perhaps sardine or mackerel.
    Synonym: sardīna
  2. A kind of stone, probably carnelian.
    Synonym: sardius

Etymology 2[edit]

From sardus (“Sardinian”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • sarda: (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsar.da/, [ˈs̠ärd̪ä]
  • sarda: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsar.da/, [ˈsärd̪ä]
  • sardā: (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsar.daː/, [ˈs̠ärd̪äː]
  • sardā: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsar.da/, [ˈsärd̪ä]

Adjective[edit]

sarda

  1. inflection of sardus:
    1. nominative/vocative feminine singular
    2. nominative/accusative/vocative neuter plural

Adjective[edit]

sardā

  1. ablative feminine singular of sardus
