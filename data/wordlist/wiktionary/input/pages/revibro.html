Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From re- +‎ vibrō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈre.u̯i.broː/, [ˈreu̯ɪbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈre.vi.bro/, [ˈrɛːvibro]

Verb[edit]

revibrō (present infinitive revibrāre, perfect active revibrāvī, supine revibrātum); first conjugation

  1. to cast back, reflect
    • Martianus Capella, De Nuptiis 2.169
  2. to send back a reflection
    • Martianus Capella, De Nuptiis 8.810
