Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French formique.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfɔr.mi.ko/
  • Rhymes: -ɔrmiko
  • Hyphenation: fòr‧mi‧co

Adjective[edit]

formico (feminine formica, masculine plural formici, feminine plural formiche)

  1. (organic chemistry) formic

Derived terms[edit]

  • acido formico

Related terms[edit]

  • formica

Anagrams[edit]

  • cofirmo, cofirmò

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From formīca (“ant”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /forˈmiː.koː/, [fɔrˈmiːkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /forˈmi.ko/, [forˈmiːko]

Verb[edit]

formīcō (present infinitive formīcāre); first conjugation, no passive, no perfect or supine stem

  1. (intransitive) to crawl like an ant
  2. (intransitive) to feel as if ants are crawling on one's skin, experience formication
