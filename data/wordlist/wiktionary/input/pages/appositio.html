English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin.

Noun[edit]

appositio (uncountable)

  1. (rhetoric) Addition of an element not syntactically required for purpose of description or explanation.

See also[edit]

  • parenthetical
  • parenthesis

Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Internationalism (see English apposition), ultimately from Latin appositiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɑpːositio/, [ˈɑ̝pːo̞ˌs̠it̪io̞]
  • Rhymes: -itio
  • Syllabification(key): ap‧po‧si‧ti‧o

Noun[edit]

appositio

  1. (grammar) apposition
  2. (rhetoric) appositio

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From appōnō +‎ -tiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ap.poˈsi.ti.oː/, [äpːɔˈs̠ɪt̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ap.poˈsit.t͡si.o/, [äpːoˈs̬it̪ː͡s̪io]

Noun[edit]

appositiō f (genitive appositiōnis); third declension

  1. apposition

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.
