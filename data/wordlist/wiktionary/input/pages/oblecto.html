Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ob- (“to, because of”) +‎ lactō (“flatter, please”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /obˈlek.toː/, [ɔbˈɫ̪ɛkt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /obˈlek.to/, [obˈlɛkt̪o]

Verb[edit]

oblectō (present infinitive oblectāre, perfect active oblectāvī, supine oblectātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to entertain, delight, amuse [+accusative (person); ablative, cum + ablative, or in + ablative (means of amusement)]
  2. (transitive) to pass (the time) agreeably
  3. (transitive) to delay, detain

Usage notes[edit]

The passive of this verb means "to have fun".
