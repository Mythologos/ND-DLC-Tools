Catalan[edit]

Catalan Wikipedia has an article on:
Tolosa
Wikipedia ca

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Latin Tolōsa, probably of Gaulish origin.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central) [tuˈlo.zə]
  • IPA(key): (Balearic) [toˈlo.zə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [toˈlo.za]
  • Rhymes: -ozə, -oza
  • Hyphenation: To‧lo‧sa

Proper noun[edit]

Tolosa f

  1. Toulouse (the capital city of the Haute-Garonne department, France; capital city of the region of Occitanie)

Derived terms[edit]

  • tolosà

Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
Tolosa
Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

Probably of Aquitanian, Iberian, or Gaulish origin; compare Ancient Greek Τολῶσσα (Tolôssa).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /toˈloː.sa/, [t̪ɔˈɫ̪oːs̠ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /toˈlo.sa/, [t̪oˈlɔːs̬ä]

Proper noun[edit]

Tolōsa f sg (genitive Tolōsae); first declension

  1. Toulouse (the capital city of the Haute-Garonne department, France; capital city of the region of Occitanie)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, with locative, singular only.

Case Singular
Nominative Tolōsa
Genitive Tolōsae
Dative Tolōsae
Accusative Tolōsam
Ablative Tolōsā
Vocative Tolōsa
Locative Tolōsae

Derived terms[edit]
