Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • refarciō

Etymology[edit]

re- +‎ farciō (“cram, stuff”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reˈfer.ki.oː/, [rɛˈfɛrkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈfer.t͡ʃi.o/, [reˈfɛrt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

referciō (present infinitive refercīre, perfect active refersī, supine refertum); fourth conjugation

  1. to fill up, cram full
  2. to pack close together
