Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From vacca (“cow”) +‎ -āricius. Attested in the Pactus Alamannorum.[1]

Adjective[edit]

vaccāricius (feminine vaccāricia, neuter vaccāricium); first/second-declension adjective (Early Medieval Latin)

  1. of or pertaining to a cow.
