Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Seemingly built from a noun like *klāmo- or *klāmā- (“shout”)[1] + -ō (verb-forming suffix), the former derived from Proto-Indo-European *kelh₁- (“shout”, verb) and possibly surviving as the first element of the adjective clāmōsus as well.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈklaː.moː/, [ˈkɫ̪äːmoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkla.mo/, [ˈkläːmo]

Verb[edit]

clāmō (present infinitive clāmāre, perfect active clāmāvī, supine clāmātum); first conjugation

  1. to cry out, clamor, shout, yell, exclaim
    Synonyms: conclāmō, vōcificō, vōciferor, clāmitō, tonō, succlāmō, acclāmō, exclāmō, inclāmō
  2. (Medieval Latin) to call, to call to
  3. (Medieval Latin) to address as, call by name
