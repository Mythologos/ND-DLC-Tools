Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Uncertain;[1] proposed derivations include:

  • From a root common to Ancient Greek ἐρευνάω (ereunáō, “to seek, to search for”) and Proto-Germanic *rūnō (“secret; rune”).
  • From Proto-Indo-European *Hrew- (“to tear out, dig out”). Cognates include Latin ruō (“I dig out”) and Russian рыть (rytʹ, “to dig”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈrus.por/, [ˈrʊs̠pɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈrus.por/, [ˈruspor]

Verb[edit]

ruspor (present infinitive ruspārī, perfect active ruspātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. to examine, explore
