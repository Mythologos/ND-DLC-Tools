English[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • attester

Etymology[edit]

attest +‎ -or

Noun[edit]

attestor (plural attestors)

  1. One who attests.

Translations[edit]

one who attests
  • German: Beglaubiger m, Beglaubigerin f
  • Spanish: fedatario m

Anagrams[edit]

  • testator

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • adtestor

Etymology[edit]

From ad- +‎ testor (“bear witness; prove”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /atˈtes.tor/, [ät̪ˈt̪ɛs̠t̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /atˈtes.tor/, [ät̪ˈt̪ɛst̪or]

Verb[edit]

attestor (present infinitive attestārī, perfect active attestātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. to bear witness to; attest, prove, confirm, corroborate
