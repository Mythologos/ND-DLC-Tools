Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin hymnus, from Ancient Greek ὕμνος (húmnos).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈin.no/
  • Rhymes: -inno
  • Hyphenation: ìn‧no

Noun[edit]

inno m (plural inni)

  1. hymn, song, anthem

Related terms[edit]

  • innario
  • inneggiare
  • inno nazionale

Further reading[edit]

  • inno in Collins Italian-English Dictionary
  • inno in Treccani.it – Vocabolario Treccani on line, Istituto dell'Enciclopedia Italiana

Anagrams[edit]

  • nino, noni, onni, onni-

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈin.noː/, [ˈɪnːoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈin.no/, [ˈinːo]

Verb[edit]

innō (present infinitive innāre, perfect active innāvī, supine innātum); first conjugation

  1. to swim in, float upon
