Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /deˈkɔl.lo/
  • Rhymes: -ɔllo
  • Hyphenation: de‧còl‧lo

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

decollo m (plural decolli)

  1. takeoff (of an aircraft etc.)

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

decollo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of decollare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dē- +‎ collum +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deːˈkol.loː/, [d̪eːˈkɔlːʲoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /deˈkol.lo/, [d̪eˈkɔlːo]

Verb[edit]

dēcollō (present infinitive dēcollāre, perfect active dēcollāvī, supine dēcollātum); first conjugation

  1. to decapitate or behead
