Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • (rare) brevisētis

Etymology[edit]

New Latin; from brevis (“short”) +‎ sēta (“hair”), changed to the first and second declension.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /bre.u̯iˈseː.tus/, [breu̯ɪˈs̠eːt̪ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /bre.viˈse.tus/, [breviˈs̬ɛːt̪us]

Adjective[edit]

brevisētus (feminine brevisēta, neuter brevisētum); first/second-declension adjective

  1. (New Latin) having short hair
    brevisētus: Phellinus brevisetus, Entodon brevisetus, Coccophagus brevisetus, Aculus brevisetus
    brevisēta: Bouteloua breviseta, Microseris breviseta, Danthonia breviseta, Ortachne breviseta
    brevisētum: Petalium brevisetum, Chondrosum brevisetum, Racomitrium brevisetum

Usage notes[edit]

  • Used almost exclusively as a taxonomic epithet and thus not normally in inflected forms other than the nominative singular.
