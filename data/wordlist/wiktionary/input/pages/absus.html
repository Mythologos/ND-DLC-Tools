Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈab.sus/, [ˈäps̠ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈab.sus/, [ˈäbsus]

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

absus m (genitive absī); second declension

  1. Alternative form of hapsus
Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural

Ablative absō absīs
Vocative abse absī

Etymology 2[edit]

From absēns (“absent”).

Adjective[edit]

absus (feminine absa, neuter absum); first/second-declension adjective (Medieval Latin)

  1. (of land) uncultivated, unoccupied
  2. (of a person) possessing no tenancy
