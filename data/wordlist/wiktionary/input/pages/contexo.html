Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ texō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈtek.soː/, [kɔn̪ˈt̪ɛks̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈtek.so/, [kon̪ˈt̪ɛkso]

Verb[edit]

contexō (present infinitive contexere, perfect active contexuī, supine contextum); third conjugation

  1. to weave, entwine or braid, knit
  2. to interweave, unite or connect
  3. to construct
