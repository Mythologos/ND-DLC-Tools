Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • supterdūcō

Etymology[edit]

From subter +‎ dūcō (“lead; draw”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /sub.terˈduː.koː/, [s̠ʊpt̪ɛrˈd̪uːkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /sub.terˈdu.ko/, [subt̪erˈd̪uːko]

Verb[edit]

subterdūcō (present infinitive subterdūcere, perfect active subterdūxī, supine subterductum); third conjugation, irregular short imperative

  1. (usually with reflexive) to take away or carry off secretly, steal
