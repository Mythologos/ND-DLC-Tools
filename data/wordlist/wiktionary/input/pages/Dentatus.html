Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dēns (“tooth”) +‎ -ātus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /denˈtaː.tus/, [d̪ɛn̪ˈt̪äːt̪ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /denˈta.tus/, [d̪en̪ˈt̪äːt̪us]

Adjective[edit]

dentātus (feminine dentāta, neuter dentātum); first/second-declension adjective

  1. toothed, having teeth
    Antonym: ēdentulus
