Catalan[edit]

Verb[edit]

solido

  1. first-person singular present indicative of solidar

Esperanto[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [soˈlido]
  • Rhymes: -ido
  • Hyphenation: so‧li‧do

Noun[edit]

solido (accusative singular solidon, plural solidoj, accusative plural solidojn)

  1. solid

Derived terms[edit]

  • solida (“solid”, adjective)

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin solidus. Doublet of sodo and soldo.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈsɔ.li.do/
  • Rhymes: -ɔlido
  • Hyphenation: sò‧li‧do

Adjective[edit]

solido (feminine solida, masculine plural solidi, feminine plural solide, superlative solidissimo)

  1. solid (not fluid)
    Antonym: liquido
  2. solid, strong, firm, sound
  3. reliable, solid
  4. (geometry) solid

Noun[edit]

solido m (plural solidi)

  1. (geometry) solid

Derived terms[edit]

  • in solido
  • solidamente
  • solidificare
  • solidità
 

Anagrams[edit]

  • dolosi

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From solidus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈso.li.doː/, [ˈs̠ɔlʲɪd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈso.li.do/, [ˈsɔːlid̪o]

Verb[edit]

solidō (present infinitive solidāre, perfect active solidāvī, supine solidātum); first conjugation

  1. to make, or become solid, firm, etc.
  2. to strengthen or consolidate
  3. to fasten together
