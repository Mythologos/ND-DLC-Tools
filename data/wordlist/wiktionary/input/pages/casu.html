Asturian[edit]

Asturian Wikipedia has an article on:
casu
Wikipedia ast

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin cāsus.

Noun[edit]

casu m (plural casos)

  1. case

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkaː.suː/, [ˈkäːs̠uː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈka.su/, [ˈkäːs̬u]

Noun[edit]

cāsū m

  1. ablative singular of cāsus (“a fall; falling down; accident, occurrence, chance event; end, termination; case”)

Adverb[edit]

cāsū (not comparable)

  1. accidentally, by accident, by chance

References[edit]

  • “casu”, in Charlton T. Lewis (1891) An Elementary Latin Dictionary, New York: Harper & Brothers

  • IPA(key): [ˈt͡sasu]

casu

Sardinian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈkasu/, [ˈkäː.zu]

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin cāseus, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kwh₂et- (“to ferment”). Compare Romanian caș, Spanish queso, Portuguese queijo.

Noun[edit]

casu m (plural casos)

  1. cheese

Etymology 2[edit]

From Latin cāsus.

Noun[edit]

casu m (plural casos)

  1. case

Sicilian[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin cāsus, likely borrowed via Italian caso.

Noun[edit]

casu m (plural casi)

  1. coincidence, accident (event that happens by chance)
    Synonym: coincidenza
    È sulamenti un casu.
    It's just a coincidence.
  2. chance, fate
    Synonyms: fatu, sorti
    M'affidu ô casu.
    I trust to chance.
  3. case, circumstance, matter
    u casu di Sciacca ― the case of Sciacca
  4. (grammar) case

References[edit]

  • Traina, Antonino (1868), “casu”, in Nuovo vocabolario Siciliano-Italiano [New Sicilian-Italian vocabulary] (in Italian), Liber Liber, published 2020, pages 760–761

Etymology 2[edit]

Inherited from Latin cāseus. Doublet of caciu, probably borrowed from Italian cacio.

Noun[edit]

casu m (plural casi)

  1. (Calabria, Salento, countable and uncountable) cheese
    Synonyms: tumazzu, caciu, furmaggiu
