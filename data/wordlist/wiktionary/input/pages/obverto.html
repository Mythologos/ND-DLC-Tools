Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ob- +‎ vertō.

Verb[edit]

obvertō (present infinitive obvertere, perfect active obvertī, supine obversum); third conjugation

  1. to turn to/towards
    • 29 BCE – 19 BCE, Virgil, Aeneid 6.3:
      Obvertunt pelago proras
      They turn their prows to the sea
