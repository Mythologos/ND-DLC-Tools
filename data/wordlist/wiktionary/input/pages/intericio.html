Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • interiaciō, interiiciō

Etymology[edit]

From inter- (“between, among”) +‎ iaciō (“throw, hurl”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.teˈri.ki.oː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛˈrɪkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.teˈri.t͡ʃi.o/, [in̪t̪eˈriːt͡ʃio]
  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈi̯i.ki.oː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈi̯ɪkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈji.t͡ʃi.o/, [in̪t̪erˈjiːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

intericiō (present infinitive intericere, perfect active interiēcī, supine interiectum); third conjugation iō-variant

  1. to throw, cast or hurl between
  2. to place, put or set between
  3. to add, join (to)
