Lucanus m

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Lūca (“a city in Etruria”) +‎ -ānus

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /luːˈkaː.nus/, [ɫ̪uːˈkäːnʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /luˈka.nus/, [luˈkäːnus]

Proper noun[edit]

Lūcānus m (genitive Lūcānī); second declension

  1. A resident of Lucania
  2. The name of a Roman gēns.
  3. The Roman poet Lucan.
