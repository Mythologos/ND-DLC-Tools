Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From metus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈme.tu.oː/, [ˈmɛt̪uoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈme.tu.o/, [ˈmɛːt̪uo]

Verb[edit]

metuō (present infinitive metuere, perfect active metuī, supine metūtum); third conjugation

  1. to fear, be afraid
    Synonyms: vereor, extimēscō, timeō, trepidō
    • 27 BCE – 25 BCE, Titus Livius, Ab Urbe Condita I.9:
      tantam ... crescentem molem sibi ac posteris suis metuebant
      they feared, both for themselves and their descendants, such growing power

Usage notes[edit]

  • The verb metuō is a Latin verb of fearing.
