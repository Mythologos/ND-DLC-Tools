English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin Ivernia.

Proper noun[edit]

Ivernia

  1. (historical, archaic) Synonym of Hibernia: Ireland (an island in Western Europe).

Related terms[edit]

  • Iverni
  • Ivernic

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Ἰ̄ουερνία (Īouernía), from Primitive Irish [script needed] (*īweriū) (compare Old Irish Ériu), from Proto-Celtic *Φīweryū.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /iːˈu̯er.ni.a/, [iːˈu̯ɛrniä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /iˈver.ni.a/, [iˈvɛrniä]

Proper noun[edit]

Īvernia f sg (genitive Īverniae); first declension

  1. (historical, obsolete) Synonym of Hibernia: Ireland (an island in Western Europe).
    • 1511, Bernardus Sylvanus ed., Jacobus Angelus's translation of Ptolemy as Liber Geographica, Bk VIII:
      In prima Tabula[:] Iuernia insula & Albion insula Brictannicae
      (please add an English translation of this quotation)

First-declension noun, singular only.
