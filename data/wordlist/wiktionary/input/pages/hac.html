Bouyei[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Tai *haːꟲ (“five”), from Old Chinese 五 (OC *ŋaːʔ, “five”). Cognate with Thai ห้า (hâa), Northern Thai ᩉ᩶ᩣ, Lao ຫ້າ (hā), Lü ᦠᦱᧉ (ḣaa²), Tai Dam ꪬ꫁ꪱ, Shan ႁႃႈ (hāa), Tai Nüa ᥞᥣᥲ (hàa), Ahom 𑜑𑜡 (hā), Zhuang haj.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /xa˥˧/

Numeral[edit]

Bouyei cardinal numbers
 <  4 5 6  > 
    Cardinal : hac

hac

  1. five

Catalan[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic, Valencian) [ˈak]

Noun[edit]

hac f (plural hacs)

  1. The name of the Latin-script letter H.

Further reading[edit]

  • “hac” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

hac

  1. first-person singular present indicative of haver
  2. first-person singular present indicative of heure

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Feminine ablative singular of hic (“this”).

Adverb[edit]

hāc (not comparable)

  1. this way
  2. here
  3. so, thus, thusly

Derived terms[edit]

  • *ecce hāc
  • *eccum hāc

Turkish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Ottoman Turkish حج (hac),[1][2] from Arabic حَجّ (ḥajj, “pilgrimage”), from حَجَّ (ḥajja, “to overcome”).[3]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈhɑd͡ʒ/
  • Audio(file)
  • Hyphenation: hac

Noun[edit]

hac (definite accusative haccı, plural haclar)

  1. (religion) The act of visiting a sacred place during certain times of the year, generally in monotheistic religions; pilgrimage.
  2. (Islam) The pilgrimage to Mecca by Muslims; the Hajj ritual.
