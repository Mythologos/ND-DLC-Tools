Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
orgasmus
Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek ὀργασμός (orgasmós).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /orˈɡas.mus/, [ɔrˈɡäs̠mʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /orˈɡas.mus/, [orˈɡäzmus]

Noun[edit]

orgasmus m (genitive orgasmī); second declension

  1. (New Latin) an orgasm
    Synonym: patrātiō

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Blend of erasmus +‎ orgasmo. Erasmus students have a reputation of being very sexually active.

Noun[edit]

orgasmus m (plural orgasmus)

  1. (humorous) a study period on the Erasmus programme
