Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek κύλινδρος (kúlindros).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kyˈlin.drus/, [kʏˈlʲɪn̪d̪rʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃiˈlin.drus/, [t͡ʃiˈlin̪d̪rus]

Noun[edit]

cylindrus m (genitive cylindrī); second declension

  1. cylinder
  2. (by extension) a roller; a cylindrical stone for levelling the ground

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.
