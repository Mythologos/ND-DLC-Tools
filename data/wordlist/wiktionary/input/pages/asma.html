Afrikaans[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Dutch astma, from Latin āsthma, from Ancient Greek ἆσθμα (âsthma).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Noun[edit]

asma (uncountable)

  1. asthma

Azerbaijani[edit]

Verb[edit]

asmá

  1. verbal noun of asmaq (“to hang”)

Adjective[edit]

asmá (not comparable)

  1. hanging, suspension
    asma körpü ― suspension bridge

Verb[edit]

ásma

  1. negative second-person singular imperative of asmaq (“to hang”)

Indonesian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈasma/
  • Hyphenation: as‧ma

Etymology 1[edit]

From Dutch astma.

Noun[edit]

asma (first-person possessive asmaku, second-person possessive asmamu, third-person possessive asmanya)

  1. (pathology) asthma

Adjective[edit]

asma

  1. (colloquial) chest tightness
Synonyms[edit]
  • bengek

Etymology 2[edit]

From Arabic أَسْمَى (ʔasmā).

Noun[edit]

asma (first-person possessive asmaku, second-person possessive asmamu, third-person possessive asmanya)

  1. (honorific) name.
    Synonym: nama

Spanish Wikipedia has an article on:

Wikipedia es

Etymology[edit]

Semi-learned borrowing from Latin āsthma, from Ancient Greek ἆσθμα (âsthma, “short-drawn breath, panting”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈasma/ [ˈaz.ma]
  • Audio (Colombia):(file)
  • Rhymes: -asma
  • Syllabification: as‧ma

Noun[edit]

asma f (plural asmas)

  1. (pathology) asthma

Usage notes[edit]

  • Feminine nouns beginning with stressed /ˈa/ like this one regularly take the singular articles el and un, usually reserved for masculine nouns.
    el asma, un asma
  • They maintain the usual feminine singular articles la and una if an adjective intervenes between the article and the noun.

Related terms[edit]

  • asmático
