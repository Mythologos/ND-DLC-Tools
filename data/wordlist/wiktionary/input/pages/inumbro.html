Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ umbrō (“to shade, overshadow”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /iˈnum.broː/, [ɪˈnʊmbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /iˈnum.bro/, [iˈnumbro]

Verb[edit]

inumbrō (present infinitive inumbrāre, perfect active inumbrāvī, supine inumbrātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to cast a shadow upon, shade, darken
  2. (transitive) to cover, obscure
