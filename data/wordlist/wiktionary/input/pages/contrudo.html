Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ trūdō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈtruː.doː/, [kɔn̪ˈt̪ruːd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈtru.do/, [kon̪ˈt̪ruːd̪o]

Verb[edit]

contrūdō (present infinitive contrūdere, perfect active contrūsī, supine contrūsum); third conjugation

  1. to thrust or crowd together; to cram
