Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Internationalism (see English dysentery), ultimately from Latin dysenteria.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈdysenteriɑ/, [ˈdys̠e̞n̪ˌt̪e̞riɑ̝]
  • Rhymes: -eriɑ
  • Syllabification(key): dy‧sen‧te‧ri‧a

Noun[edit]

dysenteria

  1. (pathology) dysentery

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek δυσεντερία (dusentería), from δυσ- (dus-, “bad”) + ἔντερα (éntera, “bowels”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /dy.senˈte.ri.a/, [d̪ʏs̠ɛn̪ˈt̪ɛriä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /di.senˈte.ri.a/, [d̪is̬en̪ˈt̪ɛːriä]

Noun[edit]

dysenteria f (genitive dysenteriae); first declension

  1. dysentery

Declension[edit]
