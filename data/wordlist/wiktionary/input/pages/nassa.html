Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin nassa.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈnas.sa/
  • Rhymes: -assa
  • Hyphenation: nàs‧sa

Noun[edit]

nassa f (plural nasse)

  1. creel, trap for fish

Anagrams[edit]

  • Sansa, sansa

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • naxa

Etymology[edit]

Probably from Old Latin nasta, from Proto-Indo-European *ned- (“to turn, twist, knot”). See also Proto-Germanic *natją (English net) and Irish nasc and snaidhm.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈnas.sa/, [ˈnäs̠ːä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈnas.sa/, [ˈnäsːä]

Noun[edit]

nassa f (genitive nassae); first declension

  1. a narrow-necked basket for catching fish, weel
  2. (figuratively) a snare, net

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Etymology[edit]

Noun[edit]

nassa f (plural nassi or nases)

  1. fish-trap
  2. trap in general
