Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • inmulgeō

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ mulgeō (“milk, extract”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /imˈmul.ɡe.oː/, [ɪmˈmʊɫ̪ɡeoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /imˈmul.d͡ʒe.o/, [imˈmul̠ʲd͡ʒeo]

Verb[edit]

immulgeō (present infinitive immulgēre, perfect active immulsī, supine immulsum); second conjugation, limited passive

  1. (transitive) to milk into
