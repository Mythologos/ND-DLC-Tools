Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • adnūtriō

Etymology[edit]

an- (“to”, “toward”) +‎ nūtriō (“I suckle”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /anˈnuː.tri.oː/, [änˈnuːt̪rioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /anˈnu.tri.o/, [änˈnuːt̪rio]

Verb[edit]

annūtriō (present infinitive annūtrīre); fourth conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. (transitive) to nourish or to train up at, near to, or on
    (Can we find and add a quotation of Pliny the Elder to this entry?)
