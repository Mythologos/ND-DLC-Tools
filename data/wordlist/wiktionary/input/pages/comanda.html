Catalan[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central) [kuˈman.də]
  • IPA(key): (Balearic) [koˈman.də]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [koˈman.da]

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

comanda f (plural comandes)

  1. order (a request for some product or service)

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

comanda

  1. inflection of comandar:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From commendo (“I entrust, commend”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /koˈman.da/, [kɔˈmän̪d̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /koˈman.da/, [koˈmän̪d̪ä]

Noun[edit]

comanda f (genitive comandae); first declension

  1. (Medieval Latin) a partnership of labor and capital

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Romanian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French commander. Cf. also the inherited doublet comânda.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Verb[edit]

a comanda (third-person singular present comandă, past participle comandat) 1st conj.

  1. to order, command, lead
    Synonyms: ordona, porunci, conduce
  2. to order
    Am comandat pizza.
    I ordered pizza
