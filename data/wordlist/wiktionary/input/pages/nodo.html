Esperanto[edit]

Esperanto Wikipedia has an article on:
nodo
Wikipedia eo

Etymology[edit]

From Latin nōdus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈnodo]
  • Rhymes: -odo
  • Hyphenation: no‧do

Noun[edit]

nodo (accusative singular nodon, plural nodoj, accusative plural nodojn)

  1. knot (looping of a piece of string or any other long, flexible material)
  2. knot (whorl in wood)
  3. (astronomy, mathematics) node

nodo (plural nodi)

Italian[edit]

Italian Wikipedia has an article on:
nodo
Wikipedia it

Etymology[edit]

From Latin nōdus, from Proto-Indo-European *gned-, *gnod- (“to bind”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈnɔ.do/
  • Rhymes: -ɔdo
  • Hyphenation: nò‧do
  • Audio(file)

Noun[edit]

nodo m (plural nodi)

  1. knot, gnarl
  2. bond, tie
  3. node, vertex
  4. crux
  5. road junction
  6. (unit of measure) knot
  7. lump
  8. bend

Derived terms[edit]

  • annodare
  • linfonodo
  • nodoso
  • nodino (e.g. a knot of mozzarella or a chop of beef)
  • nodo linfatico
  • snodare

Anagrams[edit]

  • dono, donò, ondo, ondò

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From nōdus (“knot”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈnoː.doː/, [ˈnoːd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈno.do/, [ˈnɔːd̪o]

Noun[edit]

nōdō m

  1. dative/ablative singular of nōdus

Verb[edit]

nōdō (present infinitive nōdāre, perfect active nōdāvī, supine nōdātum); first conjugation

  1. to knot, make knotted, tie in a knot
