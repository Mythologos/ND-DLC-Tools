Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

imprūdēns +‎ -ia

Noun[edit]

imprūdentia f (genitive imprūdentiae); first declension

  1. ignorance
    Synonym: ignōrantia
  2. imprudence
    Synonyms: stupiditās, īnsapientia, stultitia, sōcordia, ineptitūdō, inertia
    Antonyms: sapientia, prūdentia, calliditās, scientia
