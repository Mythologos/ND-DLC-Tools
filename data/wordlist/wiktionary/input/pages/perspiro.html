Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From per- +‎ spīrō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /perˈspiː.roː/, [pɛrˈs̠piːroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /perˈspi.ro/, [perˈspiːro]

Verb[edit]

perspīrō (present infinitive perspīrāre, perfect active perspīrāvī, supine perspīrātum); first conjugation

  1. to breathe everywhere
    • 234 BCE – 149 BCE, Cato the Elder, De Re Rustica 157
  2. to blow constantly
    • c. 77 CE – 79 CE, Pliny the Elder, Naturalis Historia 2.116
