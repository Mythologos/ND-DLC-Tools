Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dē- +‎ iugō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈdeː.i̯u.ɡoː/, [ˈd̪eːi̯ʊɡoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈde.ju.ɡo/, [ˈd̪ɛːjuɡo]

Verb[edit]

dēiugō (present infinitive dēiugāre, perfect active dēiugāvī, supine dēiugātum); first conjugation

  1. To unyoke
  2. To separate, sever
