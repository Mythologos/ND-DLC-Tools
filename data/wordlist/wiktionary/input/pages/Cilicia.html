English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Cilicia
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From Latin Cilicia, from Ancient Greek Κιλικία (Kilikía).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Received Pronunciation) IPA(key): /sɪˈlɪsɪə/, /sɪˈlɪʃə/

Proper noun[edit]

Cilicia

  1. (historical) An ancient region in southeastern Asia Minor, famed for cilice — haircloth made from the hair of Cilician goats

Derived terms[edit]

  • Cilician
  • Armenian Kingdom of Cilicia

Related terms[edit]

  • cilice

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Κιλικία (Kilikía).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kiˈli.ki.a/, [kɪˈlʲɪkiä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃiˈli.t͡ʃi.a/, [t͡ʃiˈliːt͡ʃiä]

Proper noun[edit]

Cilicia f sg (genitive Ciliciae); first declension

  1. Cilicia

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, with locative, singular only.
