Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈka.ri.ka/
  • Rhymes: -arika
  • Hyphenation: cà‧ri‧ca

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin carricō (“to load, to charge”).

Noun[edit]

carica f (plural cariche)

  1. position
  2. load
  3. (military) charge
Related terms[edit]
  • caricare

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Adjective[edit]

carica

  1. feminine singular of carico

Etymology 3[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

carica

  1. inflection of caricare:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Ellipsis of *Cārica fīcus f (“Carian fig”), from the feminine of the adjective Cāricus (“Carian”), from Cāria +‎ -icus (adjective-forming suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkaː.ri.ka/, [ˈkäːrɪkä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈka.ri.ka/, [ˈkäːrikä]

Noun[edit]

cārica f (genitive cāricae); first declension

  1. a type of fig (from Caria)
