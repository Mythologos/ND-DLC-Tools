Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From prae- +‎ rumpo.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /prae̯ˈrum.poː/, [präe̯ˈrʊmpoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /preˈrum.po/, [preˈrumpo]

Verb[edit]

praerumpō (present infinitive praerumpere, perfect active praerūpī, supine praeruptum); third conjugation

  1. to break off
  2. to tear to pieces
