Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From super- (“above, over”) +‎ fugiō (“flee”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /su.perˈfu.ɡi.oː/, [s̠ʊpɛrˈfʊɡioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /su.perˈfu.d͡ʒi.o/, [superˈfuːd͡ʒio]

Verb[edit]

superfugiō (present infinitive superfugere, perfect active superfūgī); third conjugation iō-variant, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to flee away over a thing
