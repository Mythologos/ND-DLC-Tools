Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /eksˈkep.toː/, [ɛks̠ˈkɛpt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /eksˈt͡ʃep.to/, [eksˈt͡ʃɛpt̪o]

Participle[edit]

exceptō

  1. dative/ablative masculine/neuter singular of exceptus

Verb[edit]

exceptō (present infinitive exceptāre, perfect active exceptāvī, supine exceptātum); first conjugation

  1. to take out or up
  2. to inhale

Portuguese[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Preposition[edit]

excepto

  1. Pre-reform spelling (until Brazil 1943/Portugal 1990) of exceto. Still used in countries where the agreement hasn't come into effect; may occur as a sporadic misspelling.

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin exceptus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Spain) /e(ɡ)ˈθebto/ [e(ɣ̞)ˈθeβ̞.t̪o]
  • IPA(key): (Latin America) /e(ɡ)ˈsebto/ [e(ɣ̞)ˈseβ̞.t̪o]
  • Audio (Colombia):(file)
  • Rhymes: -ebto

Preposition[edit]

excepto

  1. except

Verb[edit]

excepto

  1. first-person singular present indicative of exceptar
