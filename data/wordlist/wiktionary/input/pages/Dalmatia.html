English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Dalmatia
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From Latin Dalmatia, possibly from an Illyrian tribe called the Dalmatae who lived in the region.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /dælˈmeɪʃə/
  • Audio (U.S.A.)(file)

Proper noun[edit]

Dalmatia

  1. A region of Croatia, on the eastern coast of the Adriatic Sea.
  2. (historical) A province of the Roman Empire.

Derived terms[edit]

  • Dalmatian
  • dalmatic

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Delmatia

Etymology[edit]

From the Dalmatae.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /dalˈma.ti.a/, [d̪äɫ̪ˈmät̪iä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /dalˈmat.t͡si.a/, [d̪älˈmät̪ː͡s̪iä]

Proper noun[edit]

Dalmatia f sg (genitive Dalmatiae); first declension

  1. Dalmatia (province in the region of modern Croatia)

First-declension noun, singular only.
