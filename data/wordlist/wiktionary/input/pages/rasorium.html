Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From rādō (“shave”) +‎ -tōrium (suffix denoting a tool). Attested from the fifth century CE.[1] Compare *versōria.

Noun[edit]

rāsōrium n (genitive rāsōriī or rāsōrī); second declension (Late Latin)

  1. razor

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
