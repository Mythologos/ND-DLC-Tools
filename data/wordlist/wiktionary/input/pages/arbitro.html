Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈar.bi.tro/
  • Rhymes: -arbitro
  • Hyphenation: àr‧bi‧tro

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Latin arbitrum.

Noun[edit]

arbitro m (plural arbitri)

  1. (sports) referee, umpire
  2. arbitrator
Related terms[edit]
  • arbitrare

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

arbitro

  1. first-person singular present indicative of arbitrare

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈar.bi.troː/, [ˈärbɪt̪roː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈar.bi.tro/, [ˈärbit̪ro]

Etymology 1[edit]

From arbiter +‎ -ō.

Verb[edit]

arbitrō (present infinitive arbitrāre, perfect active arbitrāvī, supine arbitrātum); first conjugation

  1. to judge
  2. to consider
