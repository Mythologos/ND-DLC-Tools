Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

cōnsolidō +‎ -a (noun)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈso.li.da/, [kõːˈs̠ɔlʲɪd̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈso.li.da/, [konˈsɔːlid̪ä]

Noun[edit]

cōnsolida f (genitive cōnsolidae); first declension

  1. Alternative form of cōnferva (“(Symphytum officinale)”)
Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

  • Galician: consolda

  • Old French: consolde, consoulde, consoude

      • Middle English: consoude, consowde, consol, consold, consaud, consaude
        • English: consound
  • → Middle High German: cunsele, gunsel
    • German: Günsel
  • Portuguese: consolda; → consólida
  • Spanish: consuelda
  • Translingual: Consolida

References[edit]

Etymology 2[edit]

A regularly conjugated form of cōnsolidō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈso.li.daː/, [kõːˈs̠ɔlʲɪd̪äː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈso.li.da/, [konˈsɔːlid̪ä]

Verb[edit]

cōnsolidā

  1. second-person singular present active imperative of cōnsolidō
