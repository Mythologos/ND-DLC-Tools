Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From quadruplex (“fourfold”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kʷaˈdru.pli.koː/, [kʷäˈd̪rʊplʲɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kwaˈdru.pli.ko/, [kwäˈd̪ruːpliko]

Verb[edit]

quadruplicō (present infinitive quadruplicāre, perfect active quadruplicāvī, supine quadruplicātum); first conjugation

  1. to quadruple, multiply by four, make fourfold
    Synonym: quadruplō
