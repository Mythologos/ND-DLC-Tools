Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtuː.neːs/, [ˈt̪uːneːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtu.nes/, [ˈt̪uːnes]

Proper noun[edit]

Tūnēs m sg (genitive Tūnētis); third declension

  1. A town in Africa, now Tunis.

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun, with locative, singular only.

Borrowed from Arabic تُونِس (tūnis).

  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈtũ.nis/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈtũ.nis/
    • (Rio de Janeiro) IPA(key): /ˈtũ.niʃ/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈtu.nes/

  • Homophone: Túnis (Brazil)
  • Hyphenation: Tu‧nes

Proper noun[edit]

Tunes f

Tagalog[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Spanish Túnez.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Hyphenation: Tu‧nes
  • IPA(key): /ˈtunes/, [ˈtu.nɛs]

Proper noun[edit]

Tunes (Baybayin spelling ᜆᜓᜈᜒᜐ᜔)

  1. Tunisia (a country in North Africa)
