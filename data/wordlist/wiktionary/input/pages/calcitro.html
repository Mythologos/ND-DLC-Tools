Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From *calcitrum (“heel strike”) (from calx (“heel”) +‎ -trum, compare tālitrum) +‎ -ō.[1]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkal.ki.troː/, [ˈkäɫ̪kɪt̪roː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkal.t͡ʃi.tro/, [ˈkäl̠ʲt͡ʃit̪ro]

Verb[edit]

calcitrō (present infinitive calcitrāre, perfect active calcitrāvī, supine calcitrātum); first conjugation

  1. to strike with the heel (or foot)
  2. to kick (of an animal)
