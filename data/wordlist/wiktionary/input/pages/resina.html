Catalan[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • reïna

Etymology[edit]

In this form, borrowed from Latin rēsīna. The alternative form above was inherited.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [rəˈzi.nə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [reˈzi.na]

Noun[edit]

resina f (plural resines)

  1. resin, pitch

Derived terms[edit]

  • resinar
  • resiner
  • resínic
 

Related terms[edit]

  • resinífer
  • resinós
 

References[edit]

  • “resina” in Diccionari català-valencià-balear, Antoni Maria Alcover and Francesc de Borja Moll, 1962.

Further reading[edit]

  • “resina” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • rētīna (Medieval Latin)

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek ῥητῑ́νη (rhētī́nē).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reːˈsiː.na/, [reːˈs̠iːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈsi.na/, [reˈs̬iːnä]

Noun[edit]

rēsīna f (genitive rēsīnae); first declension

  1. resin

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Old Galician-Portuguese resina, from Latin rēsīna.

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ʁeˈzĩ.nɐ/ [heˈzĩ.nɐ]
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ʁeˈzĩ.nɐ/ [heˈzĩ.nɐ]
    • (Rio de Janeiro) IPA(key): /ʁeˈzĩ.nɐ/ [χeˈzĩ.nɐ]
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /ʁeˈzi.na/ [heˈzi.na]
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ʁɨˈzi.nɐ/

  • Hyphenation: re‧si‧na

Noun[edit]

resina f (plural resinas)

  1. resin (secretion of conifer trees)
  2. resin (any viscous liquid secreted by plants)

Related terms[edit]

  • resinoso
