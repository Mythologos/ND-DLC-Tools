Interlingua[edit]

Noun[edit]

uxor (plural uxores)

  1. wife

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • uxsor (Epigraphic Latin)

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *uksōr which is of unknown origin. Possibly cognate with Old Armenian ամուսին (amusin).[1][2] Alternatively a cognate to Latvian uõsis (“father-in-law”), Lithuanian uošvė (“mother-in-law”) and Ossetian ус (us, “woman”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈuk.sor/, [ˈʊks̠ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈuk.sor/, [ˈuksor]

Noun[edit]

uxor f (genitive uxōris); third declension

  1. a wife, a spouse, a consort

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative uxor uxōrēs
Genitive uxōris uxōrum
Dative uxōrī uxōribus
Accusative uxōrem uxōrēs
Ablative uxōre uxōribus
Vocative uxor uxōrēs

Synonyms[edit]

  • (wife, spouse): acoetis, coniūnx, mulier, nūpta, mātrōna

Derived terms[edit]

  • dūco uxōrem
  • et uxor, et ux
  • uxoricidium
  • uxōrius
  • iūre uxōris, jūre uxōris

Descendants[edit]

  • Old French: oissor, oissour, uissor, usor
    • Middle French: oisour (early)
  • ⇒ Aromanian: ãnsor
  • ⇒ Romanian: însura
  • → Interlingua: uxor
  • →⇒ English: uxoricide, uxorious, uxorial
