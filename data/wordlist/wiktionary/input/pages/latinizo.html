Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latīnus +‎ -izō, attested in Late Latin in Caelius Aurelianus (5th century), either a native formation by analogy with graecizō, or coined in Italiote Ancient Greek and borrowed into Latin. Compare Ῥωμαίζω (Rhōmaízō).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /la.tiːˈniz.zoː/, [ɫ̪ät̪iːˈnɪz̪d̪͡z̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /la.tiˈnid.d͡zo/, [lät̪iˈnid̪ː͡z̪o]

Verb[edit]

latīnizō (present infinitive latīnizāre, perfect active latīnizāvī, supine latīnizātum); first conjugation (Medieval Latin)

  1. to translate into Latin
    Synonym: latīnō
  2. (Medieval Latin) to speak Latin
    Synonyms: latīnō, latīnor
