Italian[edit]

Italian Wikipedia has an article on:
feudo
Wikipedia it

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Medieval Latin feudum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfɛw.do/
  • Rhymes: -ɛwdo
  • Hyphenation: fèu‧do

Noun[edit]

feudo m (plural feudi)

  1. feud, fiefdom, fief
  2. (figurative) domain, stronghold

Related terms[edit]

  • feudale
  • feudalesimo
  • feudatario

Descendants[edit]

  • → Greek: φέουδον (féoudon) (Katharevousa)
    • Greek: φέουδο (féoudo) (Modern)
  • → Romanian: feudă

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From feudum (“fief”).

Verb[edit]

feudō (present infinitive feudāre, perfect active feudāvī, supine feudātum); first conjugation

  1. to enfeoff
    • 1726, Johann Pistorius, Rerum Germanicarum veteres jam primum publicati scriptores aliquot insignes medii ævi ad Carolum V, volumes 3, 445:
      ipſi Principi Burgundiæ ducatum feudavit Gelriæ

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin feudum.

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈfew.du/ [ˈfeʊ̯.du]
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈfew.du/ [ˈfeʊ̯.du]
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈfew.do/ [ˈfeʊ̯.do]
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ˈfew.du/ [ˈfew.ðu]

  • Hyphenation: feu‧do

Noun[edit]

feudo m (plural feudos)

  1. fief, fiefdom, manor

Derived terms[edit]

  • feudal

Related terms[edit]

  • feudalismo, feudalista, feudatário

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin feudum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfeudo/ [ˈfeu̯.ð̞o]
  • Rhymes: -eudo
  • Syllabification: feu‧do

Noun[edit]

feudo m (plural feudos)

  1. fief
  2. stomping ground
  3. home, home ground, home soil

Related terms[edit]

  • feudal
