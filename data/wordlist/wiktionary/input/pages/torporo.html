Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From torpor (“numbness”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /torˈpoː.roː/, [t̪ɔrˈpoːroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /torˈpo.ro/, [t̪orˈpɔːro]

Verb[edit]

torpōrō (present infinitive torpōrāre, perfect active torpōrāvī, supine torpōrātum); first conjugation

  1. to benumb
    Synonym: torpefaciō
