English[edit]

Noun[edit]

amia (plural amias)

  1. The bowfin, Amia calva.

Anagrams[edit]

  • A.A.M.I., Maia

Dalmatian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin amita. Compare Venetian àmia.

Noun[edit]

amia

  1. aunt

Noun[edit]

amia

  1. bowfin, amia, Amia calva (species of freshwater fish native to North America)

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From New Latin, from Ancient Greek ἀμία (amía).

Noun[edit]

amia f (plural amie)

  1. bowfin (freshwater fish of the family Amiidae)

Anagrams[edit]

  • amai

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • amias

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek ἀμία (amía).

Noun[edit]

amia f (genitive amiae); first declension

  1. A sea fish, probably a tuna

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Romansch[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • amitga (Sursilvan, Sutsilvan)
  • ameia (Surmiran)

Etymology[edit]

From Latin amīca.

Noun[edit]

amia f (plural amias)

  1. (Rumantsch Grischun, Puter, Vallader) (female) friend, girlfriend

Coordinate terms[edit]

  • (gender): ami, amih
