Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

satis (“enough, sufficient”) +‎ faciō (“to make, construct”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /sa.tisˈfa.ki.oː/, [s̠ät̪ɪfˈfäkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /sa.tisˈfa.t͡ʃi.o/, [sät̪isˈfäːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

satisfaciō (present infinitive satisfacere, perfect active satisfēcī, supine satisfactum); third conjugation iō-variant, irregular passive voice

  1. to satisfy, content
  2. to secure, pay off
  3. to apologize, make amends
