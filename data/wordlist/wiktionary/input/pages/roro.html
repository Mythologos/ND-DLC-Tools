Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From rōs, rōris.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈroː.roː/, [ˈroːroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈro.ro/, [ˈrɔːro]

Verb[edit]

rōrō (present infinitive rōrāre, perfect active rōrāvī, supine rōrātum); first conjugation

  1. to cause dew, drip; be moist
  2. to drop, trickle, distil
  3. to sprinkle, drizzle (rain lightly)

Yoruba[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Compare with Olukumi rírò, Itsekiri oro likely a partial reduplication of a root rò (“to be bitter”). Compare with Igala ìlò (“bitter leaf”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ɾō.ɾò/

Verb[edit]

rorò

  1. (Ekiti, Owe, Eastern Akoko) to be bitter
    Synonyms: korò, lúkú, dúnkún
    Antonyms: dùn, yọ̀n

Adjective[edit]

rorò

  1. bitter
