English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
naevus
Wikipedia

Alternative forms[edit]

  • nævus, nevus

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin naevus.

Noun[edit]

naevus (plural naevi)

  1. (anatomy) A pigmented, raised or otherwise abnormal area on the skin, whether congenital or acquired.

Synonyms[edit]

  • birthmark
  • longing mark
  • mole
  • mother's mark
  • mother-spot
  • naeve

Derived terms[edit]

Derived terms
  • blue naevus
  • melanocytic naevus
  • naevogenesis
  • naevoid
  • naevus flammeus
  • spider naevus

Translations[edit]

abnormal area on skin — see mole

Anagrams[edit]

  • Uveans, unsave

Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin naevus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈnɑeʋus/, [ˈnɑ̝e̞ʋus̠]
  • Rhymes: -ɑeʋus
  • Syllabification(key): na‧e‧vus

Noun[edit]

naevus

  1. nevus (benign tumor on skin)

Usage notes[edit]

This word is mainly known to and thus used by the medical professionals.

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • gnaevus

Etymology[edit]

For gnaevus, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵn̥h₁iwós (“native”), from *ǵenh₁- (“to beget”). Probable doublet of Gnaeus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈnae̯.u̯us/, [ˈnäe̯u̯ʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈne.vus/, [ˈnɛːvus]

Noun[edit]

naevus m (genitive naevī); second declension

  1. birthmark
  2. mole (on the body)

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.
