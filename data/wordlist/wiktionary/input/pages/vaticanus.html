Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From vāticinor (“I prophesy”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /u̯aː.tiːˈkaː.nus/, [u̯äːt̪iːˈkäːnʊs̠] or IPA(key): /u̯aː.tiˈkaː.nus/, [u̯äːt̪ɪˈkäːnʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /va.tiˈka.nus/, [vät̪iˈkäːnus]

Proper noun[edit]

Vātī̆cānus m (genitive Vātī̆cānī); second declension

  1. The Vatican, a hill in Rome, Italy
  2. Vatican City, the small state forming the headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church
  3. The papacy, the government of the state and Catholic Church
  4. (Roman mythology) Alternative form of Vagitanus

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun, singular only.
