Crimean Tatar[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Turkish biber, from Greek πιπέρι (pipéri).

Noun[edit]

biber

  1. pepper

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • biberis, bibera, biberium, bibarium

Etymology[edit]

From the infinitive bibere (“to drink”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbi.ber/, [ˈbɪbɛr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbi.ber/, [ˈbiːber]

Noun[edit]

biber f (genitive biberis); third declension

  1. a drink, beverage.

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.

Serbo-Croatian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ottoman Turkish ببر (biber), from Greek πιπέρι, πιπεριά (pipéri, piperiá).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /bǐber/
  • Hyphenation: bi‧ber

Noun[edit]

bìber m (Cyrillic spelling бѝбер)

  1. (uncountable) pepper (spice)

Declension[edit]

biber (definite accusative biberi, plural biberler)
