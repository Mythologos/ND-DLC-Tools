Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • dēlitīscō

Etymology[edit]

From dē- +‎ latēscō (“hide”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deː.liˈteːs.koː/, [d̪eːlʲɪˈt̪eːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /de.liˈtes.ko/, [d̪eliˈt̪ɛsko]

Verb[edit]

dēlitēscō (present infinitive dēlitēscere, perfect active dēlituī); third conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to hide, or go into hiding
  2. to withdraw
  3. to take refuge
    Synonyms: concurrō, perfugiō, cōnfugiō
