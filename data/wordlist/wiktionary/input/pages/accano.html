Italian[edit]

Verb[edit]

accano

  1. first-person singular present indicative of accanare

Anagrams[edit]

  • cacano, cacona

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • accinō

Etymology[edit]

ac- (“to”, “toward”) +‎ canō (“I sing”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈak.ka.noː/, [ˈäkːänoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈak.ka.no/, [ˈäkːäno]

Verb[edit]

accanō (present infinitive accanere, perfect active accecinī); third conjugation, no supine stem, impersonal in the passive

  1. (intransitive) to sing to or with
    • 1577, Matthaeus Toscanus, Carmina illustrium poetarum italorum, page 44:
      Accecinit cantu Graio...
