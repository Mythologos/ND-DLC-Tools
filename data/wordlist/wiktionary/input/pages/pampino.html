Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin pampinus (“foliage of a vine, vine leaf”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈpam.pi.no/
  • Rhymes: -ampino
  • Hyphenation: pàm‧pi‧no

Noun[edit]

pampino m (plural pampini)

  1. vine leaf

Related terms[edit]

  • pampinifero
  • pampinoso

Anagrams[edit]

  • mappino

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From pampinus (“tendril, vine-shoot”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈpam.pi.noː/, [ˈpämpɪnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpam.pi.no/, [ˈpämpino]

Verb[edit]

pampinō (present infinitive pampināre, perfect active pampināvī, supine pampinātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to prune of superfluous tendrils, shoots and growth of vines; trim, prune
  2. (transitive) to trim or prune trees

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /pamˈpino/ [pãmˈpi.no]
  • Rhymes: -ino
  • Syllabification: pam‧pi‧no

Adjective[edit]

pampino (feminine pampina, masculine plural pampinos, feminine plural pampinas)

  1. of or from Pampas, Peru

Noun[edit]

pampino m (plural pampinos, feminine pampina, feminine plural pampinas)

  1. someone from Pampas, Peru
