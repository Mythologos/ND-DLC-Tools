Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From trīcae.

Verb[edit]

tricor (present infinitive tricārī, perfect active tricātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. to behave in an evasive manner, search for detours, dodge
  2. to make difficult ties, trifle, dally, shuffle, play tricks
