Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin lūbricus, possibly a borrowing.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈlu.bri.ko/, /luˈbri.ko/[1]
  • Rhymes: -ubriko, -iko
  • Hyphenation: lù‧bri‧co, lu‧brì‧co

Adjective[edit]

lubrico (feminine lubrica, masculine plural lubrici, feminine plural lubriche)

  1. (literary) slippery
    Synonyms: scivoloso, sdrucciolevole
  2. (literary) slimy, slick (of animals)
  3. (archaic) that evacuates easily
  4. (archaic, rare) laxative
  5. (figurative) lewd, lubricious, lubricous

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈluː.bri.koː/, [ˈɫ̪uːbrɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈlu.bri.ko/, [ˈluːbriko]

Verb[edit]

lūbricō (present infinitive lūbricāre, perfect active lūbricāvī, supine lūbricātum); first conjugation

  1. to make slippery
  2. to lubricate, oil, grease
  3. slip
