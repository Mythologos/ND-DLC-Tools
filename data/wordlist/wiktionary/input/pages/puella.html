Etymology[edit]

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From puellus (“a little boy”), contracted from puerulus (“little boy, little slave”), the diminutive of puer (“boy”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /puˈel.la/, [puˈɛlːʲä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /puˈel.la/, [puˈɛlːä]

Noun[edit]

puella f (genitive puellae, masculine puellus); first declension

  1. girl, lass, maiden (female child)
    parvola puella ― a young girl
    pueri atque puellae ― boys and girls
    pueri innuptaeque puellae ― boys and unmarried maidens
  2. (poetic) sweetheart, mistress, beloved maiden
    cara mea puella ― my beloved girl
  3. young woman, young wife
  4. (rare) female slave
