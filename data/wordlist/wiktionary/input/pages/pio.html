Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin pius.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈpi.o/
  • Rhymes: -io
  • Hyphenation: pì‧o

Adjective[edit]

pio (feminine pia, masculine plural pii, feminine plural pie)

  1. pious, devout, prayerful
  2. charitable

Synonyms[edit]

  • mansueto
  • mite
  • tranquillo

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From pius.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈpi.oː/, [ˈpioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpi.o/, [ˈpiːo]

Verb[edit]

piō (present infinitive piāre, perfect active piāvī, supine piātum); first conjugation

  1. to appease, propitiate
  2. to purify, expiate

Portuguese[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈpi.u/, /ˈpiw/ [ˈpiʊ̯]
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ˈpi.u/

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin pius.

Adjective[edit]

pio (feminine pia, masculine plural pios, feminine plural pias)

  1. pious
  2. compassionate
Related terms[edit]
  • piedade

Etymology 2[edit]

Deverbal from piar.

Noun[edit]

pio m (plural pios)

  1. chirp (sound emitted by chicks)
    Synonym: piado

Etymology 3[edit]

Verb[edit]

pio

  1. first-person singular present indicative of piar
