Esperanto[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)
  • IPA(key): [fiˈɡuro]
  • Rhymes: -uro
  • Hyphenation: fi‧gu‧ro

Noun[edit]

figuro (accusative singular figuron, plural figuroj, accusative plural figurojn)

  1. figure

Derived terms[edit]

  • ĉeffiguro

Ido[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Esperanto figuro, from English figure, French figure, German Figur, Italian figura, Russian фигура (figura), Spanish figura, all ultimately from Latin figūra.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /fiˈɡuro/
  • IPA(key): /fi.ˈɡu.ro/, /fi.ˈɡu.ɾɔ/

Noun[edit]

figuro (plural figuri)

  1. figure, configuration
  2. (rhetoric, geometry) figure

Synonyms[edit]

  • simbolo

Derived terms[edit]

  • figurala (“figurative”)
  • figurale (“figuratively”)
  • figurizar (“to typify, configure”)

Etymology 1[edit]

Noun[edit]

figuro m (plural figuri)

  1. a shady or suspicious person

Etymology 2[edit]

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From figūra (“figure”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /fiˈɡuː.roː/, [fɪˈɡuːroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /fiˈɡu.ro/, [fiˈɡuːro]

Verb[edit]

figūrō (present infinitive figūrāre, perfect active figūrāvī, supine figūrātum); first conjugation

  1. to form, fashion, shape
