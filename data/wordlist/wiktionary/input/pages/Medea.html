Medea

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Μήδεια (Mḗdeia).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /mɪˈdiːə/, /məˈdiːə/.
  • Rhymes: -iːə

Proper noun[edit]

Medea

  1. (Greek mythology) An enchantress who helped Jason obtain the Golden Fleece.

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Mēdīa

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Μήδεια (Mḗdeia).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /meːˈdeː.a/, [meːˈd̪eːä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /meˈde.a/, [meˈd̪ɛːä]

Proper noun[edit]

Mēdēa f sg (genitive Mēdēae); first declension

  1. (Greek mythology) Medea

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, singular only.

Nominative Mēdēa
Genitive Mēdēae

Old Latin genitive: Medeai
