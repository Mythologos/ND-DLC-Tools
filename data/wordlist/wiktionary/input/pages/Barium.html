English[edit]

Chemical element
Ba
Previous: caesium (Cs)
Next: lanthanum (La)

Etymology[edit]

Coined by British chemist Humphry Davy in 1808, from baryta +‎ -ium.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈbɛəɹɪəm/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɛəɹɪəm

Noun[edit]

barium (usually uncountable, plural bariums)

  2. The chemical element (symbol Ba) with an atomic number of 56. It is a soft, reactive, silvery alkaline earth metal.
  3. (countable) A single atom of this element.
  4. (medicine) barium sulfate suspension.
