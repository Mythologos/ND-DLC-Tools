Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtuː.toː/, [ˈt̪uːt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtu.to/, [ˈt̪uːt̪o]

Etymology 1[edit]

From tūtus +‎ -ō; compare tūtē.

Adverb[edit]

tūtō (comparative tūtius, superlative tūtissimē or tūtissimō)

  1. safely, securely, in safety, without danger, without risk of being harmed
    tūtō ab incursū
    safe from attack
Derived terms[edit]
  • tūtō sum

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From the Mapudungun tute (“hip”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈtuto/ [ˈt̪u.t̪o]
  • Rhymes: -uto
  • Syllabification: tu‧to

Noun[edit]

tuto m (plural tutos)

  1. (Chile) thigh, leg, shank
    Synonyms: muslo, pata, pierna
  2. (Chile, childish) sleepiness
    Synonym: sueño
    hacer tuto ― to go to sleep
    Estoy cansada, quiero hacer tuto. ― I'm tired, I want to go to sleep.
  3. (Chile, childish) security blanket (comfort object)
    Synonyms: mantita, cobijita

Derived terms[edit]

  • hacer tuto
 

Further reading[edit]

  • “tuto”, in Diccionario de la lengua española, Vigésima tercera edición, Real Academia Española, 2014
