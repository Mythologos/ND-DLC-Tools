English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin ōvum (“egg”). Doublet of egg, ey, huevo, and oeuf.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈəʊ.vəm/
  • (US) IPA(key): /ˈoʊ.vəm/
  • Audio (UK)(file)

Noun[edit]

ovum (plural ova)

  1. (cytology) The female gamete in animals; the egg cell.

Synonyms[edit]

  • megagamete

Hypernyms[edit]

  • gamete

Coordinate terms[edit]

  • spermatozoon

Derived terms[edit]

  • ovist

Related terms[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Malay ovum, from Latin ōvum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɔvʊm/
  • Rhymes: -ɔvʊm, -vʊm, -ʊm, -m
  • Hyphenation: ovum

Noun[edit]

ovum (plural ovum-ovum, first-person possessive ovumku, second-person possessive ovummu, third-person possessive ovumnya)

  1. (cytology) ovum (gamete)
    Synonym: sel telur

      Dibuahi sperma dan ludah
      Diberkati jiwa yang cacat
      Formed from despised ovum
      Fertilized sperm and saliva
      Blessed with defective soul

Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
ovum
Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂ōwyóm (“egg”).[1][2] Cognate with Ancient Greek ᾠόν (ōión).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈoː.u̯um/, [ˈoːu̯ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈo.vum/, [ˈɔːvum]

Noun[edit]

ōvum n (genitive ōvī); second declension

  1. egg

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).

Case Singular Plural
Nominative ōvum ōva
Genitive ōvī ōvōrum
Dative ōvō ōvīs
Accusative ōvum ōva
Ablative ōvō ōvīs
Vocative ōvum ōva

Derived terms[edit]

  • ōvicō
  • ōviparus
  • ōvārium
  • ōvātus
 

Related terms[edit]

  • avis (“bird”)
