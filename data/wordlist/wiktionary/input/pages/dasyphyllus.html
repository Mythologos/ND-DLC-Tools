Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek δασύς (dasús, “hairy, shaggy, dense”) + φύλλον (phúllon, “leaf”).

Adjective[edit]

dasyphyllus (feminine dasyphylla, neuter dasyphyllum); first/second-declension adjective

  1. (New Latin) used as a specific epithet in taxonomic names; Having dense, shaggy leaves; dasyphyllous.

Usage notes[edit]

  • Used exclusively as a taxonomic epithet and thus normally in the nominative singular; other inflections may be theoretical or rarely found.
