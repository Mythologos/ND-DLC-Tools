Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin mersus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmɛr.so/[1]
  • Rhymes: -ɛrso
  • Hyphenation: mèr‧so

Participle[edit]

merso (feminine mersa, masculine plural mersi, feminine plural merse)

  1. past participle of mergere

References[edit]

  1. ^ immerso in Luciano Canepari, Dizionario di Pronuncia Italiana (DiPI)

Anagrams[edit]

  • morse, sermo

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Frequentative of mergō; cf. mersus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈmer.soː/, [ˈmɛrs̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈmer.so/, [ˈmɛrso]

Verb[edit]

mersō (present infinitive mersāre, perfect active mersāvī, supine mersātum); first conjugation

  1. to immerse
  2. to overwhelm, drown
