Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From intendō +‎ -tiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈten.ti.oː/, [ɪn̪ˈt̪ɛn̪t̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈten.t͡si.o/, [in̪ˈt̪ɛnt̪͡s̪io]

Noun[edit]

intentiō f (genitive intentiōnis); third declension

  1. strain, tension
  2. increase, augmentation
  3. exertion, effort
  4. charge, accusation
  5. purpose, intention
    Synonyms: voluntās, propositum, cōnsilium, fīnis, mēns, animus
