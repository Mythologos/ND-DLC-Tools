Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /alˈtaː.ri.a/, [äɫ̪ˈt̪äːriä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /alˈta.ri.a/, [äl̪ˈt̪äːriä]

Noun[edit]

altāria

  1. nominative/accusative/vocative plural of altar
  2. nominative/accusative/vocative plural of altāre

Noun[edit]

altāria n pl (genitive altārium); third declension

  1. Alternative form of altāre

Usage notes[edit]

This plurale tantum form occurs in pre-Classical and Classical Latin.

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun (neuter, “pure” i-stem), plural only.

Polish[edit]

Polish Wikipedia has an article on:
altaria
Wikipedia pl

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin altāria.[1][2] Doublet of ołtarz. First attested in the 16th century.[3]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /alˈta.rja/
  • Rhymes: -arja
  • Syllabification: al‧ta‧ria

Noun[edit]

altaria f

  1. (archaic, religion) altar fund, altar maintenance (collection of money for an altar)
    ufundować altarię ― to fund altar maintenance

Declension[edit]

Declension of altaria
singular plural
nominative altaria altarie
genitive altarii altarii/altaryj (archaic)
dative altarii altariom
accusative altarię altarie
instrumental altarią altariami
locative altarii altariach
vocative altario altarie
