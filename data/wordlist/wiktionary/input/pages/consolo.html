Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ sōlor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈsoː.loː/, [kõːˈs̠oːɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈso.lo/, [konˈsɔːlo]

Verb[edit]

cōnsōlō (present infinitive cōnsōlāre, perfect active cōnsōlāvī, supine cōnsōlātum); first conjugation

  1. to console, cheer, comfort
    Synonym: foveō

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Deverbal from consolar.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /kõˈso.lu/

  • Hyphenation: con‧so‧lo

Noun[edit]

consolo m (plural consolos)

  1. consolation, comfort, solace
    Synonym: consolação
  2. (Brazil, colloquial) dildo
    Synonym: dildo
