English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Late Latin dēnōminātor (“that which names”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /dɪˈnɒmɪneɪtə(ɹ)/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)

Noun[edit]

denominator (plural denominators)

  1. (arithmetic) The number or expression written below the line in a fraction (such as 2 in ½).
  2. One who gives a name to something.

Antonyms[edit]

  • numerator

Derived terms[edit]

  • common denominator
  • lowest common denominator

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dēnōminō +‎ -tor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deː.noː.miˈnaː.tor/, [d̪eːnoːmɪˈnäːt̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /de.no.miˈna.tor/, [d̪enomiˈnäːt̪or]

Noun[edit]

dēnōminātor m (genitive dēnōminātōris); third declension

  1. one who names or designates
