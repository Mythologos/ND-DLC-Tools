English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
emir
Wikipedia

Alternative forms[edit]

  • amir (directly from Arabic)
  • ameer, amier, emeer (all archaic)

Etymology[edit]

From Middle French emir, from Ottoman Turkish امیر (emir), from Arabic أَمِير (ʔamīr, “commander, prince”). Akin to amir, Amir and admiral. Doublet of amira.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ɛˈmɪə(ɹ)/, /ˈɛmɪə(ɹ)/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɪə(ɹ)

Noun[edit]

emir (plural emirs or (rare) umara)

  1. A prince, commander or other leader or ruler in an Islamic nation.
  2. A descendant of the prophet Muhammad.

Derived terms[edit]

  • emirate
