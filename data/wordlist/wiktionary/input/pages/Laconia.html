English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Laconia
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Λακωνία (Lakōnía).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ləˈkəʊ.ni.ə/

Proper noun[edit]

Laconia

  1. A region in the southern Peloponnese, Greece, which has had Sparta as its capital for over 3,000 years.
  2. A city, the county seat of Belknap County, New Hampshire; named for the Laconia Car Company, a railroad car manufacturer.
  3. A town in Indiana.
  4. An unincorporated community in Tennessee.

Related terms[edit]

  • Laconian
  • laconic

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Lăcōnĭca

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Λακωνία (Lakōnía). (Can this(+) etymology be sourced?) From the Ancient Greek expression "Λακωνική (Lakōnikḗ), sc. γῆ f (gê)" (the land of the Lacons), from Λάκων (Lákōn, “Laconian”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /laˈkoː.ni.a/, [ɫ̪äˈkoːniä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /laˈko.ni.a/, [läˈkɔːniä]

Proper noun[edit]

Lacōnia f sg (genitive Lacōniae); first declension

  1. Laconia
