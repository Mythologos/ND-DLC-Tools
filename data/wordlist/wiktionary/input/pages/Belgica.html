Etymology[edit]

From Latin Belgica. Cognates include French Belgique and Spanish Bélgica.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈbeld͡ʒika/

Proper noun[edit]

Belgica f

  1. Belgium (a country in Western Europe that has borders with the Netherlands, Germany, Luxembourg and France)

Synonyms[edit]

Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
Belgica
Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

From Belgicus/belgicus, short for Gallia Belgica (“Belgic Gaul”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbel.ɡi.ka/, [ˈbɛɫ̪ɡɪkä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbel.d͡ʒi.ka/, [ˈbɛl̠ʲd͡ʒikä]

Proper noun[edit]

Belgica f sg (genitive Belgicae); first declension

  1. Belgium (a country in Western Europe that has borders with the Netherlands, Germany, Luxembourg and France)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, singular only.
