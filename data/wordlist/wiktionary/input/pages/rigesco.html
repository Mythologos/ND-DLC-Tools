Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

rigeō (“to be stiff”) +‎ -scō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /riˈɡeːs.koː/, [rɪˈɡeːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /riˈd͡ʒes.ko/, [riˈd͡ʒɛsko]

Verb[edit]

rigēscō (present infinitive rigēscere, perfect active riguī); third conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to stiffen, harden
  2. to grow numb
