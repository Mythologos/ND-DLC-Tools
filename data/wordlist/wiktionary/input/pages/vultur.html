Aromanian[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • vulturu, vãltur, vãlturu, vãltor, vãltoru

Etymology[edit]

From Latin vultur or vulturius. Compare Romanian vultur.

Noun[edit]

vultur m (plural vulturi)

  1. vulture
  2. eagle

Synonyms[edit]

  • Synonyms: ljipurar, ornji, aito, atsirã, utã, hutã, hutcã, stavrait, schiponj, schifter

Romanian[edit]

Romanian Wikipedia has an article on:
vultur
Wikipedia ro

Alternative forms[edit]

  • vulture, hultur, hulture

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Latin vultur, vulturem (“vulture”) or vulturius. The standard pronunciation has the accent on the first syllable, but there is a variant with it on the second.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈvultur], [vulˈtur]

Noun[edit]

vultur m (plural vulturi)

  1. accipitrid
    1. vulture
    2. buzzard
    3. eagle
    4. kite
    5. harrier
  2. (figuratively) a brave, admirable man
