Italian[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Latin coagulum. Compare caglio, an inherited doublet.

Noun[edit]

coagulo m (plural coaguli)

  1. clot (of blood etc.)
  2. curd (of milk etc.)
Related terms[edit]
  • coagulare

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

coagulo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of coagulare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From coāgulum +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /koˈaː.ɡu.loː/, [koˈäːɡʊɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /koˈa.ɡu.lo/, [koˈäːɡulo]

Verb[edit]

coāgulō (present infinitive coāgulāre, perfect active coāgulāvī, supine coāgulātum); first conjugation

  1. to curdle; make thick, solid
  2. to coagulate
    solve et coāgulā
    (An alchemical principle)
