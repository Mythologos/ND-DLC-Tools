Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

dē- +‎ texō (“to weave”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deːˈtek.soː/, [d̪eːˈt̪ɛks̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /deˈtek.so/, [d̪eˈt̪ɛkso]

Verb[edit]

dētexō (present infinitive dētexere, perfect active dētexuī, supine dētextum); third conjugation

  1. (transitive) to weave, plait (finish weaving, plaiting)
  2. (transitive, figuratively) to describe, complete
