English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
epulis
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from New Latin epūlis.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (General American) IPA(key): /ɪˈpju.lɪs/
  • Rhymes: -uːlɪs

Noun[edit]

epulis (plural epulides)

  1. (medicine) A hard tumour or tumorous growth developed from the gums.

References[edit]

  • “epulis”, in Merriam-Webster Online Dictionary, Springfield, Mass.: Merriam-Webster, 1996–present.

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • epūlis: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /eˈpu.lis/, [eˈpuːlis]
  • epulīs: (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈe.pu.liːs/, [ˈɛpʊlʲiːs̠]
  • epulīs: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈe.pu.lis/, [ˈɛːpulis]

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek ἐπουλῐ́ς (epoulís, “growth on the gums”).

Noun[edit]

epūlis f (genitive epūlidos); third declension (New Latin)

  1. (medicine) A growth on the gum; an epulis.
Inflection[edit]

Third-declension noun (Greek-type, normal variant).
