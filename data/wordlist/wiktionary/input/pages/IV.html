Translingual[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • IIII, iv, iiii, iiij
  • IV. (ordinal number)

Etymology[edit]

One (I) less than five (V)

Numeral[edit]

IV

  1. A Roman numeral representing four (4).

Symbol[edit]

IV

  1. Used to indicate an ordinal number; the fourth (common as a name suffix)
  2. (inorganic chemistry) Specifying an oxidation state of 4
  3. (music) major subdominant triad

English[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˌaɪ ˈviː/

Adjective[edit]

IV (not comparable)

  1. (medicine) Initialism of intravenous.

Noun[edit]

IV (plural IVs)

  1. (medicine) Initialism of intravenous injection.
  2. (cryptography) Initialism of initialization vector.
  3. (statistics) Initialism of instrumental variable.
  4. Initialism of immigrant visa.

Anagrams[edit]

  • -vi-, V. I., V.I., VI, Vi, v.i., vi, vi.

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Proper noun[edit]

IV

  1. Abbreviation of Iupiter (“Jupiter”).
