Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From prae- (“before”) +‎ fodiō (“dig”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /prae̯ˈfo.di.oː/, [präe̯ˈfɔd̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /preˈfo.di.o/, [preˈfɔːd̪io]

Verb[edit]

praefodiō (present infinitive praefodere, perfect active praefōdī, supine praefossum); third conjugation iō-variant

  1. to dig or bury before or in front of
  2. (of time) to dig or bury before
