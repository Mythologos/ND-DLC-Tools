Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From sē- +‎ moveō (“move”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /seːˈmo.u̯e.oː/, [s̠eːˈmou̯eoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /seˈmo.ve.o/, [seˈmɔːveo]

Verb[edit]

sēmoveō (present infinitive sēmovēre, perfect active sēmōvī, supine sēmōtum); second conjugation

  1. to move apart, put aside, remove, separate, part
