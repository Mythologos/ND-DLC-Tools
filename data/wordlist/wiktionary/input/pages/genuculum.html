Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Modification of earlier geniculum, diminutive of genū (“knee”). Attested from the fourth century CE.[1]

Noun[edit]

genuculum n (genitive genuculī); second declension (Late Latin)

  1. knee

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
