Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Frequentative of tangō (“I touch”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtak.soː/, [ˈt̪äks̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtak.so/, [ˈt̪äkso]

Verb[edit]

taxō (present infinitive taxāre, perfect active taxāvī, supine taxātum); first conjugation

  1. to feel, to touch sharply, to handle
  2. to charge, to twit, to reproach, to censure
  3. to rate, to appraise, to value, to estimate
  4. to judge, to compute, to reckon, to estimate

Etymology 2[edit]

From Frankish *þahs, from Proto-Germanic *þahsuz (“badger”), probably from Proto-Indo-European *teḱs- (“construct”) after the badger's construction of its setts. See texō.

Alternative forms[edit]

  • taxus

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtak.soː/, [ˈt̪äks̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtak.so/, [ˈt̪äkso]

Noun[edit]

taxō m (genitive taxonis); third declension

  1. (Late Latin) badger
Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.
