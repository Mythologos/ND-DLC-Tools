English[edit]

Chemical element
Rh
Previous: ruthenium (Ru)
Next: palladium (Pd)

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from New Latin rhodium, from Ancient Greek ῥόδον (rhódon, “rose”), because of the colour of its salts' solutions.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: rō'dēəm, IPA(key): /ˈɹoʊdiəm/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • Rhymes: -oʊdiəm

Noun[edit]

rhodium (usually uncountable, plural rhodiums)

English Wikipedia has an article on:
rhodium
Wikipedia
  2. A rare, hard, silvery-white, inert metallic chemical element (symbol Rh) with an atomic number of 45.
  3. (countable) A single atom of this element.

Derived terms[edit]

  • rhodate
  • rhodium-gold
  • rhodium trichloride

Related terms[edit]

  • rhodic
  • rhodo- and its derivatives
  • rhodonite
  • rhodium wood, lignum rhodium
