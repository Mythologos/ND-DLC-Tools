Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • circumsēpiō

Etymology[edit]

From circum- +‎ saepiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈsae̯.pi.oː/, [kɪrkũːˈs̠äe̯pioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈse.pi.o/, [t͡ʃirkumˈsɛːpio]

Verb[edit]

circumsaepiō (present infinitive circumsaepīre, perfect active circumsaepsī, supine circumsaeptum); fourth conjugation

  1. to surround or enclose with a hedge or fence
    Synonyms: circumveniō, circumeō, circumsistō, circumdō, claudō, obsideō, saepiō
