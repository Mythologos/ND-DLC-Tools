English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek Ἄγρων (Ágrōn).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (US) /æɡrɑn̩/

Proper noun[edit]

Agron

  1. (historical) An Illyrian king.

Translations[edit]

Illyrian king
  • Greek:
    Ancient: Ἄγρων (Ágrōn)
  • Italian: Agrone m
  • Latin: Agrōn m

Further reading[edit]

  • Agron of Illyria on Wikipedia.Wikipedia

Albanian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek Ἄγρων (Ágrōn), the name of an Illyrian king.

Proper noun[edit]

Agron m

  1. a male given name from Illyrian

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek Ἄγρων (Ágrōn).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈa.ɡron/, [ˈäɡrɔn]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈa.ɡron/, [ˈäːɡron]

Proper noun[edit]

Agron m sg (genitive Agronis); third declension

  1. (historical) Agron, an Illyrian king

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun, singular only.
