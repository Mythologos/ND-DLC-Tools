Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • īnfrendeō

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ frendō (“I gnash”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈfren.doː/, [ĩːˈfrɛn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈfren.do/, [iɱˈfrɛn̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

īnfrendō (present infinitive īnfrendere); third conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. (intransitive) to gnash the teeth
  2. (intransitive, with dative) to gnash the teeth at, to threaten furiously
