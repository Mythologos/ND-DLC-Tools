Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Unadapted borrowing from Ancient Greek ἐνεργούμενος (energoúmenos, participle of ἐνεργέω (energéō, “be in (supernatural) action”)).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /e.nerˈɡuː.me.nos/, [ɛnɛrˈɡuːmɛnɔs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /e.nerˈɡu.me.nos/, [enerˈɡuːmenos]

Adjective[edit]

energūmenos (feminine energūmena, neuter energūmenon); first/second-declension adjective (Greek-type)

  1. possessed by the devil
    • c. 363 CE – c. 425 CE, Sulpicius Severus, Dialogues 3.6.2:
      videres per totam ecclesiam energumenos rugientes
