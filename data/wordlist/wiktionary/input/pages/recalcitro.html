Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From re- +‎ calcitrō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reˈkal.ki.troː/, [rɛˈkäɫ̪kɪt̪roː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈkal.t͡ʃi.tro/, [reˈkäl̠ʲt͡ʃit̪ro]

Verb[edit]

recalcitrō (present infinitive recalcitrāre, perfect active recalcitrāvī, supine recalcitrātum); first conjugation

  1. to kick back (of a horse)
  2. to be disobedient
    Antonyms: pāreō, exaudiō, oboediō, obtemperō
