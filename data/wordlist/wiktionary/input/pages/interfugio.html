Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From inter- (“between”) +‎ fugiō (“flee”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈfu.ɡi.oː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈfʊɡioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈfu.d͡ʒi.o/, [in̪t̪erˈfuːd͡ʒio]

Verb[edit]

interfugiō (present infinitive interfugere, perfect active interfūgī); third conjugation iō-variant, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to flee between or into
