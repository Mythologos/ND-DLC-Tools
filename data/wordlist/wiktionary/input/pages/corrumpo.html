Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

con- +‎ rumpō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /korˈrum.poː/, [kɔrˈrʊmpoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /korˈrum.po/, [korˈrumpo]

Verb[edit]

corrumpō (present infinitive corrumpere, perfect active corrūpī, supine corruptum); third conjugation

  1. to pervert, corrupt, deprave or infect
    Synonyms: delēniō, aggredior
    • 397 CE – 401 CE, Aurelius Augustinus Hipponensis, Confessions 7.12.18:
      Et manifestātum est mihi quoniam bona sunt quae corrumpuntur.
      And it was made clear to me that all things are good even if they are corrupted.
  2. to gain to oneself by gifts, etc.; bribe
    Synonym: largior
  3. to spoil or rot
  4. to taint or contaminate
  5. to tempt or seduce
