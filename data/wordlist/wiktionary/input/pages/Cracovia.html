Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Cracōv- (compare Polish Kraków) +‎ -ia

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Cracōvia: (Classical) IPA(key): /kraˈkoː.u̯i.a/, [kräˈkoːu̯iä]
  • Cracōvia: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kraˈko.vi.a/, [kräˈkɔːviä]
  • Cracōviā: (Classical) IPA(key): /kraˈkoː.u̯i.aː/, [kräˈkoːu̯iäː]
  • Cracōviā: (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kraˈko.vi.a/, [kräˈkɔːviä]

Proper noun[edit]

Cracōvia f sg (genitive Cracōviae); first declension

  1. Cracow (a city in Poland)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, with locative, singular only.
