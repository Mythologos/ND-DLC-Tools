English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Italian adagio.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /əˈdɑːd͡ʒiəʊ/, /əˈdæd͡ʒiəʊ/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)

Noun[edit]

adagio (plural adagios)

  2. (music) A tempo mark directing that a passage is to be played rather slowly, leisurely and gracefully.
  4. (music) A passage having this mark.
  6. (dance) A male-female duet or mixed trio ballet displaying demanding balance, spins and/or lifts.

Translations[edit]

tempo mark indicating that music is to be played slowly
  • Chinese:
    Mandarin: 慢板 (zh) (mànbǎn)
  • Corsican: adaghju m
  • Finnish: adagio (fi)
  • Italian: adagio (it) m
  • Polish: adagio (pl) n
  • Portuguese: adágio (pt) m

Adverb[edit]

adagio (not comparable)

  2. (music) Played rather slowly.

Translations[edit]

Translations
  • Finnish: adagio (fi), hitaasti (fi)
  • Polish: adagio (pl)

Adjective[edit]

adagio (not comparable)

  2. (music) Describing a passage having this mark.

Translations[edit]

Translations
  • Finnish: adagio (fi)
  • Polish: adagio (pl)

Polish[edit]

Polish Wikipedia has an article on:
adagio
Wikipedia pl

Alternative forms[edit]

  • adadżjo (Pre-reform orthography (1936))
  • adadżio

Etymology[edit]

Unadapted borrowing from Italian adagio.[1][2] First attested in 1823.[3]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /aˈdad͡ʐ.jɔ/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -ad͡ʐjɔ
  • Syllabification: a‧da‧gio

Noun[edit]

adagio n (indeclinable)

  1. (music) adagio (a tempo mark directing that a passage is to be played rather slowly, leisurely and gracefully)
  2. (music) adagio (a passage having this mark)
  3. (dance) adagio (a male-female duet or mixed trio ballet displaying demanding balance, spins and/or lifts)

Declension[edit]

Indeclinable, or rarely:

Declension of adagio
singular plural
nominative adagio adagia
genitive adagia adagiów
dative adagiu adagiom
accusative adagio adagia
instrumental adagiem adagiami
locative adagiu adagiach
vocative adagio adagio

Adjective[edit]

adagio (not comparable, no derived adverb)

  1. (music) adagio (describing a passage having this mark)

Adverb[edit]

adagio (not comparable)

  1. (music) adagio (played rather slowly)
