English[edit]

Adjective[edit]

Sem

  1. Abbreviation of Semitic.

Anagrams[edit]

  • EMS, EMs, Ems, MES, MEs, MSE, SME, ems, mes, mes-

Azerbaijani[edit]

Proper noun[edit]

Sem

  1. A transliteration of the English male given name Sam.

Dutch[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Ultimately from Hebrew שֵׁם (shem).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /sɛm/
  • Hyphenation: Sem
  • Rhymes: -ɛm

Proper noun[edit]

Sem m

  1. (biblical) Shem (a son of Noah)
  2. a male given name

Related terms[edit]

  • Semiet
  • Semitisch

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Hebrew שֵׁם (shem).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈsɛm/
  • Rhymes: -ɛm
  • Hyphenation: Sèm

Proper noun[edit]

Sem m

  1. (biblical) Shem (a son of Noah)

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Sēmus

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Σήμ (Sḗm).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /seːm/, [s̠eːm]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /sem/, [sɛm]

Proper noun[edit]

Sēm m sg (indeclinable)

  1. a male given name from Hebrew, variant of Sēmus

Declension[edit]

Indeclinable noun, singular only.

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈsem/ [ˈsẽm]
  • Rhymes: -em
  • Syllabification: Sem

Proper noun[edit]

Sem m

  1. (biblical) Shem (a son of Noah)
    • 1602, La Santa Biblia (antigua versión de Casiodoro de Reina), Génesis 9:18:
      Y los hijos de Noé que salieron del arca fueron Sem, Châm y Japhet: y Châm es el padre de Canaán.
      And the sons of Noah, that went forth of the ark, were Shem, and Ham, and Japheth: and Ham is the father of Canaan.
      (KJV)
