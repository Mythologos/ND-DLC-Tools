Determiner[edit]

suus (feminine sua, neuter suum); first/second-declension determiner

  1. (possessive, reflexive) his, her, hers, its (own)
    • 405 CE, Jerome, Vulgate Lucas.9.16:
      Acceptis autem quinque panibus et duobus piscibus respexit in caelum et benedixit illis et fregit et distribuit discipulis suis ut ponerent ante turbas.
      Then [Jesus] took the five loaves and the two fishes, and looking up to heaven, he blessed them, and broke, and gave to his disciples to set before the multitude.
  2. (possessive, reflexive) their, theirs

Usage notes[edit]

The choice of gender is determined by the noun possessed, and not by the gender of the person who possesses the object. Used by both singular and plural possessors.
