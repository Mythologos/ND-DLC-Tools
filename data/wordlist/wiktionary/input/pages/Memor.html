Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

  • Some refer it to some reduplicated form of Proto-Indo-European *(s)mer- (“to think, remember”). Similar to Old English mimor.
  • Some refer it to Proto-Italic *memnos, Proto-Indo-European *me-mn-os-, a reduplicated form of *men- (“to think”), although the declension was changed from a sibilant stem to a rhotic stem.[1]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈme.mor/, [ˈmɛmɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈme.mor/, [ˈmɛːmor]

Adjective[edit]

memor (genitive memoris, comparative memorior, superlative memorissimus, adverb memoriter); third-declension one-termination adjective (non-i-stem)

  1. mindful, remembering (+ genitive)
    alicuius/alicuius rei memor ― remember someone/something
  2. heedful, recalling, suggesting
  3. that has a good memory
  4. prudent, provident, careful, cautious
