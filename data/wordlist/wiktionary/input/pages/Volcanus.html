English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin Volcanus.

Proper noun[edit]

Volcanus

  1. (Roman mythology) Synonym of Vulcan, the Roman god of fire and metalwork.

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /u̯olˈkaː.nus/, [u̯ɔɫ̪ˈkäːnʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /volˈka.nus/, [volˈkäːnus]

Proper noun[edit]

Volcānus m sg (genitive Volcānī); second declension

  1. (Roman mythology) Alternative form of Vulcanus, the Roman god of fire and metalwork

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun, singular only.

Case Singular
Nominative Volcānus
Genitive Volcānī
Dative Volcānō
Accusative Volcānum
