Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Likely dux, ducis (“guide”) +‎ -culus (diminutive ending). Attested in the sixth century.[1]

Noun[edit]

ducīculus m (genitive ducīculī); second declension (Late Latin)

  1. spigot

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
