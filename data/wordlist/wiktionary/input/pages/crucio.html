Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From crux (“cross”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkru.ki.oː/, [ˈkrʊkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkru.t͡ʃi.o/, [ˈkruːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

cruciō (present infinitive cruciāre, perfect active cruciāvī, supine cruciātum); first conjugation

  1. to crucify
  2. to torture
    Synonym: verberō
