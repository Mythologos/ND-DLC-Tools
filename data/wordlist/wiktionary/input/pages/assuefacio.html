Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • adsuēfaciō

Etymology[edit]

From assuēscō +‎ faciō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /as.su.eːˈfa.ki.oː/, [äs̠ːueːˈfäkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /as.su.eˈfa.t͡ʃi.o/, [äsːueˈfäːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

assuēfaciō (present infinitive assuēfacere, perfect active assuēfēcī, supine assuēfactum); third conjugation iō-variant, irregular passive voice

  1. (with ablative) to accustom, habituate, inure to
    Synonyms: assoleō, soleō, adsuēscō, cōnsuēscō, cōnsuēfaciō
    assuefacere aliqua re/ alicui rei/ad rem ― get used to
