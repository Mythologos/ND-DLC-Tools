Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Γέλα (Géla).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒɛ.la/
  • Rhymes: -ɛla
  • Hyphenation: Gè‧la

Proper noun[edit]

Gela f

  1. A town in Caltanissetta, Sicily, Italy

Derived terms[edit]

  • gelese

Anagrams[edit]

  • Lega, gale, lega

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈɡe.la/, [ˈɡɛɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒe.la/, [ˈd͡ʒɛːlä]

Etymology 1[edit]

From Ancient Greek Γέλα (Géla).

Proper noun[edit]

Gela f sg (genitive Gelae); first declension

  1. One of the most important cities of Sicily, situated on the southern coast of the island, now still called Gela

Etymology 2[edit]

From Ancient Greek Γέλας (Gélas).

Proper noun[edit]

Gela m sg (genitive Gelae); first declension

  1. A river in Sicily, also called Gelas

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, with locative, singular only.
