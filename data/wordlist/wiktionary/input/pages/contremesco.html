Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ tremēscō (“shake, tremble at”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kon.treˈmeːs.koː/, [kɔn̪t̪rɛˈmeːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kon.treˈmes.ko/, [kon̪t̪reˈmɛsko]

Verb[edit]

contremēscō (present infinitive contremēscere, perfect active contremuī); third conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. (Medieval Latin) Alternative form of contremīscō
