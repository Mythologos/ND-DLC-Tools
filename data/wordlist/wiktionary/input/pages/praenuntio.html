Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From prae- +‎ nūntiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /prae̯ˈnuːn.ti.oː/, [präe̯ˈnuːn̪t̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /preˈnun.t͡si.o/, [preˈnunt̪͡s̪io]

Verb[edit]

praenūntiō (present infinitive praenūntiāre, perfect active praenūntiāvī, supine praenūntiātum); first conjugation

  1. to announce beforehand
  2. to foretell or predict
    Synonym: ōminor
