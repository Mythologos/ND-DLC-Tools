Basque[edit]

Etymology[edit]

(This etymology is missing or incomplete. Please add to it, or discuss it at the Etymology scriptorium.)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)
  • IPA(key): /ito/ [i.t̪o]
  • Rhymes: -ito
  • Hyphenation: i‧to

Verb[edit]

ito da/du (imperfect participle itotzen, future participle itoko, short form ito, verbal noun itotze)

  1. to choke, suffocate
  2. to drown

  • "ito" in Euskaltzaindiaren Hiztegia [Dictionary of the Basque Academy], euskaltzaindia.eus
  • “ito” in Orotariko Euskal Hiztegia [General Basque Dictionary], euskaltzaindia.eus

Bikol Central[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • 'to
  • idto, 'dto

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Proto-Austronesian *iCu (“that”) (cf. Yami uitu, Tagalog ito).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ʔiˈto/, [ʔiˈto]
  • Hyphenation: i‧to

Pronoun[edit]

itó (Basahan spelling ᜁᜆᜓ)

  1. it; that, those (near the person spoken to, but away from the speaker)
    Coordinate terms: ini, iyan

Derived terms[edit]

  • kaito

Ido[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • to

Etymology[edit]

From ita +‎ -o.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈi.to/, /ˈi.tɔ/

Pronoun[edit]

ito (plural iti)

  1. (demonstrative pronoun) that (thing)
    Yes, ma me kredas ke ito esas plu bona. ― Yes, but I think that that (thing) is better.

Related terms[edit]

  • ita (“that (person)”)
  • iti (“that (plural)”)
  • pro ito (“therefore”)

See also[edit]

  • ibe (“there”)
  • lore (“then”)
  • tala (“such kind of”)
    • tale (“thus”)
  • tanta (“so much”)

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

itus, perfect passive participle of eō (“to go”) +‎ -tō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈi.toː/, [ˈɪt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈi.to/, [ˈiːt̪o]

Verb[edit]

itō (present infinitive itāre, perfect active itāvī); first conjugation, no supine stem

  1. (intransitive) to keep going (to...); to continually or habitually go

Tagalog[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • 'to — clipping, colloquial

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Austronesian *iCu (“that”). Compare Bikol Central ito (“that”), Malagasy ito, Yami uitu, Malay itu (“that”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Hyphenation: i‧to
  • IPA(key): /ʔiˈto/, [ʔɪˈto]
  • IPA(key): /ʔeˈto/, [ʔɛˈto] (colloquial)
  • IPA(key): /ˈto/, [ˈto] (colloquial)

Adjective[edit]

itó (Baybayin spelling ᜁᜆᜓ)

  1. this (near the speaker and the listener)
  2. this (near the speaker)
    Synonyms: (dialectal) ari, (dialectal) ire, (dialectal) ere

Derived terms[edit]

  • dito
  • ganito
  • itech
  • itey
  • naito
  • nito
 

Pronoun[edit]

itó (Baybayin spelling ᜁᜆᜓ)

  1. this (near the speaker and the listener)
  2. this (near the speaker)
    Synonyms: (dialectal) ari, (dialectal) ire, (dialectal) ere
