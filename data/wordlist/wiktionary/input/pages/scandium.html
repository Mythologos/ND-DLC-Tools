English Wikipedia has an article on:
scandium
Wikipedia
Chemical element

Previous: calcium (Ca)

Etymology[edit]

From Scandia +‎ -ium.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: skăn'dēəm, IPA(key): /ˈskændi.əm/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Noun[edit]

scandium (uncountable)

  2. A metallic chemical element, atomic number 21, obtained from some uranium ores; it is a transition element.

Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
scandium
Wikipedia la
Chemical element
Sc
Previous: calcium (Ca)
Next: titanium (Ti)

Etymology[edit]

Coined in 1869 by chemist Lars Fredrik Nilson, derived from Scandia (“Scandinavia”) +‎ -ium (chemical element suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈskan.di.um/, [ˈs̠kän̪d̪iʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈskan.di.um/, [ˈskän̪d̪ium]

Noun[edit]

scandium n (genitive scandiī); second declension

  1. (New Latin) scandium

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
