Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin casella.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [kəˈze.ʎə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [kaˈze.ʎa]

Noun[edit]

casella f (plural caselles)

  1. compartment
    Synonym: compartiment
  2. (board games) square on a game board

Derived terms[edit]

  • encasellar

Further reading[edit]

  • “casella” in Diccionari de la llengua catalana, segona edició, Institut d’Estudis Catalans.
  • “casella”, in Gran Diccionari de la Llengua Catalana, Grup Enciclopèdia Catalana, 2024
  • “casella” in Diccionari normatiu valencià, Acadèmia Valenciana de la Llengua.
  • “casella” in Diccionari català-valencià-balear, Antoni Maria Alcover and Francesc de Borja Moll, 1962.

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin casella.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /kaˈzɛl.la/, (traditional) /kaˈsɛl.la/[1]
  • Rhymes: -ɛlla
  • Hyphenation: ca‧sèl‧la

Noun[edit]

casella f (plural caselle)

  1. box (small area on a document for a tick mark)
  2. square (of a chess board)
  3. pigeonhole

Derived terms[edit]

  • casella di spunta
  • casella postale

References[edit]

  1. ^ casella in Luciano Canepari, Dizionario di Pronuncia Italiana (DiPI)

Further reading[edit]

  • casella in Treccani.it – Vocabolario Treccani on line, Istituto dell'Enciclopedia Italiana

Anagrams[edit]

  • ascella

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From casula (“hut, small cottage”) +‎ -la (diminutive suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kaˈsel.la/, [käˈs̠ɛlːʲä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kaˈsel.la/, [käˈs̬ɛlːä]

Noun[edit]

casella f (genitive casellae); first declension

  1. Diminutive of casa
    1. hut, small cottage
    2. small farm

Declension[edit]
