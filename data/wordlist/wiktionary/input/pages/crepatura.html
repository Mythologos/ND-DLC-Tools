Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Late Latin crepātūra, from Latin crepō.

Noun[edit]

crepatura f (plural crepature)

  1. crack, crevice

Related terms[edit]

  • crepare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From crepō (“crack, creak”) +‎ -tūra.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kre.paːˈtuː.ra/, [krɛpäːˈt̪uːrä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kre.paˈtu.ra/, [krepäˈt̪uːrä]

Noun[edit]

crepātūra f (genitive crepātūrae); first declension

  1. (Late Latin) crack, fissure

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.
