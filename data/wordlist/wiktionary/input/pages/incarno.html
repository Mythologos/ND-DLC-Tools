Anagrams[edit]

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ carō (“flesh”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈkar.noː/, [ɪŋˈkärnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈkar.no/, [iŋˈkärno]

Verb[edit]

incarnō (present infinitive incarnāre, perfect active incarnāvī, supine incarnātum); first conjugation

  1. (Ecclesiastical Latin, Late Latin) to make or become incarnate; to make into flesh
