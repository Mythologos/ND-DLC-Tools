Proper noun[edit]

Africa (countable and uncountable, plural Africas)

  2. The continent that is south of Europe, east of the Atlantic Ocean, west of the Indian Ocean and north of Antarctica.
    the Maghreb and sub-Saharan Africa
    Synonym: (sometimes offensive, dated, informal) Dark Continent
  3. (nonstandard, proscribed) Sub-Saharan Africa, contrasted with the Maghreb.
    • 2021 June 10, Abdelmajid Hannoum, The Invention of the Maghreb: Between Africa and the Middle East, Cambridge University Press, ‚ÜíISBN, page 205:
      The Sahara stood as an important marker between the Maghreb and Africa, not only in modern times but in times immemorial.
  4. (historical) A province of the Roman Empire containing what is now modern Tunisia and portions of Libya.
  5. A surname.
