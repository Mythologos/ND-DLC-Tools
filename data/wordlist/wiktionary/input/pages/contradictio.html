Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From contrādīcō +‎ -tiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kon.traːˈdik.ti.oː/, [kɔn̪t̪räːˈd̪ɪkt̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kon.traˈdik.t͡si.o/, [kon̪t̪räˈd̪ikt̪͡s̪io]

Noun[edit]

contrādictiō f (genitive contrādictiōnis); third declension

  1. The act of contradicting.
  2. A reply, answer, objection, counterargument, contradiction; opposition.
    Ex contradictione quodlibet.[1]
    From a contradiction anything [follows]: ¬ C → ( C → Q ) {\displaystyle \neg C\rightarrow (C\rightarrow Q)} .
