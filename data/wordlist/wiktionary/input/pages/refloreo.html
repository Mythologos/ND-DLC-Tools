Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

from re- +‎ floreo

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reˈfloː.re.oː/, [rɛˈfɫ̪oːreoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈflo.re.o/, [reˈflɔːreo]

Verb[edit]

reflōreō (present infinitive reflōrēre, perfect active reflōruī); second conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to bloom again
  2. to flourish or prosper again
