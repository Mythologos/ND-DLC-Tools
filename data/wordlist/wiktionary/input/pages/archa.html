Czech[edit]

Czech Wikipedia has an article on:
archa
Wikipedia cs

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin arca.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈarxa]

Noun[edit]

archa f

  1. ark (the ship built by Noah)

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

A medieval respelling of arca

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈar.ka/, [ˈärkä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈar.ka/, [ˈärkä]

Noun[edit]

archa f (genitive archae); first declension

  1. chest, box, coffer, safe (safe place for storing items, or anything of a similar shape)

Old Polish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin arca.[1][2] First attested in the 15th century.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (10th–15th CE) /arxa/
  • IPA(key): (15th CE) /arxa/

Noun[edit]

archa f

  1. (religion) arch (container)
    • 1930 [Fifteenth century], “II Par”, in Ludwik Bernacki, editor, Biblia królowej Zofii (Biblia szaroszpatacka)‎[1], 8, 11:
      Archa bosza bila wiszla do nyego (ingressa est in eam arca domini)
      [Archa boża była wyszła do niego (ingressa est in eam arca domini)]

Descendants[edit]

  • Polish: arka
