Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin necessārius.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ne.t͡ʃesˈsa.rjo/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -arjo
  • Hyphenation: ne‧ces‧sà‧rio

Adjective[edit]

necessario (feminine necessaria, masculine plural necessari, feminine plural necessarie)

  1. necessary
  2. indispensable
  3. required
    Synonym: richiesto
  4. inevitable
    Synonym: inevitabile

Synonyms[edit]

  • cruciale
  • indispensabile
  • necesse (literary)
  • vitale

Antonyms[edit]

  • superfluo

Derived terms[edit]

  • necessariamente

Related terms[edit]

  • necessità
  • necessitare

Noun[edit]

necessario m (plural necessari)

  1. necessary, what is necessary
  2. the essentials
  3. materials

Synonyms[edit]

  • necesse (literary)

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ne.kesˈsaː.ri.oː/, [nɛkɛs̠ˈs̠äːrioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ne.t͡ʃesˈsa.ri.o/, [net͡ʃesˈsäːrio]

Etymology 1[edit]

Adverb[edit]

necessāriō (comparative necessāriius, superlative necessāriissimē)

  1. Alternative form of necessāriē

Etymology 2[edit]

Adjective[edit]

necessāriō

  1. dative/ablative masculine/neuter singular of necessārius

References[edit]

  • “necessario”, in Charlton T. Lewis and Charles Short (1879) A Latin Dictionary, Oxford: Clarendon Press
  • “necessario”, in Charlton T. Lewis (1891) An Elementary Latin Dictionary, New York: Harper & Brothers
  • necessario in Gaffiot, Félix (1934) Dictionnaire illustré latin-français, Hachette.
