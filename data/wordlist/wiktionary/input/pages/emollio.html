Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From molliō, from mollis.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /eːˈmol.li.oː/, [eːˈmɔlːʲioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /eˈmol.li.o/, [eˈmɔlːio]

Verb[edit]

ēmolliō (present infinitive ēmollīre, perfect active ēmollīvī or ēmolliī, supine ēmollītum); fourth conjugation

  1. to soften
  2. to enervate
  3. (figuratively) to (take) shelter behind
