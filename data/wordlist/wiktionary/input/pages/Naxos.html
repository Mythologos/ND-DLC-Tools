English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Νάξος (Náxos).

Proper noun[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Naxos
Wikipedia

Naxos

  1. An island in the central Cyclades
  2. A town in Greece located on an homonymous island.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Νάξος (Náxos).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈnak.sos/, [ˈnäks̠ɔs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈnak.sos/, [ˈnäksos]

Proper noun[edit]

Naxos f sg (genitive Naxī); second declension

  1. The largest island of the Cyclades

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (Greek-type), singular only.

Case Singular
Nominative Naxos
Genitive Naxī
Dative Naxō
Accusative Naxon
Ablative Naxō
Vocative Naxe
