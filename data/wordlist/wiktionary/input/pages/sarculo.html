Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From sarculum (“hoe”, noun) +‎ -ō (verb-forming suffix). Attested from ca. 400 CE.[1]

Verb[edit]

sarculō (present infinitive sarculāre, perfect active sarculāvī, supine sarculātum); first conjugation (Late Latin)

  1. to hoe, weed
