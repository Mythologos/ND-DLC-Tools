Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • subjugō

Etymology[edit]

sub- +‎ iugō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsub.i̯u.ɡoː/, [ˈs̠ʊbi̯ʊɡoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsub.ju.ɡo/, [ˈsubjuɡo]

Verb[edit]

subiugō (present infinitive subiugāre, perfect active subiugāvī, supine subiugātum); first conjugation

  1. to subjugate, make subject (bring under the yoke)
    Synonyms: subigō, subiciō, obnoxiō
