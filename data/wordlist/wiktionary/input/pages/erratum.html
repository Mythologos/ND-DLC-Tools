English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin errātum, neuter of errātus (“mistaken”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio (US)(file)
English Wikipedia has an article on:
erratum
Wikipedia
  • (UK) IPA(key): /ɛˈɹɑːtəm/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • (US) IPA(key): /ɛˈɹɑtəm/
  • Rhymes: -ɑːtəm

Noun[edit]

erratum (plural errata)

  1. An error, especially one in a printed work.
    Synonym: corrigendum

Translations[edit]

corrigendum — see corrigendum

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /erˈraː.tum/, [ɛrˈräːt̪ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /erˈra.tum/, [erˈräːt̪um]

Verb[edit]

errātum

  1. accusative supine of errō

Participle[edit]

errātum

  1. inflection of errātus:
    1. nominative/accusative/vocative neuter singular
    2. accusative masculine singular

Noun[edit]

errātum n (genitive errātī); second declension

  1. error, mistake, fault

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
