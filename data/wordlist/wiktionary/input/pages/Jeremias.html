English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

New Testament name for the prophet Jeremiah.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˌd͡ʒɛɹəˈmaɪəs/

Proper noun[edit]

Jeremias

  1. (biblical) New Testament name for the prophet Jeremiah.
    • 1611, The Holy Bible, […] (King James Version), London: […] Robert Barker, […], →OCLC, Matthew 16:14:
      Some say that thou art John the Baptist; some, Elias; and others, Jeremias or one of the prophets.
  2. A male given name from Hebrew, more common in the forms Jeremy and Jeremiah.

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Hiē̆rē̆mī̆ās, Īē̆remīās

Etymology[edit]

From the Ancient Greek Ἰερεμίας (Ieremías), from the Biblical Hebrew יִרְמְיָה.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /i̯eː.reːˈmiː.aːs/, [i̯eːreːˈmiːäːs̠] or IPA(key): /i̯eːˈre.mi.aːs/, [i̯eːˈrɛmiäːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /je.reˈmi.as/, [jereˈmiːäs] or IPA(key): /jeˈre.mi.as/, [jeˈrɛːmiäs]

Proper noun[edit]

Jērē̆mī̆ās m sg (genitive Jērē̆mī̆ae); first declension

  1. a male given name from Hebrew — famously held by:
    1. (biblical) Jeremiah (major prophet of the Hebrew Bible)
  2. (biblical, more fully “Liber Jeremiae”) (Book of) Jeremiah (book of the Old Testament)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun (masculine Greek-type with nominative singular in -ās), singular only.
