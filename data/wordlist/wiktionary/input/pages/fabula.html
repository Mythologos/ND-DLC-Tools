English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Latin fabula (“story”). Doublet of fable.

Noun[edit]

fabula (plural fabulae)

  1. (narratology) A series of events forming the basis of a story or narrative.

French[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)
  • Homophones: fabulas, fabulât

Verb[edit]

fabula

  1. third-person singular past historic of fabuler

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin fābula. Doublet of favola, fiaba, and fola.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfa.bu.la/
  • Rhymes: -abula
  • Hyphenation: fà‧bu‧la

Noun[edit]

fabula f (plural fabulae)

  1. (literature, film studies) fabula

Related terms[edit]

  • favola

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From for, fā- (“speak, say”) +‎ -bula (instrument noun suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈfaː.bu.la/, [ˈfäːbʊɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈfa.bu.la/, [ˈfäːbulä]

Noun[edit]

fābula f (genitive fābulae); first declension

  1. discourse, narrative
  2. a fable, tale, story
  3. a poem, play
  4. concern, matter
  5. romance
