Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ hērēditō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.heːˈreː.di.toː/, [ɪn(ɦ)eːˈreːd̪ɪt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /i.neˈre.di.to/, [ineˈrɛːd̪it̪o]

Verb[edit]

inhērēditō (present infinitive inhērēditāre, perfect active inhērēditāvī, supine inhērēditātum); first conjugation

  1. (Late Latin) to appoint an heir
