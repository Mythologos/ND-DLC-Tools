Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From sēnsus (“perception, feeling”) + faciō (“do, make”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /senˈsi.fi.koː/, [s̠ẽːˈs̠ɪfɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /senˈsi.fi.ko/, [senˈsiːfiko]

Verb[edit]

sēnsificō (present infinitive sēnsificāre, perfect active sēnsificāvī, supine sēnsificātum); first conjugation

  1. to make sensible, endow with sensation
