Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From circum- +‎ mūniō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈmuː.ni.oː/, [kɪrkʊmˈmuːnioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈmu.ni.o/, [t͡ʃirkumˈmuːnio]

Verb[edit]

circummūniō (present infinitive circummūnīre, perfect active circummūnīvī, supine circummūnītum); fourth conjugation

  1. to wall up or around
  2. to hem in
  3. to fortify or secure
