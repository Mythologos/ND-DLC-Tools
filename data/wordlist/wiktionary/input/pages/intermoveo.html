Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From inter- +‎ moveo.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈmo.u̯e.oː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈmou̯eoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈmo.ve.o/, [in̪t̪erˈmɔːveo]

Verb[edit]

intermoveō (present infinitive intermovēre, perfect active intermōvī, supine intermōtum); second conjugation

  1. to move or pass between
