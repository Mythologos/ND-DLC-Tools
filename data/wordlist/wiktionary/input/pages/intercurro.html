Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From inter- +‎ curro.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈkur.roː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈkʊrːoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈkur.ro/, [in̪t̪erˈkurːo]

Verb[edit]

intercurrō (present infinitive intercurrere, perfect active intercurrī or intercucurrī, supine intercursum); third conjugation

  1. to run between, or along with
  2. to mingle with
