Catalan[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central) [əˈβi.ə]
  • IPA(key): (Balearic) [əˈvi.ə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [aˈvi.a]

Verb[edit]

avia

  1. inflection of aviar:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Esperanto[edit]

Etymology[edit]

avio +‎ -a

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [aˈvia]
  • Rhymes: -ia
  • Hyphenation: a‧vi‧a

Adjective[edit]

avia (accusative singular avian, plural aviaj, accusative plural aviajn)

  1. Relating to the science of aircraft; aeronautical, aviational.

Usage notes[edit]

According to national language customs, the adjective aera is often used in the sense of avia.

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Feminine counterpart to avus (“grandfather”) formed with *-ih₂ ~ *-yeh₂.[1]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • ava (Late Latin)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈa.u̯i.a/, [ˈäu̯iä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈa.vi.a/, [ˈäːviä]

Noun[edit]

avia f (genitive aviae); first declension

  1. grandmother (mother of someone’s parent)
