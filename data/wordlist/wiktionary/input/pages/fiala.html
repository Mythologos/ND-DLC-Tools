Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin phiala.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfja.la/, /fiˈa.la/[1]
  • Rhymes: -ala
  • Hyphenation: fià‧la, fi‧à‧la

Noun[edit]

fiala f (plural fiale)

  1. phial

Descendants[edit]

  • → German: Fiale

References[edit]

  1. ^ fiala in Luciano Canepari, Dizionario di Pronuncia Italiana (DiPI)

Further reading[edit]

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈfi.a.la/, [ˈfiäɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈfi.a.la/, [ˈfiːälä]

Noun[edit]

fiala f (genitive fialae); first declension

  1. drinking plate

Declension[edit]

Polish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfja.la/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -ala
  • Syllabification: fia‧la

Noun[edit]

fiala f

  1. (architecture) pinnacle
    Synonyms: pinakiel, sterczyna

Rohingya[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Noun[edit]

fiala

  1. cup

Verb[edit]
