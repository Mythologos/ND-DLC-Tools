Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

vītis (“vine, grape vine”) +‎ folius (“leaf”)

Adjective[edit]

vītifolius (feminine vītifolia, neuter vītifolium); first/second-declension adjective

  1. (New Latin) a taxonomic epithet meaning "having leaves like that of a grape vine"

Usage notes[edit]

  • Used exclusively as a taxonomic epithet and thus normally in the nominative singular; other inflections may be theoretical or rarely found.
