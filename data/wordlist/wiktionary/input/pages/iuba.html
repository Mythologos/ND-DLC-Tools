Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈju.ba/
  • Rhymes: -uba
  • Hyphenation: iù‧ba

Noun[edit]

iuba f (plural iube)

  1. (literary, rare) Alternative form of giubba (“mane”)

Anagrams[edit]

  • buia

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Apparently from the same Proto-Indo-European source as iubeō as in "moving", "billowing".

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈi̯u.ba/, [ˈi̯ʊbä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈju.ba/, [ˈjuːbä]

Noun[edit]

iuba f (genitive iubae); first declension

  1. mane; the flowing hair on the neck of an animal
  2. hair of the head
  3. a crest on a helmet; tuft; comb of a rooster
