Bambara[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From den (“child”) +‎ so (“house”).

Noun[edit]

denso

  1. womb, uterus
    Synonym: dɛbɛ

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Rhymes: -enso

Noun[edit]

denso (slang)

    Synonyms: see Thesaurus:juoppo

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈden.soː/, [ˈd̪ẽːs̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈden.so/, [ˈd̪ɛnso]

Etymology 1[edit]

From densus +‎ -ō.

Verb[edit]

dēnsō (present infinitive dēnsāre, perfect active dēnsāvī, supine dēnsātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to make thick, thicken, condense
  2. (transitive) to crowd together, press together
  3. (transitive, figuratively, of speech) to condense
