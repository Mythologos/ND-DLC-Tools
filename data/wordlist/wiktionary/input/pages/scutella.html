English[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Rhymes: -ɛlə

Noun[edit]

scutella

  1. plural of scutellum

Noun[edit]

scutella (plural scutellae)

  1. A scutellum.

References[edit]

  • “scutella”, in Webster’s Revised Unabridged Dictionary, Springfield, Mass.: G. & C. Merriam, 1913, →OCLC.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From scutra (“tray, dish, platter”) +‎ -la (diminutive suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /skuˈtel.la/, [s̠kʊˈt̪ɛlːʲä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /skuˈtel.la/, [skuˈt̪ɛlːä]

Noun[edit]

scutella f (genitive scutellae); first declension

  1. a small or shallow bowl, basin or dish
  2. a small salver or tray

Declension[edit]
