Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • aberceō

Etymology[edit]

From ab- +‎ arceō (“keep off”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /aˈbar.ke.oː/, [äˈbärkeoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /aˈbar.t͡ʃe.o/, [äˈbärt͡ʃeo]

Verb[edit]

abarceō (present infinitive abarcēre); second conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to keep away, ward off

Conjugation[edit]

  • Passive forms are attested post-Classically.
