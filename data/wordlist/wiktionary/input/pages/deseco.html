Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dē- +‎ secō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈdeː.se.koː/, [ˈd̪eːs̠ɛkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈde.se.ko/, [ˈd̪ɛːs̬eko]

Verb[edit]

dēsecō (present infinitive dēsecāre, perfect active dēsecuī, supine dēsectum); first conjugation

  1. to sever, cut off
  2. to cut, reap or mow (a crop)
    Synonym: metō
