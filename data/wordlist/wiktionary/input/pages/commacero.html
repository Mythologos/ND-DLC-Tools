Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ mācerō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /komˈmaː.ke.roː/, [kɔmˈmäːkɛroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /komˈma.t͡ʃe.ro/, [komˈmäːt͡ʃero]

Verb[edit]

commācerō (present infinitive commācerāre, perfect active commācerāvī, supine commācerātum); first conjugation

  1. to soften by steeping, macerate
    • c. 400 CE, Marcellus Empiricus, De Medicamentis 36
    • St. Ambrose, Expositio evangelii secundum Lucam 5.6
