Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /amˈplek.soː/, [ämˈpɫ̪ɛks̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /amˈplek.so/, [ämˈplɛkso]

Participle[edit]

amplexō

  1. dative/ablative masculine/neuter singular of amplexus

Verb[edit]

amplexō (present infinitive amplexāre, perfect active amplexāvī, supine amplexātum); first conjugation

  1. Alternative form of amplexor

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin amplexum, from amplexus (“hug”).

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ɐ̃ˈplɛk.su/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ɐ̃ˈplɛk.su/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /ɐ̃ˈplɛk.so/
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ɐ̃ˈplɛ.ksu/

Noun[edit]

amplexo m (plural amplexos)

  1. (formal, poetic) hug; embrace
    Synonym: abraço
  2. (zoology) amplexus (pseudocopulation in which a male grasps a female with his front legs)
