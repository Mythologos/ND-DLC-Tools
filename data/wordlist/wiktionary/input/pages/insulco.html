Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ sulcō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈsul.koː/, [ĩːˈs̠ʊɫ̪koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈsul.ko/, [inˈsulko]

Verb[edit]

īnsulcō (present infinitive īnsulcāre, perfect active īnsulcāvī, supine īnsulcātum); first conjugation

  1. to furrow (make furrows)
