Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

de- +‎ amō (“to love”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈde.a.moː/, [ˈd̪eämoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈde.a.mo/, [ˈd̪ɛːämo]

Verb[edit]

deamō (present infinitive deamāre, perfect active deamāvī, supine deamātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to be desperately in love with
  2. (transitive) to be very grateful to
