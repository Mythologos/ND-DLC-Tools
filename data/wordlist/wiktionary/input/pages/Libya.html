English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Libya
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

Via Latin Libya, from Ancient Greek Λιβύη (Libúē). English since the 16th century.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: lĭbʹē-ə, IPA(key): /ˈlɪbi.ə/
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • Hyphenation: Lib‧ya
  • Rhymes: -ɪbiə

Proper noun[edit]

Libya

  2. A country in North Africa. Capital: Tripoli.
  3. (historical) Africa; that is, the part of North Africa known in classical antiquity.

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Libua
  • Libyē

Spelled Libua by Plautus, Libya in Pliny, Virgil and Cicero. Juvenal has Libyē.

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Λιβύη (Libúē).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈli.by.a/, [ˈlʲɪbyä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈli.bi.a/, [ˈliːbiä]

Proper noun[edit]

Libya f sg (genitive Libyae); first declension

  1. Africa, i.e. the part of North Africa known in classical antiquity
  2. (New Latin) Libya (a country in North Africa)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, singular only.
