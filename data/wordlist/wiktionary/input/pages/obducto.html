Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From obdūcō +‎ -tō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /obˈduk.toː/, [ɔbˈd̪ʊkt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /obˈduk.to/, [obˈd̪ukt̪o]

Verb[edit]

obductō (present infinitive obductāre); first conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to lead or conduct in opposition or rivalry to another
