English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
orans
Wikipedia

Alternative forms[edit]

  • orant
  • oranta, orante

Etymology[edit]

Derived from Latin orans (“praying”).

Noun[edit]

orans (plural orantes)

  2. (art, Christianity) A figure in an attitude of prayer, usually standing, with the elbows close to the sides of the body and with the hands outstretched sideways, palms up.
  3. (Christianity) The posture or bodily attitude of such a figure.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Present active participle of ōrō (“speak, orate”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈoː.rans/, [ˈoːrä̃ːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈo.rans/, [ˈɔːräns]

Participle[edit]

ōrāns (genitive ōrantis); third-declension one-termination participle

  1. speaking, orating
  2. pleading, begging
  3. praying, entreating
