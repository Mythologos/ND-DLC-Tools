English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Italian secondo (“second”). Doublet of second.

Noun[edit]

secundo (plural secundos)

  1. (music) The secondary part of a duet.

Antonyms[edit]

  • primo

See also[edit]

  • secundo-primary
  • trigesimo-secundo

Anagrams[edit]

  • dounces

French[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /sə.ɡɔ̃.do/
  • Audio(file)

Adverb[edit]

secundo

  1. secondly
    Synonyms: deuxièmement, deuxio

Related terms[edit]

  • primo
  • tertio
  • quarto

Further reading[edit]

  • “secundo”, in Trésor de la langue française informatisé [Digitized Treasury of the French Language], 2012.

Interlingua[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Compare Spanish según.

Preposition[edit]

secundo

  1. according to

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /seˈkun.doː/, [s̠ɛˈkʊn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /seˈkun.do/, [seˈkun̪d̪o]

Numeral[edit]

secundō

  1. dative/ablative masculine/neuter singular of secundus

Verb[edit]

secundō (present infinitive secundāre, perfect active secundāvī, supine secundātum); first conjugation

  1. to adjust, adapt, accommodate
