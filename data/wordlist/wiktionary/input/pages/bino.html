English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

(This etymology is missing or incomplete. Please add to it, or discuss it at the Etymology scriptorium.)

Noun[edit]

bino (plural binos)

  1. (physics) The superpartner of the gauge boson corresponding to weak hypercharge.

Translations[edit]

Hiligaynon[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From Spanish vino.

Noun[edit]

bíno

  1. wine

Etymology 2[edit]

Unknown.

binó

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin bīnus (“double, twofold”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈbi.no/
  • Rhymes: -ino
  • Hyphenation: bì‧no

Adjective[edit]

bino (feminine bina, masculine plural bini, feminine plural bine)

  1. (literary) double
    Synonyms: doppio, gemello

Related terms[edit]

  • binario

Anagrams[edit]

  • boni

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbiː.noː/, [ˈbiːnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbi.no/, [ˈbiːno]

Etymology 1[edit]

bīnus (“occurring twice”) +‎ -ō

Verb[edit]

bīnō (present infinitive bīnāre, perfect active bīnāvī, supine bīnātum); first conjugation

  1. (Medieval Latin) to repeat; to replough
  2. (Christianity) to binate, to celebrate Mass twice in one day
