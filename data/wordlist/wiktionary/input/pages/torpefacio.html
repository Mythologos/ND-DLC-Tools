Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From torpeō +‎ faciō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /tor.peˈfa.ki.oː/, [t̪ɔrpɛˈfäkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /tor.peˈfa.t͡ʃi.o/, [t̪orpeˈfäːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

torpefaciō (present infinitive torpefacere, perfect active torpefēcī, supine torpefactum); third conjugation iō-variant, irregular passive voice

  1. to benumb, to make torpid
    Synonym: torpōrō
