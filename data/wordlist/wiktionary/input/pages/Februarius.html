Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From februum (“means of purification, an offering”), via the purification ritual Februa held on February 15 in the old Roman calendar.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /fe.bruˈaː.ri.us/, [fɛbruˈäːriʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /fe.bruˈa.ri.us/, [februˈäːrius]

Adjective[edit]

februārius (feminine februāria, neuter februārium); first/second-declension adjective

  1. Alternative form of Februārius
