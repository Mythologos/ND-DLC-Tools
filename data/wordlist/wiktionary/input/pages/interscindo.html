Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From inter- +‎ scindō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈskin.doː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈs̠kɪn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈʃin.do/, [in̪t̪erˈʃin̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

interscindō (present infinitive interscindere, perfect active interscidī, supine interscissum); third conjugation

  1. to tear asunder or breakdown
  2. to separate or interrupt
