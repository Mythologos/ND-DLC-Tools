Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • robō, rubō, rupō

Etymology[edit]

From Frankish *raubōn. Attested in the Lex Salica.[1]

Verb[edit]

raubō (present infinitive raubāre, perfect active raubāvī, supine raubātum); first conjugation (Late Latin)

  1. to rob, plunder
