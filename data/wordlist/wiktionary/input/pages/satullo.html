Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From satullus;[1] may be seen as a "diminutive" form of saturō.

Verb[edit]

satullō (present infinitive satullāre, perfect active satullāvī, supine satullātum); first conjugation

  1. to satisfy, satiate
