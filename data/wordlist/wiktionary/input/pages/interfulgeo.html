Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From inter- +‎ fulgeō (“shine”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.terˈful.ɡe.oː/, [ɪn̪t̪ɛrˈfʊɫ̪ɡeoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.terˈful.d͡ʒe.o/, [in̪t̪erˈful̠ʲd͡ʒeo]

Verb[edit]

interfulgeō (present infinitive interfulgēre, perfect active interfulsī); second conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to shine or glitter between or among
