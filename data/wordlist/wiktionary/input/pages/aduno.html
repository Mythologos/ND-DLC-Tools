Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /aˈdu.no/
  • Rhymes: -uno
  • Hyphenation: a‧dù‧no

Verb[edit]

aduno

  1. first-person singular present indicative of adunare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ad- +‎ ūnus (“one, single, sole”) +‎ -ō. Compare the similarly formed English atone.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /aˈduː.noː/, [äˈd̪uːnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /aˈdu.no/, [äˈd̪uːno]

Verb[edit]

adūnō (present infinitive adūnāre, perfect active adūnāvī, supine adūnātum); first conjugation

  1. to unite, make one

Welsh[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ad- +‎ uno (“to unite”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (North Wales) IPA(key): /aˈdɨ̞nɔ/
  • (South Wales) IPA(key): /aˈdiːnɔ/, /aˈdɪnɔ/

Verb[edit]

aduno (first-person singular present adunaf)

  1. to reunite, to rejoin
