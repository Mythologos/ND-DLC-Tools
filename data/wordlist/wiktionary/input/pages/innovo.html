Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ novō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈin.no.u̯oː/, [ˈɪnːou̯oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈin.no.vo/, [ˈinːovo]

Verb[edit]

innovō (present infinitive innovāre, perfect active innovāvī, supine innovātum); first conjugation

  1. to renew, restore
  2. to alter, innovate
  3. to return to
