Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsa.ti.oː/, [ˈs̠ät̪ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsat.t͡si.o/, [ˈsät̪ː͡s̪io]

Etymology 1[edit]

satis (“ample, sufficient”) +‎ -ō

Verb[edit]

satiō (present infinitive satiāre, perfect active satiāvī, supine satiātum); first conjugation

  1. to satisfy
  2. to sate, satiate
  3. to saturate, impregnate (fill to satiety)
  4. to glut, cloy

Etymology 2[edit]

From serō +‎ -tiō.

Noun[edit]

satiō f (genitive satiōnis); third declension

  1. sowing, planting
  2. sowing time
