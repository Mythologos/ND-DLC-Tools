Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

ex- +‎ mātūrēscō (“to ripen”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /eː.maː.tuːˈreːs.koː/, [eːmäːt̪uːˈreːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /e.ma.tuˈres.ko/, [emät̪uˈrɛsko]

Verb[edit]

ēmātūrēscō (present infinitive ēmātūrēscere); third conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to become quite ripe
  2. (figurative) to soften, be mitigated
