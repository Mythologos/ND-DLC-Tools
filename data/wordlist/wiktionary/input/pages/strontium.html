English[edit]

Chemical element
Sr
Previous: rubidium (Rb)
Next: yttrium (Y)

Etymology[edit]

Coined by British chemist Humphry Davy in 1808, after the earlier term strontianite, for the name of the Scottish town Strontian.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: strŏn'tēəm, IPA(key): /ˈstɹɒn.ti.əm/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Noun[edit]

strontium (usually uncountable, plural strontiums)

English Wikipedia has an article on:
strontium
Wikipedia
  1. The metallic chemical element (symbol Sr) with an atomic number of 38. It is a soft, reactive, silvery alkaline earth metal.
  2. (countable) A single atom of this element.
