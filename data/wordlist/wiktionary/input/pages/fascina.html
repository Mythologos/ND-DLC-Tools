English[edit]

Noun[edit]

fascina

  1. plural of fascinum

Catalan[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [fəˈsi.nə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [faˈsi.na]

Verb[edit]

fascina

  1. inflection of fascinar:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Esperanto[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)
  • IPA(key): [fasˈt͡sina]
  • Rhymes: -ina
  • Hyphenation: fas‧ci‧na

Adjective[edit]

fascina (accusative singular fascinan, plural fascinaj, accusative plural fascinajn)

  1. fascinating

French[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /fa.si.na/

Verb[edit]

fascina

  1. third-person singular past historic of fasciner

Anagrams[edit]

  • fianças

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin fascīna. Compare French and English fascine.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /faʃˈʃi.na/
  • Rhymes: -ina
  • Hyphenation: fa‧scì‧na

Noun[edit]

fascina f (plural fascine)

  1. faggot (bundle of sticks)
  2. reap (bundle of grain)

Anagrams[edit]

  • sfianca

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Diminutive of fascis (“bundle of wood”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /fasˈkiː.na/, [fäs̠ˈkiːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /faʃˈʃi.na/, [fäʃˈʃiːnä]

Noun[edit]

fascīna f (genitive fascīnae); first declension

  1. A bundle of sticks.

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Genitive fascīnae fascīnārum

Related terms[edit]
