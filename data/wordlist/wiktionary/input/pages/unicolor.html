English[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • unicolour (British spelling)

Etymology[edit]

uni- +‎ color

Adjective[edit]

unicolor (not comparable)

  1. (American spelling) Of one color; monochromatic.

Derived terms[edit]

  • unicolored

Translations[edit]

of one color — see monochrome

Anagrams[edit]

  • colour in, in colour

Galician[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • unicor

Etymology[edit]

From uni- +‎ color.

Adjective[edit]

unicolor m or f (plural unicolores)

  1. unicolor/unicolour, one-color/one-colour, monocolor/monocolour, monochromatic, monochrome
    Synonym: monocolor

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ūni- +‎ color.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /uːˈni.ko.lor/, [uːˈnɪkɔɫ̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /uˈni.ko.lor/, [uˈniːkolor]

Adjective[edit]

ūnicolor (genitive ūnicolōris); third-declension one-termination adjective

  1. Of one colour.
  2. All of the same colour.

Declension[edit]

Third-declension one-termination adjective.
