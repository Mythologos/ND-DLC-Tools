German[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin tornus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Noun[edit]

Turnus m (strong, genitive Turnus or Turnusses, plural Turnusse)

  1. rotation (regular change)

Declension[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtur.nus/, [ˈt̪ʊrnʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtur.nus/, [ˈt̪urnus]

Proper noun[edit]

Turnus m sg (genitive Turnī); second declension

  1. (Roman mythology) A king of the Rutuli killed by Aeneas

Declension[edit]
