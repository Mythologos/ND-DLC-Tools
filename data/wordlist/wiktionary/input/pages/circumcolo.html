Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From circum- (“circum-”) +‎ colō (“I inhabit”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kirˈkun.ko.loː/, [kɪrˈkʊŋkɔɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃirˈkun.ko.lo/, [t͡ʃirˈkuŋkolo]

Verb[edit]

circumcolō (present infinitive circumcolere, perfect active circumcoluī, supine circumcultum); third conjugation

  1. to inhabit, live around or near
