Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From tessella +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /tesˈsel.loː/, [t̪ɛs̠ˈs̠ɛlːʲoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /tesˈsel.lo/, [t̪esˈsɛlːo]

Verb[edit]

tessellō (present infinitive tessellāre, perfect active tessellāvī, supine tessellātum); first conjugation

  1. to tile, to tessellate, to lay mosaic on
