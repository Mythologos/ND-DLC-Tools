Italian[edit]

Verb[edit]

dica

  1. inflection of dire:
    1. first/second/third-person singular present subjunctive
    2. third-person singular imperative

Anagrams[edit]

  • -cida, Ciad, cadi, cadì

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek δῐ́κη (díkē, “custom; order; judgement”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈdi.ka/, [ˈd̪ɪkä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈdi.ka/, [ˈd̪iːkä]

Noun[edit]

dica f (genitive dicae); first declension

  1. (law) trial, lawsuit, prosecution
Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative dica dicae
Genitive dicae dicārum
Dative dicae dicīs
Accusative dicam dicās
Ablative dicā dicīs
Vocative dica dicae
Derived terms[edit]
  • dicam scribō

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈdi.kaː/, [ˈd̪ɪkäː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈdi.ka/, [ˈd̪iːkä]

Verb[edit]

dicā

  1. second-person singular present active imperative of dicō

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Originated in Brazilian Portuguese. Ultimately from indicar (“to indicate”).

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒi.kɐ/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒi.kɐ/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒi.ka/
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ˈdi.kɐ/

  • Rhymes: -ikɐ
  • Hyphenation: di‧ca
  • Audio(file)

Noun[edit]

dica f (plural dicas)

  1. tip (piece of helpful information)
