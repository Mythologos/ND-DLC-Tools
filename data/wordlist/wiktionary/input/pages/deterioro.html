Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dēterior (“worse”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deː.te.riˈoː.roː/, [d̪eːt̪ɛriˈoːroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /de.te.riˈo.ro/, [d̪et̪eriˈɔːro]

Verb[edit]

dēteriōrō (present infinitive dēteriōrāre, perfect active dēteriōrāvī, supine dēteriōrātum); first conjugation

  1. (Late Latin) to worsen, deteriorate
