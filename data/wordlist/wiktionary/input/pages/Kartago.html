Basque[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /kartaɡo/ [kar.t̪a.ɣ̞o]
  • Rhymes: -aɡo
  • Hyphenation: Kar‧ta‧go

Proper noun[edit]

Kartago inan

  1. Carthage (an ancient city in North Africa, in modern Tunisia)
  2. Carthage (an ancient civilisation in North Africa, centred on the city of Carthage)

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Karthāgō

Etymology[edit]

From Phoenician 𐤒𐤓𐤕-𐤇𐤃𐤔𐤕 (qrt-ḥdšt, “New City”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /karˈtaː.ɡoː/, [kärˈt̪äːɡoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /karˈta.ɡo/, [kärˈt̪äːɡo]

Proper noun[edit]

Kartāgō f sg (genitive Kartāginis); third declension

  1. Archaic spelling of Carthāgō.

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun, with locative, singular only.
