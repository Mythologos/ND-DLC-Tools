Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From per- +‎ cēnseō (“count, reckon”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /perˈken.se.oː/, [pɛrˈkẽːs̠eoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /perˈt͡ʃen.se.o/, [perˈt͡ʃɛnseo]

Verb[edit]

percēnseō (present infinitive percēnsēre, perfect active percēnsuī, supine percēnsum); second conjugation

  1. to count over, reckon up, enumerate, go through, go over
  2. to survey, view; review, examine
  3. to go or travel through or over
