Catalan[edit]

Verb[edit]

vindico

  1. first-person singular present indicative of vindicar

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From vindex (“defender, protector”), from dīcō (“say; declare, state”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈu̯in.di.koː/, [ˈu̯ɪn̪d̪ɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈvin.di.ko/, [ˈvin̪d̪iko]

Verb[edit]

vindicō (present infinitive vindicāre, perfect active vindicāvī, supine vindicātum); first conjugation

  1. to avenge, vindicate, claim, punish
    Synonyms: pūniō, ulciscor
  2. to liberate, deliver, spare, rescue
    Synonyms: līberō, eximō, absolvō, excipiō, exuō, servō, prīvō
    Antonyms: refrēnō, coerceō, saepiō, officiō, obstō, comprimō, impediō, arceō, supprimō
  3. to protect someone from. (coupled with a, ab, e, ex + ablative)
    Synonyms: salvō, tūtor, cū̆stōdiō, sospitō, teneō, adimō, prōtegō, tegō, dēfendō, sustineō, adsum, tueor, ēripiō, arceō, servō, prohibeō
    Antonyms: immineō, īnstō, obiectō
    • 27 BCE – 25 BCE, Titus Livius, Ab Urbe Condita I.4:
      Nec dii nec homines [...] ipsam [...] a crudelitate regia vindicant
      Nor gods nor men protect him from the royal cruelty
