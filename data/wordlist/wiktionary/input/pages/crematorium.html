English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
crematorium
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From New Latin crematorium.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /kɹɛməˈtɔːɹɪəm/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • (US) IPA(key): /kɹɛməˈtɔːɹɪəm/, /kɹiːməˈtɔːɹi.əm/

Noun[edit]

crematorium (plural crematoriums or crematoria)

  1. A place where the bodies of dead people are cremated.

Related terms[edit]

  • cremate
  • cremation
  • crematory

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

cremō +‎ -tōrium

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kre.maːˈtoː.ri.um/, [krɛmäːˈt̪oːriʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kre.maˈto.ri.um/, [kremäˈt̪ɔːrium]

Noun[edit]

cremātōrium n (genitive cremātōriī or cremātōrī); second declension

  1. (New Latin) crematorium
