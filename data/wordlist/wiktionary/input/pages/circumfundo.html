Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From circum- +‎ fundō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈfun.doː/, [kɪrkũːˈfʊn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈfun.do/, [t͡ʃirkumˈfun̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

circumfundō (present infinitive circumfundere, perfect active circumfūdī, supine circumfūsum); third conjugation

  1. to pour out or round; to surround with liquid
  2. to crowd around
  3. to overwhelm
