Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈmae̯s.toː/, [ˈmäe̯s̠t̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈmes.to/, [ˈmɛst̪o]

Adjective[edit]

maestō

  1. dative/ablative masculine/neuter singular of maestus

Etymology 2[edit]

From maestus (“sad”) +‎ -ō.

Verb[edit]

maestō (present infinitive maestāre, perfect active maestāvī, supine maestātum); first conjugation

  1. to make sad
  2. to grieve, afflict
