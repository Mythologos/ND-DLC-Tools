Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Ἄκραι (Ákrai).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈa.krae̯/, [ˈäkräe̯]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈa.kre/, [ˈäːkre]

Proper noun[edit]

Acrae f pl (genitive Acrārum); first declension

  1. A city of the interior of Sicily and colony of Syracusae, now Palazzolo Acreide

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, with locative, plural only.
