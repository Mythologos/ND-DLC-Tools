English[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Anglicization of Irish Síne and Scottish Gaelic Sìne. Doublet of Jane.

Proper noun[edit]

Sina

  1. A unisex given name from Scottish Gaelic.
Related terms[edit]
  • Sheena, Sinah

Etymology 2[edit]

From Hebrew.

Proper noun[edit]

Sina

  1. (biblical) (in some versions) Sinai
    • 1611, The Holy Bible, […] (King James Version), London: […] Robert Barker, […], →OCLC, Acts 7:30:
      And when forty years were expired, there appeared to him in the wilderness of mount Sina an angel of the Lord in a flame of fire in a bush.

Etymology 3[edit]

From Persian سینا (sinâ).

Proper noun[edit]

Sina

  1. A transliteration of the Persian male given name سینا (sinâ).
Related terms[edit]
  • Avicenna

Etymology 4[edit]

Proper noun[edit]

Sina

  1. Alternative form of Shina: a Kashmiri people and language.

Etymology 5[edit]

Proper noun[edit]

Sina

  1. Alternative form of Sinam surname of Meitei origin.

Anagrams[edit]

  • ANSI, ASIN, ISNA, Isan, Nias, Sain, Sani, Sian, anis, as in, nais, nasi, nasi', nias, sain

German[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Short form of names ending in -sina, such as Rosina or Teresina.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Proper noun[edit]

Sina

  1. a female given name

Etymology 1[edit]

From Ancient Greek Σῖναι (Sînai) of uncertain etymology, but probably from Sanskrit चीन (Cīna, “China”), possibly via Arabic صِين (Ṣīn, “China; the Chinese”) and usually held to derive from Old Chinese 秦 (*zin, “Qin”). See "Names of China" at Wikipedia.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsiː.na/, [ˈs̠iːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsi.na/, [ˈsiːnä]

Proper noun[edit]

Sīna f sg (genitive Sīnae); first declension

  1. China
Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, singular only.

Etymology 2[edit]

From Ancient Greek Σινάι (Sinái), itself from Biblical Hebrew סִינַי (Sináy).

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Sīnai

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈsiː.na/, [ˈs̠iːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈsi.na/, [ˈsiːnä]

Proper noun[edit]

Sīna ? sg (genitive Sīnae); first declension

  1. A peninsula in eastern Egypt, bordering Asia.
  2. A mountain in Sinai Peninsula; the location where Moses is said to have received the Ten Commandments from God.

First-declension noun, singular only.
