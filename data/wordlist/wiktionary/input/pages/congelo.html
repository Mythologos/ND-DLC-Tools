Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From con- +‎ gelō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkon.ɡe.loː/, [ˈkɔŋɡɛɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkon.d͡ʒe.lo/, [ˈkɔn̠ʲd͡ʒelo]

Verb[edit]

congelō (present infinitive congelāre, perfect active congelāvī, supine congelātum); first conjugation

  1. to freeze together, congeal
  2. (figurative, intransitive) to grow stiff
  3. to freeze, make stiff
