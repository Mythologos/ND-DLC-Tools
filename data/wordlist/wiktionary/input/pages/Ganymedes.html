English[edit]

Noun[edit]

Ganymedes

  1. plural of Ganymede

Anagrams[edit]

  • megadynes

Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek Γανυμήδης (Ganumḗdēs)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɡɑnymedes/, [ˈɡɑ̝nyˌme̞de̞s̠]
  • Rhymes: -edes
  • Syllabification(key): Ga‧ny‧me‧des

Proper noun[edit]

Ganymedes

  1. (Greek mythology) Ganymede
  2. (astronomy) Ganymede, the satellite of Jupiter.

Latin[edit]

Latin Wikipedia has an article on:
Ganymedes
Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

Ancient Greek Γανυμήδης (Ganumḗdēs). Doublet of Catamītus, whence catamītus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ɡa.nyˈmeː.deːs/, [ɡänʏˈmeːd̪eːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ɡa.niˈme.des/, [ɡäniˈmɛːd̪es]

Proper noun[edit]

Ganymēdēs m sg (genitive Ganymēdis); third declension

  1. (Greek mythology) Ganymede
  2. (New Latin) Ganymede (moon)

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun, singular only.
