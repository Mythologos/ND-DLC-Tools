Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From crispus (“curled, crimped”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkriːs.poː/, [ˈkriːs̠poː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkris.po/, [ˈkrispo]

Verb[edit]

crīspō (present infinitive crīspāre, perfect active crīspāvī, supine crīspātum); first conjugation

  1. to curl, crimp
  2. (figuratively) to make rough or variegated; strew or plant (with)
  3. (figuratively) to swing, brandish
