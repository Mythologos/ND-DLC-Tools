Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin flātus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfla.to/
  • Rhymes: -ato
  • Hyphenation: flà‧to

Noun[edit]

flato m (plural flati)

  1. flatus
  2. fart
  3. burp
  4. flatulence

See also[edit]

  • loffa
  • peto
  • scorreggia
  • vescia

Anagrams[edit]

  • Tolfa, folta

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Late Latin. Frequentative of flō through its past participle, flātus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈflaː.toː/, [ˈfɫ̪äːt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈfla.to/, [ˈfläːt̪o]

Verb[edit]

flātō (present infinitive flātāre, perfect active flātāvī, supine flātātum); first conjugation

  1. to blow

Portuguese[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfla.tu/

  • Rhymes: -atu
  • Hyphenation: fla‧to

Noun[edit]

flato m (plural flatos)

  1. (medicine) flatus (expulsion of gas through the anus)

Related terms[edit]

  • flatulência

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin flatus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈflato/ [ˈfla.t̪o]
  • Rhymes: -ato
  • Syllabification: fla‧to

Noun[edit]

flato m (plural flatos)

  1. flatulence, flatus (gas generated in the digestive tract)
    Synonyms: aire, flatulencia
