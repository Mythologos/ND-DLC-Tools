Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek ἰσομετρία (isometría), from ἰσο- (iso-) + -μετρία (-metría), from ἴσος (ísos, “equal”) and μέτρον (métron, “measurement”) + -ια (-ia, “-y”).

Noun[edit]

īsometria f (genitive īsometriae); first declension

  1. (New Latin) isometry

Declension[edit]

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin isometria, from Ancient Greek.

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /i.zo.meˈtɾi.ɐ/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /i.zo.meˈtɾi.ɐ/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /i.zo.meˈtɾi.a/

Noun[edit]

isometria f (plural isometrias)

  1. (mathematics) isometry (function in which the distance between two images is equal to the distance between their pre-images)
