Finnish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Late Latin mammona, from Ancient Greek μαμωνᾶς (mamōnâs), from Aramaic מָמוֹנָא.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmɑmːonɑ/, [ˈmɑ̝mːo̞nɑ̝]
  • Rhymes: -ɑmːonɑ
  • Syllabification(key): mam‧mo‧na

Noun[edit]

mammona

  1. mammon, Mammon (wealth, material avarice)

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • mamōna

Etymology[edit]

From Hellenistic Ancient Greek μαμμωνᾶς (mammōnâs), from Aramaic מָמוֹנָא (māmōnā, “riches”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /mamˈmoː.na/, [mämˈmoːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /mamˈmo.na/, [mämˈmɔːnä]

Noun[edit]

mammōna m (genitive mammōnae); first declension

  1. riches, wealth, mammon

Declension[edit]
