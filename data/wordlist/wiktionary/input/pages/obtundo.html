Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ob- (“upon”) +‎ tundo (“I strike”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /obˈtun.doː/, [ɔpˈt̪ʊn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /obˈtun.do/, [obˈt̪un̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

obtundō (present infinitive obtundere, perfect active obtudī, supine obtūsum); third conjugation

  1. to strike, beat or batter
  2. to blunt
  3. to deafen
  4. to buffet
