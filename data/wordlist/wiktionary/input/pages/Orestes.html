English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Ὀρέστης (Oréstēs).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ɒˈɹɛstiːz/

Proper noun[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Orestes
Wikipedia

Orestes

  1. (Greek mythology) The son of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra, who killed his mother, avenging his father's death, and was then pursued by the Furies.
  2. A region of Macedonia.
  3. A town in Indiana.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Ὀρέστης (Oréstēs).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /oˈres.teːs/, [ɔˈrɛs̠t̪eːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /oˈres.tes/, [oˈrɛst̪es]

Proper noun[edit]

Orestēs m sg (variously declined, genitive Orestis or Orestae or Orestī); third declension, first declension

  1. Orestes, the son of Agamemnon
