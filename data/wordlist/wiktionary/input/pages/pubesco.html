Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From pūbēs +‎ -ēscō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /puːˈbeːs.koː/, [puːˈbeːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /puˈbes.ko/, [puˈbɛsko]

Verb[edit]

pūbēscō (present infinitive pūbēscere, perfect active pūbuī); third conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to become pubescent
  2. to ripen, mature
