Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • exenterō, extenterō

Etymology[edit]

exenterō with assimilation to interāneum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ekˈsin.te.roː/, [ɛkˈs̠ɪn̪t̪ɛroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ekˈsin.te.ro/, [eɡˈzin̪t̪ero]

Verb[edit]

exinterō (present infinitive exinterāre, perfect active exinterāvī, supine exinterātum); first conjugation

  1. to disembowel
  2. to torture, torment
  3. to empty, gut, remove everything from inside something
