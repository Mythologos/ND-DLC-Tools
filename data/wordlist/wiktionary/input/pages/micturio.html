Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From mingō (“to urinate”) +‎ -turiō (“to desire or wish”, desiderative suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /mikˈtu.ri.oː/, [mɪkˈt̪ʊrioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /mikˈtu.ri.o/, [mikˈt̪uːrio]

Verb[edit]

micturiō (present infinitive micturīre, perfect active micturīvī or micturiī, supine micturītum); fourth conjugation

  1. to go to urinate
    • c. 100 CE – c. 130 CE, Juvenal, Satires 6.309
    • c. 100 CE – c. 130 CE, Juvenal, Satires 16.46
