English[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈdæni/
  • Audio (Southern England)(file)
  • Rhymes: -æni

Proper noun[edit]

Dani

  1. A diminutive of the female given name Danielle and of its variant forms.
  2. A rare spelling variant of Danny, diminutive of the male given name Daniel.

Etymology 2[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Dani (surname)
Wikipedia
  • As an Italian, Slovak (Dáni), Romanian, and German surname, from diminutives of the given name Daniel.
  • As an Albanian surname, from Dan, a short form of the Islamic name Ramadan.
  • As an Indian surname, borrowed from Gujarati દાણી (dāṇī), from Sanskrit दानिन् (dānin, “giving, generous”).

Proper noun[edit]

Dani (plural Danis)

  1. A surname from Gujarati.
Statistics[edit]
  • According to the 2010 United States Census, Dani is the 36337th most common surname in the United States, belonging to 616 individuals. Dani is most common among White (48.54%) and Asian/Pacific Islander (42.37%) individuals.

Further reading[edit]

  • Hanks, Patrick, editor (2003), “Dani”, in Dictionary of American Family Names, volume 1, New York City: Oxford University Press, →ISBN, page 409.

Anagrams[edit]

  • -inda, Adin, Andi, Dain, Dina, Inda, NAID, Nida, ad in

Albanian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Dan (Dan) +‎ -i (-(of) the / -s). See Dan (“Dan, Danny”) for more.

Proper noun[edit]

Dani m (definite Dani)

  1. nominative singular definite of Dan
  2. genitive singular indefinite of Dan
  3. dative singular indefinite of Dan
  4. ablative singular indefinite of Dan

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Rhymes: -ni

Proper noun[edit]

Dani

  1. a diminutive of the male given name Dániel

Icelandic[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Old Norse danir, from Proto-Germanic *daniz.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈtaːnɪ/
    Rhymes: -aːnɪ

Noun[edit]

Dani m (genitive singular Dana, nominative plural Danir)

  1. Dane (person from Denmark or of Danish descent)

Further reading[edit]

  • “Dani” in the Dictionary of Modern Icelandic (in Icelandic) and ISLEX (in the Nordic languages)

Anagrams[edit]

  • andi

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Proto-Germanic *daniz.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈdaː.niː/, [ˈd̪äːniː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈda.ni/, [ˈd̪äːni]

Proper noun[edit]

Dānī m pl (genitive Dānōrum); second declension

  1. (Late Latin) the Danes (natives of Denmark)
    • Saxo Grammaticus, Gesta Danorum
      Danorum Regum heroumque Historiae
      "History of the Kings and heroes of the Danes"
    (Can we find and add a quotation of Venantius Fortunatus to this entry?)

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun, plural only.
