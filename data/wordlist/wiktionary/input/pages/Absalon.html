Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Abessālōm
  • Abessālōn

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek Ἀβεσαλώμ (Abesalṓm), derived from Biblical Hebrew אַבְשָׁלוֹם (Avshalóm).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈab.sa.loːn/, [ˈäps̠äɫ̪oːn]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈab.sa.lon/, [ˈäbsälon]

Proper noun[edit]

Absalōn m sg (genitive Absalōnis); third declension

  1. (biblical) Absalom

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun, singular only.

Nominative Absalōn

Old Polish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin Absalōn. First attested in c. 1500. Compare Polish Absalon.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (10th–15th CE) /apsalʲɔn/
  • IPA(key): (15th CE) /apsalʲɔn/

Proper noun[edit]

Absalon m pers

  1. (biblical) Absalom
