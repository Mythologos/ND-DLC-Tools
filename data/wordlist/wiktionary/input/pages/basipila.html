Wikipedia la

Etymology[edit]

From basis (“base”) +‎ pila (“ball (game)”), a calque of English baseball.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /baˈsi.pi.la/, [bäˈs̬iːpilä]

Noun[edit]

basipila f sg (genitive basipilae); first declension

  1. (Contemporary Latin) baseball (sport)
    • 1996, Latinitas‎[1], volume 44, Vatican City: Pontifical Academy for Latin, →ISSN, page 350:
      Sub finem mensis Novembris nata crudelis, Roderico iuvante, suum patrem suamque matrem baculo, quo basipila (base-ball) ludentes utuntur, verberavit, percussit, interfecit et ambo sanguinem e corporibus excruciatis manantem biberunt.
