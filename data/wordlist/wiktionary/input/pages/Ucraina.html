Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Old Ruthenian Оукраи́на (Ukraína, “Ukraine”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /uː.kraːˈiː.na/, [uːkräːˈiːnä] or IPA(key): /u.kraːˈiː.na/, [ʊkräːˈiːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /u.kraˈi.na/, [ukräˈiːnä]
  • (Classical) IPA(key): /uˈkra.i.na/, [ʊˈkräɪnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /uˈkra.i.na/, [uˈkräːinä]

Proper noun[edit]

Ū̆crāīna f sg (genitive Ū̆crāīnae); first declension or Ucraina f sg (genitive Ucrainae); first declension

  1. (New Latin) Ukraine (a country in Eastern Europe, bordering on the north coast of the Black Sea)
    • 1710 April 5, Philippus Orlik, chapter I, in Contenta Pactorum inter Ducem et Exercitum Zaporoviensem conventorum, in Compendium brevi stylo collecta.‎[1], Bendery, archived from the original on 9 April 2022:
      Religionem in Ucraina oportet esse solam et unam Orthodoxae Orientalis Confessionis, [...]
