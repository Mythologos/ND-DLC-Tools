Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Coined by Plautus, from re- + Charmides + -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reˈkʰar.mi.doː/, [rɛˈkʰärmɪd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈkar.mi.do/, [reˈkärmid̪o]

Verb[edit]

recharmidō (present infinitive recharmidāre, perfect active recharmidāvī, supine recharmidātum); first conjugation

  1. (hapax, humorous) to stop acting like Charmides, to decharmidize oneself
    • c. 195 BCE, Plautus, Trinummus 977:
      “proin tu te, itidem ut charmidatus es, rursum recharmida.”
