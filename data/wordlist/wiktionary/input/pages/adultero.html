Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ad (“to”) +‎ alterum/alteram (“the other”) +‎ -ō. The vowel change is the same as is between alo, ad-ulesco and ad-ultus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /aˈdul.te.roː/, [äˈd̪ʊɫ̪t̪ɛroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /aˈdul.te.ro/, [äˈd̪ul̪t̪ero]

Verb[edit]

adulterō (present infinitive adulterāre, perfect active adulterāvī, supine adulterātum); first conjugation

  1. to commit adultery (with)
  2. (figuratively) to falsify, counterfeit, adulterate; to pollute, corrupt, defile
