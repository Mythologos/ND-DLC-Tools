Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • condēnseō

Etymology[edit]

From con- (“with, together”) +‎ dēnsō (“thicken”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /konˈden.soː/, [kɔn̪ˈd̪ẽːs̠oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /konˈden.so/, [kon̪ˈd̪ɛnso]

Verb[edit]

condēnsō (present infinitive condēnsāre, perfect active condēnsāvī, supine condēnsātum); first conjugation

  1. to make very dense, press close together, condense
