English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
germen
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From Latin germen (“germ, seed”). Doublet of germ.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈdʒɜː.mən/
  • (US) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒɝ.mən/
  • Homophone: German
  • Rhymes: -ɜː(ɹ)mən

Noun[edit]

germen (plural germens or germina)

  1. (biology) The basic form of an organism; the seed or germ.

Anagrams[edit]

  • Mergen

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Syncopated from Proto-Italic *genamen, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵénh₁mn̥ (“offspring”, “seed”), from the root *ǵenh₁- (“to beget”, “to give birth”).[1] Equivalent to gignō (“I beget”) +‎ -men (noun-forming suffix). Compare with genimen.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈɡer.men/, [ˈɡɛrmɛn]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒer.men/, [ˈd͡ʒɛrmen]

Noun[edit]

germen n (genitive germinis); third declension

  1. shoot, sprout, bud
  2. germ, seed, origin
  3. embryo, fetus

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun (neuter, imparisyllabic non-i-stem).

Romanian[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • germene

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin germen.

Noun[edit]

germen m (plural germeni)

  1. embryo
    Synonym: embrion
  2. (biology) seed, germ (small mass of cells from which an organism grows)
  3. germ
    Synonym: microb
  4. principle, element, component
    Synonyms: principiu, element
