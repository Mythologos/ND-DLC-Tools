Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From inter (“between; among”) +‎ rogō (“ask; request”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈter.ro.ɡoː/, [ɪn̪ˈt̪ɛrːɔɡoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈter.ro.ɡo/, [in̪ˈt̪ɛrːoɡo]

Verb[edit]

interrogō (present infinitive interrogāre, perfect active interrogāvī, supine interrogātum); first conjugation

  1. (transitive) to ask, question, inquire, interrogate, to seek information from
    Synonyms: scīscitor, quaerō, inquīrō, requīrō, percontor, scīscō, investīgō, explōrō
    cāsus interrogandī ― the genitive case
    Aemilia interrogat "Quis mē vocat?"[1]
    Aemilia asks "Who called me?"
  2. (law) to put a formal question to (in making a stipulation)
  3. (law, transitive) to question judicially, examine, interrogate
  4. (transitive with sententiam) to ask (a senator) for his opinion, consult
  5. (transitive, often with lēge) to call to account in a court of law, arraign, indict, sue
  6. (intransitive) to argue, reason (syllogistically)
