Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • sacrificor, sacrufico

Etymology[edit]

From sacer (“sacred”) +‎ -ficō (“I do, make”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /saˈkri.fi.koː/, [s̠äˈkrɪfɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /saˈkri.fi.ko/, [säˈkriːfiko]

Verb[edit]

sacrificō (present infinitive sacrificāre, perfect active sacrificāvī, supine sacrificātum); first conjugation

  1. to make or offer a sacrifice; to sacrifice
