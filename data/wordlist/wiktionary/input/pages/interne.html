  1. Archaic spelling of intern.

Verb[edit]

  1. Archaic spelling of intern.

Anagrams[edit]

French[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin internus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ɛ̃.tɛʁn/
  • audio(file)

Adjective[edit]

interne (plural internes)

  1. internal
    Antonym: externe

Derived terms[edit]

  • classe interne
  • hémorragie interne
  • horloge interne
  • interner
  • internaliser
  • oreille interne

Related terms[edit]

  • intérieur

Noun[edit]

interne m or f by sense (plural internes)

  1. intern (as in a medical student)
  2. boarder (pupil living at school)
    Synonym: pensionnaire

Verb[edit]

interne

  1. inflection of interner:
    1. first/third-person singular present indicative/subjunctive
    2. second-person singular imperative

Further reading[edit]

  • “interne”, in Trésor de la langue française informatisé [Digitized Treasury of the French Language], 2012.

Anagrams[edit]

  • nièrent, renient

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From internus (“inward, internal”) +‎ -ē (adverbial suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈter.neː/, [ɪn̪ˈt̪ɛrneː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈter.ne/, [in̪ˈt̪ɛrne]

Adverb[edit]

internē (comparative internius, superlative internissimē)

  1. inwardly, internally
