Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

inter- +‎ volō (“to fly”)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈter.u̯o.loː/, [ɪn̪ˈt̪ɛru̯ɔɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈter.vo.lo/, [in̪ˈt̪ɛrvolo]

Verb[edit]

intervolō (present infinitive intervolāre, perfect active intervolāvī, supine intervolātum); first conjugation, no passive

  1. (transitive, intransitive, literally and figuratively) to fly between or among
