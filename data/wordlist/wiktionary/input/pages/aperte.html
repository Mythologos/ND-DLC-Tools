Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From apertus (“open, uncovered; frank, candid”) +‎ -ē (“-ly”, adverbial suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /aˈper.teː/, [äˈpɛrt̪eː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /aˈper.te/, [äˈpɛrt̪e]

Adverb[edit]

apertē (comparative apertius, superlative apertissimē)

  1. openly, clearly, plainly
    Synonyms: pūblicē, vulgō, palam
    Antonym: clam
    aperte dēscīscere ― to defect plainly
  2. (writing) plainly, clearly, freely, without reserve

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /aˈper.te/, [äˈpɛrt̪ɛ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /aˈper.te/, [äˈpɛrt̪e]

Participle[edit]

aperte

  1. vocative masculine singular of apertus
