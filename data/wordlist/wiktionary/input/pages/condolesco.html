Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

con- +‎ doleō +‎ -scō. Not from condoleō; this is an Ecclesiastical Latin word which does not appear until much later.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kon.doˈleːs.koː/, [kɔn̪d̪ɔˈɫ̪eːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kon.doˈles.ko/, [kon̪d̪oˈlɛsko]

Verb[edit]

condolēscō (present infinitive condolēscere, perfect active condoluī); third conjugation, no supine stem

  1. to (begin to) feel severe pain, suffer greatly, ache
