Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Inherited from Early Medieval Latin manicus, derived from Latin manus (“hand”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈma.ni.ko/
  • Rhymes: -aniko
  • Hyphenation: mà‧ni‧co

Noun[edit]

manico m (plural manici or manichi)

  1. handle
  2. shaft
  3. neck (of a violin etc.)

See also[edit]

  • manica

Anagrams[edit]

  • Monica, camino, camion, cimano, macino, macinò, monaci, monica, nomica

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Probably ultimately from māne (“morning”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈmaː.ni.koː/, [ˈmäːnɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈma.ni.ko/, [ˈmäːniko]

Verb[edit]

mānicō (present infinitive mānicāre, perfect active mānicāvī, supine mānicātum); first conjugation

  2. to come in the morning; rise and set out in the morning
