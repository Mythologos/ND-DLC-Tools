Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dē- +‎ populus +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /deːˈpo.pu.loː/, [d̪eːˈpɔpʊɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /deˈpo.pu.lo/, [d̪eˈpɔːpulo]

Verb[edit]

dēpopulō (present infinitive dēpopulāre, perfect active dēpopulāvī, supine dēpopulātum); first conjugation

  1. to sack, plunder, pillage, despoil
  2. to ravage, devastate, destroy, lay waste
