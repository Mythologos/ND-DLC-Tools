Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ mūgiō (“to low, moo”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /imˈmuː.ɡi.oː/, [ɪmˈmuːɡioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /imˈmu.d͡ʒi.o/, [imˈmuːd͡ʒio]

Verb[edit]

immūgiō (present infinitive immūgīre, perfect active immūgīvī or immūgiī, supine immūgītum); fourth conjugation, no passive

  1. (intransitive) to bellow in or at
