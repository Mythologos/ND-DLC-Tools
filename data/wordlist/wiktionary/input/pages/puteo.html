Galician[edit]

Verb[edit]

puteo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of putear

Ido[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowing from Latin puteus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /puˈte.o/

Noun[edit]

puteo (plural putei)

  1. well: shaft sunk in the ground for water, oil

Derived terms[edit]

  • puteokordo (“well rope”)
  • puteoarteza (“Artesian well”)

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *pūtēō, from Proto-Indo-European *puH-. Cognate with English foul.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈpuː.te.oː/, [ˈpuːt̪eoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpu.te.o/, [ˈpuːt̪eo]

Verb[edit]

pūteō (present infinitive pūtēre); second conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to stink, be rotten, putrid

Spanish[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /puˈteo/ [puˈt̪e.o]
  • Rhymes: -eo
  • Syllabification: pu‧te‧o

Etymology 1[edit]

Deverbal from putear.

Noun[edit]

puteo m (plural puteos)

  1. annoyance; pain in the neck
  2. whoring

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

puteo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of putear
