Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Probably related to rēsīna (“resin”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈraː.sis/, [ˈräːs̠ɪs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈra.sis/, [ˈräːs̬is]

Noun[edit]

rāsis f (genitive rāsis); third declension

  1. A kind of raw pitch
Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun (i-stem).

Etymology 2[edit]

Participle[edit]

rāsīs

  1. dative/ablative masculine/feminine/neuter plural of rāsus

Malay[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from English racist.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /rasis/, /resis/
  • Rhymes: -sis, -is

Adjective[edit]

rasis (Jawi spelling راسيس)

  1. Racist; constituting, exhibiting, advocating, or pertaining to racism.
    Synonym: rasialis (uncommon)

Related terms[edit]

  • ras
  • rasisme
