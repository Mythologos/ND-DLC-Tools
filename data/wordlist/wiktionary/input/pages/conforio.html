Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

con- +‎ foriō, from foria (“diarrhea”).

Verb[edit]

cōnforiō (present infinitive cōnforīre, perfect active cōnforiī); fourth conjugation, no supine stem[1]

  1. (vulgar) to defile, pollute with ordure, diarrhea
    Conforisti me, Diomedes
