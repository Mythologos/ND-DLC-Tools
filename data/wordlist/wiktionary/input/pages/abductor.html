English[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (General American) IPA(key): /æbˈdʌk.tɚ/
  • Audio (CA)(file)

Etymology 1[edit]

abduct +‎ -or

Noun[edit]

abductor (plural abductors)

  1. One who abducts; a kidnapper. [mid 19th century][1]
Antonyms[edit]

Derived terms[edit]

Etymology 2[edit]

From Medieval Latin abductor, from abdūcō + -tor.

Noun[edit]

abductor (plural abductors or abductores)

  2. (anatomy) A muscle which serves to draw a part out, or from the median line of the body[early 17th century][1]
    the abductor oculi draws the eye outward.
Antonyms[edit]
  • abductee
  • adductor

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From abdūcō (“I abduct”) +‎ -tor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /abˈduk.tor/, [äbˈd̪ʊkt̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /abˈduk.tor/, [äbˈd̪ukt̪or]

Noun[edit]

abductor m (genitive abductōris); third declension

  1. (Medieval Latin) abductor
    Synonyms: plagiātor, plagiārius

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun.

Dative abductōrī abductōribus

Ablative abductōre abductōribus
