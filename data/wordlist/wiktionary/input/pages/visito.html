Istriot[edit]

Noun[edit]

visito

  1. little face
    • 1877, Antonio Ive, Canti popolari istriani: raccolti a Rovigno, volume 5, Ermanno Loescher, page 128:
      Caro, cun quil visito bianco e russo.
      Dear, with that little white and red face.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From vīsō +‎ -itō (frequentative suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈu̯iː.si.toː/, [ˈu̯iːs̠ɪt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈvi.si.to/, [ˈviːs̬it̪o]

Verb[edit]

vīsitō (present infinitive vīsitāre, perfect active vīsitāvī, supine vīsitātum); first conjugation

  1. to see
  2. to go to see; to visit
  3. (law) to send
  4. (Ecclesiastical Latin) to punish
