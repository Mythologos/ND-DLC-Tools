Kari'na[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (West Suriname) IPA(key): [ɲaːɺ̢o]

Pronoun[edit]

inaro

  1. (West Suriname) Alternative form of inaron (“the animate plural anaphoric demonstrative pronoun; the ones aforementioned, the ones that were just spoken of”)

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- (“in, at, on”) +‎ arō (“plough”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈi.na.roː/, [ˈɪnäroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈi.na.ro/, [ˈiːnäro]

Verb[edit]

inarō (present infinitive inarāre, perfect active inarāvī, supine inarātum); first conjugation

  1. to plough in, cover by ploughing
  2. (by extension) to till, cultivate, plough
  3. (figuratively) to enter or write in a list
  4. (figuratively) to mark
