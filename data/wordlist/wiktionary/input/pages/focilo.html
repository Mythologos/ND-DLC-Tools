Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Commonly attested reduction of the intertonic /u/, which often further resulted in its deletion.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈfoː.ki.loː/, [ˈfoːkɪɫ̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈfo.t͡ʃi.lo/, [ˈfɔːt͡ʃilo]

Verb[edit]

fōcilō (present infinitive fōcilāre, perfect active fōcilāvī, supine fōcilātum); first conjugation

  1. (post-Classical) Alternative form of fōculō (“to cherish, resuscitate”)
