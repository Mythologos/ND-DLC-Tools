Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From tremō (“tremble, shake”) +‎ -īscō (inchoative).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /treˈmiːs.koː/, [t̪rɛˈmiːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /treˈmis.ko/, [t̪reˈmisko]

Verb[edit]

tremīscō (present infinitive tremīscere); third conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. Alternative form of tremēscō
