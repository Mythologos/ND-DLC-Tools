English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Late Latin rotella, diminutive of rota (“wheel”). Doublet of rowel.

Noun[edit]

rotella

  1. A disc.
  2. A round shield.

Anagrams[edit]

  • reallot, tallero

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Late Latin rotella, diminutive of Latin rota (“wheel”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /roˈtɛl.la/
  • Rhymes: -ɛlla
  • Hyphenation: ro‧tèl‧la

Noun[edit]

rotella f (plural rotelle)

  1. small wheel (in any context); roller; roller skate
  2. (usually in the plural) wheel-shaped pasta
  3. a small round shield used in the 16th century
  4. (anatomy) rotula
    Synonym: rotula

Related terms[edit]

  • ruota
  • rotella tagliapasta (pastry wheel)

Anagrams[edit]

  • Tellaro, allerto, allertò, rollate, tallero, tollera

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Found in Late Latin, diminutive of rota (“wheel”) (with the suffix -ella).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /roˈtel.la/, [rɔˈt̪ɛlːʲä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /roˈtel.la/, [roˈt̪ɛlːä]

Noun[edit]

rotella f (genitive rotellae); first declension

  1. (Late Latin) small wheel

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.
