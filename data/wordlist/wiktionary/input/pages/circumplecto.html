Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

See circumplector.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kir.kumˈplek.toː/, [kɪrkʊmˈpɫ̪ɛkt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /t͡ʃir.kumˈplek.to/, [t͡ʃirkumˈplɛkt̪o]

Verb[edit]

circumplectō (present infinitive circumplectere, perfect active circumplexī, supine circumplexum); third conjugation

  1. Alternative form of circumplector

Usage notes[edit]

This verb is much more frequently encountered as a deponent verb, circumplector.
