Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

con- +‎ calleō (“to be callous, shrewd”) +‎ -scō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /kon.kalˈleːs.koː/, [kɔŋkälˈlʲeːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /kon.kalˈles.ko/, [koŋkälˈlɛsko]

Verb[edit]

concallēscō (present infinitive concallēscere, perfect active concalluī); third conjugation, no supine stem

  1. to become callous, become insensitive
  2. to become shrewd
