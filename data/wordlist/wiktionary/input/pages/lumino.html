Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From lume +‎ -ino.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /luˈmi.no/
  • Rhymes: -ino
  • Hyphenation: lu‧mì‧no

Noun[edit]

lumino m (plural lumini)

  1. Diminutive of lume (“small lamp or light”)

Further reading[edit]

  • lumino in Treccani.it – Vocabolario Treccani on line, Istituto dell'Enciclopedia Italiana

Anagrams[edit]

  • mulino, mulinò

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From lūmen +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈluː.mi.noː/, [ˈɫ̪uːmɪnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈlu.mi.no/, [ˈluːmino]

Verb[edit]

lūminō (present infinitive lūmināre, perfect active lūmināvī, supine lūminātum); first conjugation

  1. to illuminate
  2. to reveal (throw light on)
  3. to brighten
