Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dis- +‎ fundō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /difˈfun.doː/, [d̪ɪfˈfʊn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /difˈfun.do/, [d̪ifˈfun̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

diffundō (present infinitive diffundere, perfect active diffūdī, supine diffūsum); third conjugation

  1. to diffuse, scatter
    Synonyms: fundō, effundō, confundō, dēfundō, differō, dissipō, indūcō, sternō
  2. to decant, spread or pour out
