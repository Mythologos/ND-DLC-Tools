English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin naris (“nostril”). Itself from Latin nāsus with rhotacism.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈnɛə.ɹɪs/

Noun[edit]

naris (plural nares)

  1. a nostril

Related terms[edit]

  • posterior naris

Anagrams[edit]

  • Nairs, Nasir, Rains, Risan, arnis, nasri, rains, ranis, sarin, siRNA, sirna

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From nāsus with rhotacism.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈnaː.ris/, [ˈnäːrɪs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈna.ris/, [ˈnäːris]

Noun[edit]

nāris f (genitive nāris); third declension

  1. (usually in the plural) (anatomy) A nostril, nose.
  2. An opening, orifice, vent, air-hole.

Declension[edit]

Third-declension noun (i-stem).
