Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From earlier *balaneum, a borrowing from Ancient Greek βαλανεῖον (balaneîon), which displays vowel reduction of a to i.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Plautine) IPA(key): /ˈba.li.ne.um/, [ˈbälʲɪneʊ̃ˑ]
  • (Classical) IPA(key): /baˈli.ne.um/, [bäˈlʲɪneʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /baˈli.ne.um/, [bäˈliːneum]

Noun[edit]

balineum n (genitive balineī); second declension

  1. Plautine and later archaic form of balneum.

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
