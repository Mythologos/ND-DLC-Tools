Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek ὑδρομαντεία (hudromanteía), compound of ὑδρο- (hudro-, “hydro-, water”) + μαντεία (manteía, “divination”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /hy.dro.manˈtiː.a/, [hʏd̪rɔmän̪ˈt̪iːä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /i.dro.manˈti.a/, [id̪romän̪ˈt̪iːä]

Noun[edit]

hydromantīa f (genitive hydromantīae); first declension

  1. hydromancy
