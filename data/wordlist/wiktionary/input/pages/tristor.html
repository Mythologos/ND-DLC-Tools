Catalan[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From trist +‎ -or.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [tɾisˈto]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [tɾisˈtoɾ]

Noun[edit]

tristor f (plural tristors)

  1. sadness
    Synonym: tristesa

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From trīstis.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈtriːs.tor/, [ˈt̪riːs̠t̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈtris.tor/, [ˈt̪rist̪or]

Verb[edit]

trīstor (present infinitive trīstārī, perfect active trīstātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. to be sad, dejected, downcast
  2. to grieve
