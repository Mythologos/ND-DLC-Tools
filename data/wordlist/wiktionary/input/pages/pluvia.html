Interlingua[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin pluvia (“rain”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈpluvja/

Noun[edit]

pluvia (plural pluvias)

  1. rain

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • *plovia > *ploia (Vulgar Latin)

Etymology[edit]

Feminine form of pluvius.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈplu.u̯i.a/, [ˈpɫ̪uː̯iä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈplu.vi.a/, [ˈpluːviä]

Noun[edit]

pluvia f (genitive pluviae); first declension

  1. rain
    • c. 99 BCE – 55 BCE, Lucretius, De rerum natura 6.671:
      tempestātēs pluviae
      tempests of rain
  2. (later) a shower (of something)

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin pluvia. Doublet of lluvia.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈplubja/ [ˈplu.β̞ja]
  • Rhymes: -ubja
  • Syllabification: plu‧via

Noun[edit]

pluvia f (plural pluvias)

  1. rain
  2. shower (precipitation)
