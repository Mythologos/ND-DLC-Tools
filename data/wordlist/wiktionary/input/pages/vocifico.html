Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From vōx (“voice”) + facio (“do, make, produce”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /u̯oːˈki.fi.koː/, [u̯oːˈkɪfɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /voˈt͡ʃi.fi.ko/, [voˈt͡ʃiːfiko]

Verb[edit]

vōcificō (present infinitive vōcificāre, perfect active vōcificāvī, supine vōcificātum); first conjugation

  1. to cry aloud, utter a loud cry; proclaim
    Synonyms: conclāmō, exclāmō, acclāmō, succlāmō, clāmō, vōciferor, clāmitō, tonō, inclāmō
