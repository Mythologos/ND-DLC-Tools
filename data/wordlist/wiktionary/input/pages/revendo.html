Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From re- (“re-: back, again”) +‎ vēndō (“sell”), from vēnum dō (“give for sale”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /reˈu̯eːn.doː/, [reˈu̯eːn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /reˈven.do/, [reˈvɛn̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

revēndō (present infinitive revēndere, perfect active revēndidī, supine revēnditum); third conjugation

  1. to resell, revend

Usage notes[edit]

In Classical Latin, the only passive forms used for vēndō were the past participle revēnditus and the future participle revēndendus; other forms were later supplied by analogy with vēneō.
