English[edit]

Adjective[edit]

bicolor (not comparable)

  1. (American spelling) Alternative form of bicolour

Noun[edit]

bicolor (plural bicolors)

  1. (American spelling) Alternative form of bicolour

Anagrams[edit]

  • brocoli

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • bicolōrus

Etymology[edit]

From bis (“twice”) + color (“color, hue”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbi.ko.lor/, [ˈbɪkɔɫ̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbi.ko.lor/, [ˈbiːkolor]

Adjective[edit]

bicolor (genitive bicolōris); third-declension one-termination adjective

  1. Of two colors or having two colors, two-colored.
    Synonym: dichrōus

Declension[edit]

Third-declension one-termination adjective.
