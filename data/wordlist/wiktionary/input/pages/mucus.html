English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
mucus
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin mūcus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈmjuːkəs/
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • Rhymes: -uːkəs
  • Hyphenation: mu‧cus
  • Homophone: mucous

Noun[edit]

mucus (usually uncountable, plural mucuses or muci)

  1. (physiology) A slippery secretion from the lining of the mucous membranes.
    Hyponyms: phlegm, rheum

Usage notes[edit]

  • Do not confuse mucous (adjective) with mucus (noun).

Derived terms[edit]

  • mucoid
  • mucoidal
 

Related terms[edit]

  • mucilaginous
  • mucosa
  • mucositis
  • mucous

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • muccus

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *moukos, from Proto-Indo-European *mew-k- (“slimy, slippery”). Cognates include Ancient Greek μύκης (múkēs, “mushroom”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈmuː.kus/, [ˈmuːkʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈmu.kus/, [ˈmuːkus]

Noun[edit]

mūcus m (genitive mūcī); second declension

  1. mucus
