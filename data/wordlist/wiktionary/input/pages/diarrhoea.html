English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
diarrhoea
Wikipedia

Noun[edit]

diarrhoea (usually uncountable, plural diarrhoeas)

  1. British standard spelling of diarrhea.

Derived terms[edit]

  • diarrhoeal

References[edit]

  • “diarrhoea”, in Lexico, Dictionary.com; Oxford University Press, 2019–2022.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek διάρροια (diárrhoia).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /di.arˈroe̯.a/, [d̪iärˈroe̯ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /di.arˈre.a/, [d̪iärˈrɛːä]

Noun[edit]

diarrhoea f (genitive diarrhoeae); first declension

  1. (Late Latin) diarrhoea

Declension[edit]
