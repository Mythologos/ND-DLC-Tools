Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

ferōx (“fierce”) +‎ -iō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /feˈroː.ki.oː/, [fɛˈroːkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /feˈro.t͡ʃi.o/, [feˈrɔːt͡ʃio]

Verb[edit]

ferōciō (present infinitive ferōcīre, perfect active ferōcīvī or ferōciī, supine ferōcītum); fourth conjugation

  1. to be fierce, unruly, ungovernable
  2. to rage, to be furious
