Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From auctiō (“public sale, auction”) +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /au̯k.tiˈoː.nor/, [äu̯kt̪iˈoːnɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /au̯k.t͡siˈo.nor/, [äu̯kt̪͡s̪iˈɔːnor]

Verb[edit]

auctiōnor (present infinitive auctiōnārī, perfect active auctiōnātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. (intransitive) to hold an auction or public sale; make a sale by auction
  2. (transitive) to buy at auction
