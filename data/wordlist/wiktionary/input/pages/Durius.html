Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Latinized Celtic name, from Proto-Celtic *dubros (“water”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈduː.ri.us/, [ˈd̪uːriʊs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈdu.ri.us/, [ˈd̪uːrius]

Proper noun[edit]

Dūrius m sg (genitive Dūriī or Dūrī); second declension

  1. Douro (a river in Portugal and Spain)

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun, singular only.

Descendants[edit]

  • Old Galician-Portuguese: Doiro, Doyro
    • Portuguese: Douro
  • Spanish: Duero
