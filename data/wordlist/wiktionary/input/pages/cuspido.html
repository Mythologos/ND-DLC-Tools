Galician[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /kusˈpiðo̝/

Adjective[edit]

cuspido (feminine cuspida, masculine plural cuspidos, feminine plural cuspidas)

  1. alike
    Es cuspido a teu pai! ― You are alike your father! / You're the spitting image of your father!

Derived terms[edit]

  • cuspidiño

Participle[edit]

cuspido (feminine cuspida, masculine plural cuspidos, feminine plural cuspidas)

  1. past participle of cuspir
  2. spat. past participle of cusp

  • “cuspido” in Dicionario de Dicionarios da lingua galega, SLI - ILGA 2006–2013.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From cuspis +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈkus.pi.doː/, [ˈkʊs̠pɪd̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈkus.pi.do/, [ˈkuspid̪o]

Verb[edit]

cuspidō (present infinitive cuspidāre, perfect active cuspidāvī, supine cuspidātum); first conjugation

  1. to make pointed
