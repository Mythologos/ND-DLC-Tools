Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From fossa. Compare with Neapolitan fuosso.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈfɔs.so/
  • Rhymes: -ɔsso
  • Hyphenation: fòs‧so

Noun[edit]

fosso m (plural fossi)

  1. ditch
  2. moat

Derived terms[edit]

  • refosso / rifosso

Descendants[edit]

  • → Spanish: foso

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Frequentative of fodiō (“dig, excavate; mine”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈfos.soː/, [ˈfɔs̠ːoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈfos.so/, [ˈfɔsːo]

Verb[edit]

fossō (present infinitive fossāre, perfect active fossāvī, supine fossātum); first conjugation

  1. (with the sense of being done often) to dig, pierce
