Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From perpetuus (“perpetual, continuous”) +‎ -itō (frequentative suffix).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /per.peˈtu.i.toː/, [pɛrpɛˈt̪uɪt̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /per.peˈtu.i.to/, [perpeˈt̪uːit̪o]

Verb[edit]

perpetuitō (present infinitive perpetuitāre); first conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to perpetuate
