Italian[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /d͡ʒerˈmi.no/
  • Rhymes: -ino
  • Hyphenation: ger‧mì‧no

Noun[edit]

germino m (plural germini)

  1. Diminutive of germe: small germ, small seed

Etymology 2[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒɛr.mi.no/
  • Rhymes: -ɛrmino
  • Hyphenation: gèr‧mi‧no

Verb[edit]

germino

  1. first-person singular present indicative of germinare

Anagrams[edit]

  • mingerò

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From germen.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈɡer.mi.noː/, [ˈɡɛrmɪnoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈd͡ʒer.mi.no/, [ˈd͡ʒɛrmino]

Verb[edit]

germinō (present infinitive germināre, perfect active germināvī, supine germinātum); first conjugation

  1. to sprout
  2. to bud
  3. to grow
