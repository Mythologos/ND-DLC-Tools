French[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio(file)

Verb[edit]

diligenter

  1. (transitive) to expedite

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dīligēns +‎ -ter, from the present active participle of dīligō (“I esteem, I love”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /diː.liˈɡen.ter/, [d̪iːlʲɪˈɡɛn̪t̪ɛr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /di.liˈd͡ʒen.ter/, [d̪iliˈd͡ʒɛn̪t̪er]

Adverb[edit]

dīligenter (comparative dīligentius, superlative dīligentissimē)

  1. diligently, carefully

Related terms[edit]

  • dīligēns
  • dīligō

    • to attend carefully: diligenter attendere (aliquid)
