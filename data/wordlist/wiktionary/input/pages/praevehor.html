Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From prae- +‎ vehor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈprae̯.u̯e.hor/, [ˈpräe̯u̯e(ɦ)ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpre.ve.or/, [ˈprɛːveor]

Verb[edit]

praevehor (present infinitive praevehī, perfect active praevectus sum); third conjugation, deponent

  1. to travel past or along
