Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From praeter- +‎ labor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /prae̯.terˈlaː.bor/, [präe̯t̪ɛrˈɫ̪äːbɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /pre.terˈla.bor/, [pret̪erˈläːbor]

Verb[edit]

praeterlābor (present infinitive praeterlābī, perfect active praeterlāpsus sum); third conjugation, deponent

  1. to slip, glide or flow by
