English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
Bosnia
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From the name of the river Bosna.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈbɒz.ni.ə/
  • (US) IPA(key): /ˈbɑz.ni.ə/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Proper noun[edit]

Bosnia

  1. The northern three-fourths of the country of Bosnia and Herzegovina, as a geographic and historical entity, not an administrative unit.
  2. Ellipsis of Bosnia and Herzegovina. (the whole country).

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Bosna

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbos.ni.a/, [ˈbɔs̠niä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbos.ni.a/, [ˈbɔzniä]

Proper noun[edit]

Bosnia f sg (genitive Bosniae); first declension

  1. (New Latin) Bosnia
  2. (Medieval Latin, obsolete) Synonym of Mysia Inferior.
    • 1511, Bernardus Sylvanus ed., Jacobus Angelus's translation of Ptolemy as Liber Geographica, Bk VIII:
      In nona Tabula[:] Iaziges metanastae[,] Dacia Vulachia[,] Mysia superior: Seruia[,] Mysia inferior Bosna[,] Tratia[,] Chersonesus.
