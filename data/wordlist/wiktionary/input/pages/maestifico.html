Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

Adjective[edit]

maestificō

  1. dative/ablative masculine/neuter singular of maestificus

Etymology 2[edit]

From maestus (“sad”) +‎ faciō (“I make”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /mae̯sˈti.fi.ko/, [mäe̯s̠ˈt̪ɪfɪkɔ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /mesˈti.fi.ko/, [mesˈt̪iːfiko]

Verb[edit]

maestificō (present infinitive maestificāre, perfect active maestificāvī, supine maestificātum); first conjugation

  1. to make sad or sorrowful, sadden
