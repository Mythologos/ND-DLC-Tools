Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

vir (“man, male”) +‎ -tūs (abstract noun-forming suffix)

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈu̯ir.tuːs/, [ˈu̯ɪrt̪uːs̠]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈvir.tus/, [ˈvirt̪us]

Noun[edit]

virtūs f (genitive virtūtis); third declension

  1. manhood, manliness, virility
  2. courage, valor, resoluteness, gallantry
    Synonyms: spīritus, fortitūdō, fīdūcia, animus
    • c. 52 BCE, Julius Caesar, Commentarii de Bello Gallico 1.1:
      Qua de causa Helvetii quoque reliquos Gallos virtute praecedunt, quod fere cotidianis proeliis cum Germanis contendunt, cum aut suis finibus eos prohibent aut ipsi in eorum finibus bellum gerunt.
      • Translation by W. A. McDevitte and W. S. Bohn
        For which reason the Helvetii also surpass the rest of the Gauls in valor, as they contend with the Germans in almost daily battles, when they either repel them from their own territories, or themselves wage war on their frontiers.
  3. virtue, goodness
    Synonyms: bonum, rēctum
    Antonyms: vitium, culpa, malum, iniūria, noxa, peccātum, dēlictum, error
  4. excellence, merit, worth, character
  5. (figuratively) strength (of medicine or herbs)
