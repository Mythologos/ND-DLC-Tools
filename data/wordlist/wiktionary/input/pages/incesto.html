Esperanto[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin incestus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [inˈt͡sesto]
  • Rhymes: -esto
  • Hyphenation: in‧ces‧to

Noun[edit]

incesto (uncountable, accusative inceston)

  1. incest
    Synonym: sangadulto

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Semi-learned borrowing from Latin incestus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /inˈt͡ʃɛ.sto/
  • Rhymes: -ɛsto
  • Hyphenation: in‧cè‧sto

Noun[edit]

incesto m (plural incesti)

  1. incest

Derived terms[edit]

  • incestuoso

Anagrams[edit]

  • censito, cestino, cestinò, cestoni, contesi, costine, nocesti

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From incestus (“polluted, defiled”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈkes.toː/, [ɪŋˈkɛs̠t̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈt͡ʃes.to/, [in̠ʲˈt͡ʃɛst̪o]

Verb[edit]

incestō (present infinitive incestāre, perfect active incestāvī, supine incestātum); first conjugation

  1. to pollute, defile
  2. to dishonor, disgrace

  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ĩˈses.tu/, /ĩˈsɛs.tu/

  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ĩˈseʃ.tu/

incesto m (plural incestos)
