lanthanum

Alternative forms[edit]

  • lantanium (obsolete)
  • lanthanium (obsolete)

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek λανθάνω (lanthánō, “to escape notice”) +‎ -um (“a chemical element”), because it had gone long undetected in mineral ores.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • enPR: lăn'thənəm, IPA(key): /ˈlænθənəm/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Noun[edit]

lanthanum (countable and uncountable, plural lanthanums)

  2. (uncountable) A chemical element (symbol La) with an atomic number of 57: a soft, ductile, silvery-white metal that tarnishes slowly when exposed to air.
  3. (countable) An atom of this element.
