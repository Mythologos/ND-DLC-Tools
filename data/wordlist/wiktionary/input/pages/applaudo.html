Italian[edit]

Verb[edit]

applaudo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of applaudire

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • adplaudō

Etymology[edit]

From ad- (“toward”) +‎ plaudō (“to strike, clap”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /apˈplau̯.doː/, [äpˈpɫ̪äu̯d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /apˈplau̯.do/, [äpˈpläːu̯d̪o]

Verb[edit]

applaudō (present infinitive applaudere, perfect active applausī, supine applausum); third conjugation

  1. to clap (strike one thing against another)
  2. to clap the hands together, applaud
    Synonym: acclāmō
