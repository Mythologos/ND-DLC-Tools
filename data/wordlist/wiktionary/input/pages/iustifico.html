Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • jūstificō

Etymology[edit]

From iūstus (“just”) +‎ -ficō (“make”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /i̯uːsˈti.fi.koː/, [i̯uːs̠ˈt̪ɪfɪkoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /jusˈti.fi.ko/, [jusˈt̪iːfiko]

Verb[edit]

iūstificō (present infinitive iūstificāre, perfect active iūstificāvī, supine iūstificātum); first conjugation (Late Latin)

  1. to act justly (towards)
  2. to forgive, pardon or vindicate
  3. to justify
