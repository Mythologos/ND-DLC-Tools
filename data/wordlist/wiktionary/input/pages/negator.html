English[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From negate +‎ -or, or directly from Latin negātor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /nəˈɡeɪtəɹ/
  • Rhymes: -eɪtə(ɹ)

Noun[edit]

negator (plural negators)

  1. One who, or that which, negates.
  2. (grammar) A word (or other structural element) which causes negation (such as the word not in English).

Translations[edit]

word causing negation
  • Hungarian: tagadószó (hu)
  • Icelandic: neitunarorð n

Anagrams[edit]

  • NOR gate, argento-, teragon, tronage

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From negō (“deny, refuse”) +‎ -tor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /neˈɡaː.tor/, [nɛˈɡäːt̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /neˈɡa.tor/, [neˈɡäːt̪or]

Noun[edit]

negātor m (genitive negātōris); third declension

  1. a denier; apostate
