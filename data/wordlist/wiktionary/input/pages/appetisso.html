Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • adpetissō

Etymology[edit]

From appetō (“strive, desire, long for”) +‎ -essō (intensive verb forming suffix) or from ad- +‎ petessō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ap.peˈtis.soː/, [äpːɛˈt̪ɪs̠ːoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ap.peˈtis.so/, [äpːeˈt̪isːo]

Verb[edit]

appetissō (present infinitive appetissere, perfect active apetissīvī or apetissiī or apetissī, supine apetessītum); third conjugation

  1. to strive for, to seek earnestly
