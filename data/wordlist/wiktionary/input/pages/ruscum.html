Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • rūscus, brūscus, brīscus f

Etymology[edit]

Unknown; Late Latin forms possibly contaminated by *brūcus (“heather”).[1]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈruːs.kum/, [ˈruːs̠kʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈrus.kum/, [ˈruskum]

Noun[edit]

rūscum n (genitive rūscī); second declension[2][3][4][5][6]

  1. butcher's broom (Ruscus aculeatus)

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
