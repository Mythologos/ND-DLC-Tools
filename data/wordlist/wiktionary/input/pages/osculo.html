Italian[edit]

Italian Wikipedia has an article on:
osculo
Wikipedia it

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɔ.sku.lo/
  • Rhymes: -ɔskulo
  • Hyphenation: ò‧scu‧lo

Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Latin ōsculum, diminutive of ōs (“mouth”).

Noun[edit]

osculo m (plural osculi)

  1. (literary, humorous) kiss
    Synonym: bacio
  2. (biology) osculum
Related terms[edit]
  • osculare
  • osculatore
  • osculazione

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

osculo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of osculare

Latin[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Noun[edit]

ōsculō

  1. dative/ablative singular of ōsculum

Etymology 2[edit]

From ōsculum (“a kiss”) +‎ -ō.

Verb[edit]

ōsculō (present infinitive ōsculāre, perfect active ōsculāvī, supine ōsculātum); first conjugation

  1. to kiss
