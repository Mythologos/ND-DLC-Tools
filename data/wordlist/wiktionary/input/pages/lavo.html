Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Proto-Italic *lawāō, from Proto-Indo-European *(le-)lówh₃-ti (reduplicated o-grade present), from Proto-Indo-European *lewh₃- (“to wash”). Cognates include Ancient Greek λούω (loúō), λοέω (loéō), Albanian laj, Old Armenian լոգանամ (loganam), and Old English lēaþor (English lather). Doublet with the later back-formed luō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈla.u̯oː/, [ˈɫ̪äu̯oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈla.vo/, [ˈläːvo]

Verb[edit]

lavō (present infinitive lavāre, perfect active lāvī, supine lavātum); first conjugation

  1. to wash, bathe
  2. to wet, moisten

Usage notes[edit]

  • The supine is sometimes lautum or lōtum.
