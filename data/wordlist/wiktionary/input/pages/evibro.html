Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ex- +‎ vibrō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈeː.u̯i.broː/, [ˈeːu̯ɪbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈe.vi.bro/, [ˈɛːvibro]

Verb[edit]

ēvibrō (present infinitive ēvibrāre, perfect active ēvibrāvī, supine ēvibrātum); first conjugation

  1. to swing forward, hurl, throw
    • c. 330 CE – c. 390 CE, Ammianus Marcellinus, Rerum Gestarum 24.4.16
  2. to set in motion, move, excite
    • c. 177 CE, Aulus Gellius, Noctes Atticae 1.11.1
    • c. 330 CE – c. 390 CE, Ammianus Marcellinus, Rerum Gestarum 14.1.10
