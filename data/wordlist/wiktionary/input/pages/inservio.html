Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

in- +‎ serviō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈser.u̯i.oː/, [ĩːˈs̠ɛru̯ioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈser.vi.o/, [inˈsɛrvio]

Verb[edit]

īnserviō (present infinitive īnservīre, perfect active īnservīvī or īnserviī, supine īnservītum); fourth conjugation

  1. to be serviceable
  2. to defer to
  3. to serve, to attend to, to be devoted to, to look after

Usage notes[edit]

īnserviō takes a dative direct object and rarely an accusative.
