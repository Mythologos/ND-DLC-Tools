Mauritania
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From Latin Maurītānia, from Maurus (“Moor”). Doublet of Mauretania.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˌmɒɹɪˈteɪni.ə/
  • (US) IPA(key): /ˌmɔːɹɪˈteɪni.ə/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Proper noun[edit]

Mauritania

  2. A country in West Africa. Official name: Islamic Republic of Mauritania. Capital: Nouakchott.

Derived terms[edit]

  • Mauritanian

Related terms[edit]

  • Mauretania
  • Moor

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Maurētānia

Etymology[edit]

From Maurītānus (“Mauritanian, [one] of the Moors”) +‎ -ia (country name forming suffix). The first element is from Maurus (“Moor”), from Ancient Greek Μαυρός (Maurós), the second from Ancient Greek -ία (-ía). More at μαυρός (maurós).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /mau̯.riːˈtaː.ni.a/, [mäu̯riːˈt̪äːniä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /mau̯.riˈta.ni.a/, [mäu̯riˈt̪äːniä]

Proper noun[edit]

Maurītānia f sg (genitive Maurītāniae); first declension

  1. Mauretania (ancient Berber kingdom)
  2. (New Latin) Mauritania (a country in West Africa)

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, singular only.
