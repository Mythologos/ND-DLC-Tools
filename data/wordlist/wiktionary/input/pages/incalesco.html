Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ calēscō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /in.kaˈleːs.koː/, [ɪŋkäˈɫ̪eːs̠koː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /in.kaˈles.ko/, [iŋkäˈlɛsko]

Verb[edit]

incalēscō (present infinitive incalēscere, perfect active incaluī); third conjugation, no passive, no supine stem

  1. to grow warm
  2. to glow
