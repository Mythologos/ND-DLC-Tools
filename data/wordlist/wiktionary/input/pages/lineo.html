Ido[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from English line, French ligne, German Linie, Italian linea, Russian ли́ния (línija), Spanish línea. Compare Esperanto linio.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /liˈneo/

Noun[edit]

lineo (plural linei)

  1. (genealogy, geometry, military, printing, telegraphy, writing, etc.) line

Derived terms[edit]

  • bazolineo
  • eklinea (“offline”)
  • enlinea (“online”)
  • lineeskar (“to line up”, intransitive verb)
  • lineigar (“to line up”, transitive verb)
  • lineizar (“to cover with lines, rule”)
 

References[edit]

  • Progreso III (in Ido), 1910–1911, pages 386, 467
  • Progreso VII (in Ido), 1914, page 284

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From līnea (“line”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈliː.ne.oː/, [ˈlʲiːneoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈli.ne.o/, [ˈliːneo]

Verb[edit]

līneō (present infinitive līneāre, perfect active līneāvī, supine līneātum); first conjugation

  1. to make straight or perpendicular
