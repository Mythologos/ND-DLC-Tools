English[edit]

English Wikipedia has an article on:
insomnia
Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

From Latin in- (“without”) +‎ somnus (“sleep”, noun) +‎ -ia.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ɪnˈsɒmniə/
  • Audio (US)(file)

Noun[edit]

insomnia (countable and uncountable, plural insomnias)

  1. (medicine) A sleeping disorder that is known for its symptoms of unrest and the inability to sleep.
    My mother suffers from insomnia.

Synonyms[edit]

  • sleeplessness

Antonyms[edit]

  • hypersomnia

Hyponyms[edit]

  • big eye (Antarctica)
  • fatal familial insomnia

Derived terms[edit]

  • coronasomnia
  • insomniac

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /inˈsom.ni.a/, [ĩːˈs̠ɔmniä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /inˈsom.ni.a/, [inˈsɔmniä]

Etymology 1[edit]

From insomnis +‎ -ia.

Noun[edit]

īnsomnia f (genitive īnsomniae); first declension

  1. sleeplessness, insomnia
Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Dative īnsomniae īnsomniīs
Accusative īnsomniam īnsomniās
Ablative īnsomniā īnsomniīs
Vocative īnsomnia īnsomniae
Descendants[edit]
