Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /raˈpi.na/
  • Rhymes: -ina
  • Hyphenation: ra‧pì‧na

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin rapīna.

Noun[edit]

rapina f (plural rapine)

  1. robbery, mugging
  2. plunder
Derived terms[edit]
  • rapinare

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

rapina

  1. inflection of rapinare:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From rapiō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /raˈpiː.na/, [räˈpiːnä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /raˈpi.na/, [räˈpiːnä]

Noun[edit]

rapīna f (genitive rapīnae); first declension

  1. robbery, plundering, pillage, rapine
  2. plunder, booty
    Synonyms: manubia, praeda, spolium

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.
