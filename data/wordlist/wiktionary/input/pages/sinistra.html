Catalan[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): (Central, Balearic) [siˈnis.tɾə]
  • IPA(key): (Valencian) [siˈnis.tɾa]
  • Homophone: sinistre (Balearic, Central)

Noun[edit]

sinistra f (plural sinistres)

  1. left hand

Adjective[edit]

sinistra

  1. feminine singular of sinistre

Ido[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Italian sinistro, Latin sinister.

Adjective[edit]

sinistra

  1. left

Antonyms[edit]

  • dextra (“right”)

Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /siˈni.stra/
  • Rhymes: -istra
  • Hyphenation: si‧nì‧stra

Etymology 1[edit]

Inherited from Latin sinister

Noun[edit]

sinistra f (plural sinistre)

  1. left (hand, direction)
    Synonym: (nautical) babordo
    Antonyms: destra, dritta
Related terms[edit]
  • sinistrese
  • sinistro
  • sinistrorso

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

sinistra

  1. inflection of sinistrare:
    1. third-person singular present indicative
    2. second-person singular imperative

Etymology 3[edit]

Adjective[edit]

sinistra

  1. feminine singular of sinistro

Anagrams[edit]

  • astrinsi, nitrassi

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /siˈnis.tra/, [s̠ɪˈnɪs̠t̪rä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /siˈnis.tra/, [siˈnist̪rä]

Adjective[edit]

sinistra

  1. inflection of sinister:
    1. nominative/vocative feminine singular
    2. nominative/accusative/vocative neuter plural

Adjective[edit]

sinistrā

  1. ablative feminine singular of sinister

Noun[edit]

sinistra f (genitive sinistrae); first declension

  1. the left-hand side
    ad sinistram
    on the left hand side, to the left

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative sinistra sinistrae
Genitive sinistrae sinistrārum
Dative sinistrae sinistrīs
Accusative sinistram sinistrās
Ablative sinistrā sinistrīs
Vocative sinistra sinistrae
