Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From per- +‎ pellō (“push, drive”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /perˈpel.loː/, [pɛrˈpɛlːʲoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /perˈpel.lo/, [perˈpɛlːo]

Verb[edit]

perpellō (present infinitive perpellere, perfect active perpulī, supine perpulsum); third conjugation

  1. to strike or push violently
  2. (figuratively) to drive, urge, force, enforce, constrain, compel
    Synonyms: cieo, molior, ago, excito, impellō, cōgō, concieō, impingō, compellō, subigō
  3. to prevail upon; influence
    Synonyms: persuādeō, convincō, moveō, trahō, flectō, admoneō, afficiō, īnfluō
