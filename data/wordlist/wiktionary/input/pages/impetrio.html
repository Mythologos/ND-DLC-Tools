Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

impetrō (“to obtain”) +‎ -iō

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /imˈpe.tri.oː/, [ɪmˈpɛt̪rioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /imˈpe.tri.o/, [imˈpɛːt̪rio]

Verb[edit]

impetriō (present infinitive impetrīre, supine impetrītum); fourth conjugation, no perfect stem

  1. to seek to obtain through omens, to inquire about by consulting auspices
