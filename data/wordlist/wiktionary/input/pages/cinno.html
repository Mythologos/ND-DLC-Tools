Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From cinnus (“wink”, noun) +‎ -ō (verb-forming suffix). Found in the Reichenau Glossary.[1]

Verb[edit]

cinnō (present infinitive cinnāre, perfect active cinnāvī, supine cinnātum); first conjugation (Early Medieval Latin)

  1. to blink
