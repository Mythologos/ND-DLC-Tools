Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Latin illicium.

Noun[edit]

illicio m (plural illici)

  1. (zoology) illicium

Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • inliciō

Etymology[edit]

From in- +‎ laciō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ilˈli.ki.oː/, [ɪlˈlʲɪkioː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ilˈli.t͡ʃi.o/, [ilˈliːt͡ʃio]
  • The length of the vowel in the perfect stem (illē̆x-) is not entirely certain, but it is most likely short. See references at alliciō.

Verb[edit]

illiciō (present infinitive illicere, perfect active illē̆xī, supine illectum); third conjugation iō-variant

  1. to entice, seduce
