Italian[edit]

Verb[edit]

dilacero

  1. first-person singular present indicative of dilacerare

Anagrams[edit]

  • Alderico, Cordelia, Doralice, cedolari, cordiale, coridale, declorai, dolciera

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From dis- +‎ lacerō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /diːˈla.ke.roː/, [d̪iːˈɫ̪äkɛroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /diˈla.t͡ʃe.ro/, [d̪iˈläːt͡ʃero]

Verb[edit]

dīlacerō (present infinitive dīlacerāre, perfect active dīlacerāvī, supine dīlacerātum); first conjugation

  1. to tear apart, or to pieces
