Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • Ariadnē

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek Ἀριάδνη (Ariádnē).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /a.riˈad.na/, [äriˈänːä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /a.riˈad.na/, [äriˈäd̪nä]

Proper noun[edit]

Ariadna f sg (genitive Ariadnae); first declension

  1. a female given name from Ancient Greek

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun, singular only.

Accusative Ariadnam

Wikipedia pl

Etymology[edit]

Learned borrowing from Latin Ariadne, from Ancient Greek Ἀριάδνη (Ariádnē).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /aˈrjad.na/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -adna
  • Syllabification: A‧riad‧na

Proper noun[edit]

Ariadna f

  1. (Greek mythology) Ariadne (daughter of King Minos)

Proper noun[edit]
