Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From re- +‎ stipulor.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /resˈti.pu.lor/, [rɛs̠ˈt̪ɪpʊɫ̪ɔr]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /resˈti.pu.lor/, [resˈt̪iːpulor]

Verb[edit]

restipulor (present infinitive restipulārī, perfect active restipulātus sum); first conjugation, deponent

  1. to stipulate or promise in return
