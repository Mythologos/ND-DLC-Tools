Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From niger (“black”) +‎ -eō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈni.ɡre.oː/, [ˈnɪɡreoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈni.ɡre.o/, [ˈniːɡreo]

Verb[edit]

nigreō (present infinitive nigrēre); second conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. to be or become black or dark

Conjugation[edit]
