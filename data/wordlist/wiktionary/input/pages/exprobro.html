Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ex- +‎ probrum.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈek.spro.broː/, [ˈɛks̠prɔbroː] or IPA(key): /ekˈsprob.roː/, [ɛkˈs̠prɔbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈek.spro.bro/, [ˈɛksprobro] or IPA(key): /ekˈsprob.ro/, [ekˈsprɔbro]

Verb[edit]

exprobrō (present infinitive exprobrāre, perfect active exprobrāvī, supine exprobrātum); first conjugation

  1. to reproach, upbraid or reprove
    Synonyms: obiūrgō, castīgō, accūsō, obloquor, inclāmō, incūsō, obiectō, corripiō, increpō, arguō, reprehendō, perstringō
