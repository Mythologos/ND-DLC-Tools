Etymology[edit]

Latin

Noun[edit]

falsum (uncountable)

  1. (logic) An arbitrary contradiction, denoted ⊥.

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From falsus, perfect passive participle of fallō (“deceive, trick”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈfal.sum/, [ˈfäɫ̪s̠ʊ̃ˑ]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈfal.sum/, [ˈfälsum]

Noun[edit]

falsum n (genitive falsī); second declension

  1. An untruth, falsehood, fraud, deceit, lie; forgery.
    Ex falso quodlibet.[1]
    From falsehood anything [follows]: ⊥ → Q {\displaystyle \bot \rightarrow Q} .

Declension[edit]

Second-declension noun (neuter).
