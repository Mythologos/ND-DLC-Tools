Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin glaber.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɡla.bro/
  • Rhymes: -abro
  • Hyphenation: glà‧bro

Adjective[edit]

glabro (feminine glabra, masculine plural glabri, feminine plural glabre)

  1. hairless, glabrous

Latin[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈɡla.broː/, [ˈɡɫ̪äbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈɡla.bro/, [ˈɡläːbro]

Etymology 1[edit]

glaber (“hairless”) +‎ -ō

Verb[edit]

glabrō (present infinitive glabrāre); first conjugation, no perfect or supine stem

  1. (transitive) to denude of hair or bristles
