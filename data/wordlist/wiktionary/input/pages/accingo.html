Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /atˈt͡ʃin.ɡo/
  • Rhymes: -inɡo
  • Hyphenation: ac‧cìn‧go

Verb[edit]

accingo

  1. first-person singular present indicative of accingere

Anagrams[edit]

  • accigno, cicogna

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From ad- +‎ cingō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /akˈkin.ɡoː/, [äkˈkɪŋɡoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /atˈt͡ʃin.ɡo/, [ätˈt͡ʃiŋɡo]

Verb[edit]

accingō (present infinitive accingere, perfect active accīnxī, supine accīnctum); third conjugation

  1. to gird on or about
  2. to equip or provide with
    Synonyms: exōrnō, adōrnō, ōrnō, praebeō, apparō, īnstruō, parō, suggerō, comparō, afferō
  3. (passive or reflexive, + dative or in + accusative or ad + accusative) to prepare for, arrange to do something
    accingor ― to prepare oneself for
    accingor armis ― to prepare for battle, take up arms
    • 27 BCE – 25 BCE, Titus Livius, Ab Urbe Condita I.47:
      Quīn accingeris?
      Why not prepare yourself?
