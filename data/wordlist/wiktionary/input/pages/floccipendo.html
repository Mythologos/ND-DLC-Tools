Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From the compounding of floccus (“flock”) and pendere (“to weigh”), maybe "to weigh the flocks" in a figurative sense.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /flok.kiˈpen.doː/, [fɫ̪ɔkːɪˈpɛn̪d̪oː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /flot.t͡ʃiˈpen.do/, [flotː͡ʃiˈpɛn̪d̪o]

Verb[edit]

floccipendō (present infinitive floccipendere, perfect active floccipendī, supine floccipēnsum); third conjugation

  1. (transitive) To take
  2. (transitive) To consider
