Italian[edit]

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /flaˈd͡ʒɛl.lo/
  • Rhymes: -ɛllo
  • Hyphenation: fla‧gèl‧lo

Etymology 1[edit]

From Latin flagellum.

Noun[edit]

flagello m (plural flagelli)

  1. scourge
  2. bane
  3. (biology) flagellum

Etymology 2[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Verb[edit]

flagello

  1. first-person singular present indicative of flagellare

Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From flagellum +‎ -ō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /flaˈɡel.loː/, [fɫ̪äˈɡɛlːʲoː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /flaˈd͡ʒel.lo/, [fläˈd͡ʒɛlːo]

Verb[edit]

flagellō (present infinitive flagellāre, perfect active flagellāvī, supine flagellātum); first conjugation

  1. to flog, whip, lash or scourge
  2. to thresh or flail (grain)
