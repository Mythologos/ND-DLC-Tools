Latin[edit]

Alternative forms[edit]

  • puertia

Etymology[edit]

From puer (“male child, boy”) +‎ -itia.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /pu.eˈri.ti.a/, [puɛˈrɪt̪iä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /pu.eˈrit.t͡si.a/, [pueˈrit̪ː͡s̪iä]

Noun[edit]

pueritia f (genitive pueritiae); first declension

  1. boyhood, childhood, youth
  2. (figuratively) innocence; childishness, callowness
