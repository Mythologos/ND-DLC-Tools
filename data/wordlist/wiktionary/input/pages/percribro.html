Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From per- +‎ crībrō.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /perˈkriː.broː/, [pɛrˈkriːbroː]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /perˈkri.bro/, [perˈkriːbro]

Verb[edit]

percrībrō (present infinitive percrībrāre, perfect active percrībrāvī, supine percrībrātum); first conjugation

  1. to sift thoroughly
    • c. 1 AD — c. 50 AD, Scribonius Largus, De Compositione Medicamentorum, 87; 289
