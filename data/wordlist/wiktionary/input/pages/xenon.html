English[edit]

Chemical element
Xe
Previous: iodine (I)
Next: caesium (Cs)

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek ξένον (xénon), neuter of ξένος (xénos, “foreign, strange”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (UK) IPA(key): /ˈziːnɒn/
  • (US) enPR: zē'nŏn, IPA(key): /ˈzinɑn/; enPR: zē'nən, IPA(key): /zinən/
  • Audio (US)(file)
  • (General Australian) IPA(key): /zenɔn/; IPA(key): /zɪinɔn/
  • Rhymes: -iːnɒn
  • Hyphenation: xe‧non

Noun[edit]

xenon (usually uncountable, plural xenons)

English Wikipedia has an article on:
xenon
Wikipedia
  1. The chemical element (symbol Xe) with an atomic number of 54. It is a colorless, odorless, unreactive noble gas, used notably in camera flash technology.
