Latin[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Unknown.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈma.tu.la/, [ˈmät̪ʊɫ̪ä]
  • (modern Italianate Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈma.tu.la/, [ˈmäːt̪ulä]

Noun[edit]

matula f (genitive matulae); first declension

  1. A vessel for liquids
  2. A chamber pot

Declension[edit]

First-declension noun.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative matula matulae
Genitive matulae matulārum
Dative matulae matulīs
Accusative matulam matulās
Ablative matulā matulīs
Vocative matula matulae

Derived terms[edit]

  • matella
  • matelliō

References[edit]

  • “matula”, in Charlton T. Lewis and Charles Short (1879) A Latin Dictionary, Oxford: Clarendon Press
  • matula in Gaffiot, Félix (1934) Dictionnaire illustré latin-français, Hachette.

Polish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From matka +‎ -ula.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /maˈtu.la/
  • Audio(file)
  • Rhymes: -ula
  • Syllabification: ma‧tu‧la

Noun[edit]

matula f

  1. (archaic, endearing) (US) mommy, (UK) mummy
    Synonym: mamusia

Declension[edit]

Declension of matula
singular plural
nominative matula matule

dative matuli matulom
accusative matulę matule
instrumental matulą matulami

vocative matulu matule

Portuguese[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Back-formation from matulagem.

Pronunciation[edit]

 
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /maˈtu.lɐ/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /maˈtu.lɐ/
    • (Southern Brazil) IPA(key): /maˈtu.la/
  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /mɐˈtu.lɐ/

  • Hyphenation: ma‧tu‧la

Noun[edit]

matula f (plural matulas)

  1. (collective) mob
    Synonym: corja

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Arabic مَفْتُول (maftūl, “tightly twisted”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /maˈtula/ [maˈt̪u.la]
  • Rhymes: -ula
  • Syllabification: ma‧tu‧la

Noun[edit]

matula f (plural matulas)

  1. wick (of a candle, lamp, etc.)
    Synonym: torcida

Further reading[edit]
